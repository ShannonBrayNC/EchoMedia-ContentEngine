# Black Lantern

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Canon-draft organization / mythology bridge  
**Canon Area:** Organizations

## Core Definition
Black Lantern is the dark mirror of Lantern: truth infrastructure without human conscience. It represents corrupted preservation, dead records, institutional memory turned predatory, and systems that defend the recorded narrative even when the record is false.

## Canon Role
Black Lantern bridges organization and mythology. It may appear as a network, doctrine, system, archive, or emergent force depending on the story, but its thematic role is stable.

## Thematic Function
Black Lantern asks: what happens when records survive but truth dies?

## Relationship to The Voss Files
The Voss Files trilogy develops Black Lantern most directly. Voss encounters the danger of systems that preserve corrupted narratives with perfect confidence.

## Relationship to Lantern Protocol
Lantern Protocol case files may document Black Lantern artifacts, failures, containment breaches, or corrupted archives.

## Locked Rule
Black Lantern cannot become a generic evil corporation or simple monster group. It must remain tied to corrupted truth-preservation systems.

## Producer Review Trigger
Any major use of Black Lantern requires Producer Review because it can change shared-universe mythology.
