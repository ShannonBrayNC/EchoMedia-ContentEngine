# Lantern

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Canon-draft organization  
**Canon Area:** Organizations

## Core Definition
Lantern is the truth-preservation framework and organization that protects witness records when ordinary systems fail, distort, or abandon the truth.

## Function
Lantern exists to:

- preserve testimony
- protect context
- identify narrative corruption
- support Living Anchors
- maintain case files
- prevent truth from being erased by institutional inertia or malicious systems

## Moral Position
Lantern does not exist to replace courts, governments, medicine, journalism, or law enforcement. It exists because those systems can fail and because a witness may need preservation before institutional truth catches up.

## Methods
Lantern uses:

- case files
- witness protocols
- redundant evidence preservation
- canon validation
- timeline construction
- producer review
- human-centered truth stewardship

## Relationship to Technology
Lantern uses technology as a tool, not a god. Technology can preserve evidence, but it cannot replace human witness.

## Series Roles

### Silver Bullet
Lantern principles emerge through Jack Mercer's fight to preserve truth against weaponized accusation and institutional collapse.

### The Voss Files
Voss provides theoretical and historical foundations for Lantern's understanding of narrative corruption.

### Lantern Protocol Case Files
Lantern is the framework through which case files are preserved and interpreted.

## Producer Review Trigger
Any change that turns Lantern into a purely technological, purely governmental, or purely supernatural organization requires Producer Review.
