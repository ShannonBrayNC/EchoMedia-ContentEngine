# Lantern Universe Glossary

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Active canon reference

## Purpose
This glossary standardizes core terms across Lantern Universe projects so books, case files, scripts, audio packages, and marketing materials use consistent language.

## Terms

### Black Lantern
A dark mythological and organizational concept associated with corrupted archives, failed containment, and truth systems turned against human witnesses. Black Lantern is not merely a villain brand; it represents the danger of truth infrastructure without conscience.

### Canon Bible
The authoritative source of truth for Lantern Universe rules, mythology, organizations, terminology, and continuity.

### Case File
A Lantern Protocol story unit built around an incident, anomaly, witness record, or investigation. Case files may stand alone but must not violate shared canon.

### Lantern
The truth-preservation framework and organization that gathers, protects, validates, and transmits witness records when ordinary institutions fail.

### Lantern Protocol
The procedures, case records, and witness-preservation practices used when narrative corruption, evidence suppression, or reality-level manipulation threatens truth.

### Living Anchor
A human witness whose memory, testimony, and moral presence preserve truth when records, institutions, and technology become corrupted. Technology can support a Living Anchor but cannot replace one.

### Narrative Corruption
A process by which truth is distorted through record manipulation, gossip, institutional incentives, social pressure, trauma, fear, or malicious storytelling until false narratives become operational reality.

### Producer Review
The central approval gate that protects canon, continuity, IP, likeness, legal/reputation risk, emotional tone, publishing readiness, and brand value.

### Silver Bullet
A targeted accusation or social weapon that can destroy a veteran's family, career, reputation, clearance, housing, identity, and support network before truth can be established.

### The Voss Files
An independent trilogy centered on Elias Voss, narrative-system corruption, archival horror, and the discovery that technology cannot be the Living Anchor.

### Widow Circle
A malicious belief network that weaponizes gossip, accusation, social proof, and ritualized moral certainty. Its members believe they are protecting society, which makes them more dangerous than ordinary conspirators.

### Witness Protocol
A method for preserving testimony, memory, evidence, and human continuity when systems are compromised.
