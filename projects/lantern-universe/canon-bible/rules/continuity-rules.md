# Continuity Rules

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Active continuity governance  
**Canon Area:** Rules

## Purpose
Continuity rules prevent timeline drift, character drift, mythology drift, and cross-series contradictions as the Lantern Universe scales to hundreds of books and case files.

## Rule 1: Stable IDs
Every book, series, case file, character, organization, location, artifact, and major event must eventually receive a stable identifier.

## Rule 2: Timeline Impact
Any event that changes a character's status, public reputation, employment, family structure, injury state, legal status, or institutional relationship must be recorded in the timeline registry.

## Rule 3: Character State
Character status must be tracked across books: alive, dead, missing, institutionalized, accused, exiled, transformed, compromised, or unknown.

## Rule 4: Mythology Escalation
Mythology must escalate intentionally. No story may casually introduce a power, organization, archive, ritual, or anomaly that changes the universe without Producer Review.

## Rule 5: Series Autonomy
Each series must remain readable on its own while contributing to the shared universe.

## Rule 6: Case File Boundary
Case files may reveal anomalies, but they should not casually resolve trilogy-level mysteries.

## Rule 7: Evidence Continuity
Evidence introduced in one story must remain traceable unless explicitly lost, destroyed, corrupted, suppressed, or contradicted by narrative corruption.

## Rule 8: Trauma Continuity
Trauma has consequences. A character cannot suffer major psychological, reputational, legal, or physical damage without later continuity impact.

## Rule 9: Review Before Release
Continuity review must occur before Producer Review and publishing package release.

## Rule 10: Drift Handling
When a conflict is found, mark it as `canon-conflict` and resolve it through documented revision rather than ignoring it.
