# Canon Rules

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Active canon governance  
**Canon Area:** Rules

## Purpose
Canon rules define how Lantern Universe truth is created, changed, reviewed, and protected.

## Rule 1: Canon Bible Authority
The Canon Bible is the highest authority for shared-universe mythology, organizations, terminology, and cross-series rules.

## Rule 2: Human Anchor Rule
The Living Anchor must remain human. Technology can support witness preservation but cannot replace human witness.

## Rule 3: Voss Independence Rule
The Voss Files is an independent trilogy. It may connect to Lantern mythology but must not become dependent on Silver Bullet to function.

## Rule 4: Silver Bullet Grounded Horror Rule
Silver Bullet remains grounded in veteran psychological horror, institutional failure, weaponized accusation, trauma, and witness preservation. Mythology should deepen the horror, not replace the human stakes.

## Rule 5: Case File Flexibility Rule
Lantern Protocol Case Files may vary in format, scope, and tone, but they must not contradict locked mythology.

## Rule 6: Producer Review Rule
Canon-changing work requires Producer Review before publication or release packaging.

## Rule 7: No Silent Retcon Rule
Canon cannot be silently overwritten. Any retcon must be documented, justified, and reviewed.

## Rule 8: Registry Consistency Rule
Characters, organizations, timelines, and artifacts must use stable identifiers once created.

## Rule 9: Emotional Truth Rule
Lantern stories may use horror, mystery, and thriller mechanics, but trauma must not be reduced to decoration. Trauma must affect plot, character decisions, and stakes.

## Rule 10: Human Cost Rule
The Lantern Universe is strongest when systems-level horror is shown through human cost.
