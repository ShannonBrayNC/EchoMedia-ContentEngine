# Witness Protocols

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Canon-locked mythology  
**Canon Area:** Mythology

## Core Definition
Witness Protocols are the methods Lantern uses to preserve testimony, memory, evidence, and human continuity when systems become unreliable or hostile.

## Purpose
Witness Protocols exist because records alone are not enough. Records can be altered, hidden, misread, deleted, weaponized, or stripped of context.

## Protocol Layers

### Memory Preservation
Protect the witness's timeline, recollection, sensory impressions, and emotional truth without forcing premature certainty.

### Evidence Preservation
Store artifacts, documents, images, messages, logs, recordings, timelines, and metadata in redundant forms.

### Context Preservation
Maintain the circumstances around evidence so isolated fragments cannot be weaponized against the truth.

### Human Continuity
Protect the person carrying the truth from isolation, gaslighting, institutional abandonment, or coerced silence.

### Producer Review Alignment
Ensure case materials do not violate canon, likeness, legal risk, emotional tone, or publishing readiness.

## Series Roles

### Silver Bullet
Witness Protocols are the countermeasure to targeted accusation and social destruction. Jack's survival depends on preserving context before the false narrative becomes irreversible.

### The Voss Files
Voss develops the theory that witness-preservation must include human testimony, not only machine records.

### Lantern Protocol Case Files
Each case file may represent a different application or failure of Witness Protocols.

## Locked Rule
Witness Protocols do not guarantee justice. They preserve the possibility of truth.

## Producer Review Trigger
Any story that introduces a new Witness Protocol or changes the purpose of the protocols requires Producer Review.
