# Narrative Corruption

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Canon-locked mythology  
**Canon Area:** Mythology

## Core Definition
Narrative corruption is the process by which truth is distorted, suppressed, inverted, or replaced until a false story begins to function as reality.

## Sources of Corruption
Narrative corruption can emerge through:

- gossip networks
- institutional self-protection
- weaponized accusations
- corrupted records
- selective evidence retention
- social incentives
- trauma-induced memory fragmentation
- fear of reputational damage
- automated systems that preserve the wrong version of events

## Operational Effect
A corrupted narrative can cause real consequences even before truth is established:

- job loss
- relationship collapse
- custody loss
- clearance loss
- social exile
- wrongful arrest
- medical or psychological mislabeling
- public reputational destruction

## Horror Function
Narrative corruption is horrifying because the victim is not merely attacked. The world around the victim is rewritten until the attack appears justified.

## Series Roles

### Silver Bullet
The Silver Bullet is a targeted narrative corruption event. It begins as an accusation but spreads through gossip, systems, and fear until the victim's life collapses.

### The Voss Files
Voss discovers that records and systems can preserve corrupted stories as if they were objective truth.

### Lantern Protocol Case Files
Case files document incidents where narrative corruption spreads beyond one person and threatens communities, institutions, or reality perception.

## Antidotes
The Lantern Universe recognizes several partial antidotes:

- Living Anchors
- preserved testimony
- redundant evidence
- witness networks
- canon validation
- ethical recordkeeping
- refusal to let institutions define truth alone

## Producer Review Trigger
Any new story that changes how narrative corruption works, spreads, or resolves requires Producer Review.
