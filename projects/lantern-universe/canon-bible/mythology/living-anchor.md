# Living Anchor

**Sprint:** PUB-006  
**Status:** Canon-locked mythology  
**Canon Area:** Mythology

## Core Definition
A Living Anchor is a human witness whose memory, testimony, continuity, and moral presence preserve truth when records, institutions, and technology become corrupted.

## Locked Rule
Technology cannot be the Living Anchor. Technology can record, amplify, archive, or transmit evidence, but the Anchor itself must be human.

## Why This Matters
The Lantern Universe is not about machines replacing truth. It is about the danger of systems that appear objective but can be corrupted, weaponized, erased, or redirected. The Living Anchor is the human counterweight to corrupted record systems.

## Anchor Traits
A Living Anchor may possess:

- direct witness memory
- moral refusal to accept the false narrative
- continuity across erased or altered records
- trauma-bearing testimony
- evidence stewardship
- resistance to narrative collapse

## Limitations
A Living Anchor is not omniscient, magical, invulnerable, or automatically believed. Their power is narrative and moral, not superheroic.

## Series Roles

### The Voss Files
Elias Voss discovers that technology cannot serve as the Anchor. His work reveals that a human witness is required.

### Silver Bullet
Jack Mercer becomes thematically aligned with the Living Anchor through survival, memory, evidence preservation, and refusal to let weaponized accusation become the final truth.

### Lantern Protocol Case Files
Case files document incidents where Living Anchors preserve reality, memory, or testimony against narrative corruption.

## Producer Review Trigger
Any story that changes the definition, mechanics, or limits of the Living Anchor requires Producer Review.
