# Book 2 Concept Lock — The Echo Ledger

**Sprint:** SB-008  
**Book:** Book 02  
**Locked Title:** The Echo Ledger  
**Status:** Concept locked

## Core Concept
After preserving enough context to avoid being erased, Jack Mercer discovers that his Silver Bullet was not an isolated event. Similar distortions have happened before. The survivors, witnesses, and records are scattered, damaged, frightened, and incomplete.

Book 2 follows Jack as he traces the fragments of prior cases and learns that narrative corruption leaves a ledger: not a clean archive, but a haunted accounting of lives reduced to portable stories.

## Core Promise
Jack follows echoes from his own case into a wider pattern and discovers that every prior target left behind a fragment of truth someone was too afraid, too tired, or too compromised to carry alone.

## Genre Position
Grounded psychological thriller / veteran psychological horror with investigative and techno-thriller elements.

## Trilogy Function
Book 2 expands the trilogy from:

- one man under attack

to:

- a pattern of social and institutional narrative corruption.

## Emotional Engine
Book 2 is not about Jack becoming a clean hero. It is about Jack being asked to carry more truth while still wounded by Book 1.

## Structural Direction
Book 2 should move through:

1. **Aftershock** — Jack is not erased, but his life remains damaged.
2. **First Echo** — a new fragment suggests prior targets.
3. **The Ledger Opens** — Jack finds a pattern of entries, not a complete archive.
4. **Witness Cost** — prior witnesses are afraid, compromised, or broken by what they preserved.
5. **Circle Recognition** — the Widow Circle appears larger, older, and more adaptive than Book 1 revealed.
6. **False Pattern Trap** — Jack risks seeing patterns everywhere and must distinguish evidence from trauma-driven overreach.
7. **Ledger Assembly** — the scattered cases form a partial map.
8. **Book 2 Breakpoint** — Jack learns that preserving the ledger exposes him and others to deeper consequences.

## Canon Constraints

- Jack remains human and wounded.
- The Living Anchor remains human, not technology.
- Lantern mythology may become more visible but should remain partial.
- The Voss Files remains independent.
- The Widow Circle remains fictionalized and non-instructional.
- Book 2 should not fully resolve the trilogy.

## End State
By the end of Book 2:

- Jack knows his case was part of a pattern.
- The Echo Ledger exists in partial form.
- At least one prior target's story is restored enough to matter.
- The Widow Circle's reach is clearer but not fully defeated.
- Lantern / Living Anchor themes are significantly stronger.
- Book 3 is set up as the final confrontation over whether truth can survive when every record can be bent.

## Producer Decision
Concept status: **locked for development**.
