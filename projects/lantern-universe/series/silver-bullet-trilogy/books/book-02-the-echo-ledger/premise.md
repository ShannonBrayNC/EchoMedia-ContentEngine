# Book 2 Premise — The Echo Ledger

**Sprint:** SB-008  
**Book:** Book 02 — The Echo Ledger  
**Status:** Concept locked

## Logline
After surviving the first Silver Bullet, Jack Mercer follows a surviving contradiction into a hidden pattern of prior targets, damaged witnesses, and context-stripped records that suggest his case was only one entry in a much larger ledger.

## Short Premise
Jack Mercer has not been cleared. He has not recovered. But he has preserved enough truth to remain visible.

When Mara Venn points him toward a prior case with the same concern language, the same record distortions, and the same social erasure pattern, Jack begins tracing fragments left behind by people who were not able to preserve their own context. The deeper he looks, the more he realizes that the Widow Circle was not merely a local pressure network around Evelyn Blackwood. It may be part of a repeatable belief system that turns rumor into record and fear into social law.

Book 2 follows Jack as he assembles the Echo Ledger: a partial, dangerous account of prior cases where truth survived only in fragments. But building the ledger forces Jack into a new danger. The more he sees the pattern, the more he risks becoming unable to distinguish evidence from trauma, connection from coincidence, and witness from obsession.

## Core Conflict
Jack must assemble enough of the Echo Ledger to prove the pattern exists without letting the pattern consume him.

## External Conflict
Prior targets, frightened witnesses, incomplete records, and social pressure create a hostile environment where every recovered fragment can expose someone new.

## Internal Conflict
Jack wants truth to scale. But truth at scale requires trust, and Book 1 taught him that trust can become a weapon.

## Mythology Conflict
Lantern themes become more visible as Jack learns that records alone cannot carry the ledger. Human witness is required, but human witnesses break, hide, forget, and protect themselves.

## Book 2 Ending Promise
Book 2 should end with the Echo Ledger partially assembled and dangerous enough to force Book 3. Jack will know more, but knowing more will cost more.

## Producer Note
Book 2 should remain grounded. The pattern may feel mythic, but the scenes should stay human, social, procedural, and emotionally plausible.
