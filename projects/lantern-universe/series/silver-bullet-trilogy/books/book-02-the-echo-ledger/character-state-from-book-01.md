# Character State from Book 1

**Sprint:** SB-008  
**Book:** Book 02 — The Echo Ledger  
**Status:** Concept locked

## Purpose
Define the character-state inheritance from Book 1 into Book 2. Book 2 must not reset Jack Mercer.

## Jack Mercer Opening State
Jack begins Book 2 changed but not healed.

He has preserved enough context to avoid total erasure, but he has not recovered his prior life. His reputation remains damaged, his trust network is smaller, and his body carries the same limitations under more stress.

## Carried Wounds

### Social Wound
Jack knows that people can withdraw before asking what happened. He enters Book 2 wary of warmth, kindness, and concern language.

### Professional Wound
Jack's technical competence remains intact, but his professional standing has been made fragile. Book 2 should show him trying to work while knowing reputation can become conditional.

### Body Wound
Physical limitation and pain continue. Book 2 should preserve body continuity from SB-003 and SB-006.

### Trust Wound
Jack distrusts systems, witnesses, friends, and his own desire to see patterns. Trust must be earned scene by scene.

### Record Wound
Jack no longer believes records are neutral. He understands they can preserve truth, distort truth, or bury truth depending on context.

## New Strengths

### Witness Discipline
Jack has learned to preserve sequence, context, contradiction, and testimony without immediately turning every fragment into a conclusion.

### Pattern Awareness
Jack can identify repeated language, social drift, and institutional caution more quickly than before.

### Technical Witness Practice
Jack's engineering instincts now serve the witness arc: version comparison, evidence packaging, context envelopes, timeline reconstruction, and metadata preservation.

### Moral Restraint
Jack has learned that anger must be placed accurately. Book 2 should test this restraint.

## New Risks

### Pattern Overreach
Because Jack has seen one pattern clearly, he may be tempted to see patterns too quickly elsewhere.

### Isolation as Control
Jack may confuse isolation with safety.

### Documentation Burden
Documentation can become both witness discipline and emotional prison.

### Savior Trap
Jack may try to preserve truth for others before acknowledging his own limits.

### Trust Bottleneck
If Jack becomes the only trusted carrier of the ledger, the ledger becomes vulnerable.

## Character Arc Direction
Book 2 should move Jack from **survivor-witness** to **reluctant ledger keeper**.

By the end of Book 2, Jack should understand that a ledger carried by one wounded man is not enough. Truth requires distributed witness.

## Producer Note
Do not make Jack suddenly powerful. His strength should remain costly, limited, human, and rooted in witness discipline.
