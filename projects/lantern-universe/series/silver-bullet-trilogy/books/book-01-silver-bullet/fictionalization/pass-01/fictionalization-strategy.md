# SB-010 Fictionalization Strategy

**Sprint:** SB-010  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Active fictionalization controls

## Purpose
Define the fiction-first strategy for transforming Book 1 into publication-safe original fiction before Pass 2 prose expansion.

## Strategy Principle
Silver Bullet may carry emotional truth, but it must not read as a direct record of identifiable real people, institutions, correspondence, case history, or chronology.

## Transformation Layers

## 1. Character Composite Transformation
Every major character should have invented details that create distance from any real-world analogue.

Required transformations:

- invented full name
- invented biographical details
- invented relationship history
- altered professional/social role
- distinct speech pattern
- distinct motive architecture

## 2. Setting Transformation
Book 1 should use fictional or composite locations rather than directly recognizable places.

Required transformations:

- invented town/county/local geography where possible
- composite neighborhoods
- invented meeting places
- altered spatial relationships
- non-specific regional markers unless needed for story

## 3. Institution Transformation
Institutional scenes should use fictional entities or generalized process settings.

Required transformations:

- invented organization names
- invented project names
- invented process labels
- no real case numbers
- no real agency, company, court, or workplace identifiers unless fully fictionalized

## 4. Correspondence Transformation
Messages, notices, meeting summaries, and documentation should be original fiction.

Required transformations:

- do not reuse real wording
- alter sequence, sender role, medium, and context
- combine multiple pressures into composite artifacts
- remove any metadata that maps to real events

## 5. Chronology Transformation
The order of events should serve fiction and should not mirror any real-world sequence.

Required transformations:

- alter dates and timing
- combine or split events
- change which character delivers which pressure
- avoid exact real-world calendar alignment

## 6. Theme Preservation
Fictionalization should preserve:

- social echo
- context loss
- record distortion
- body limitation
- institutional caution
- witness preservation
- Living Anchor foreshadowing

## 7. Specificity Replacement
When removing real-world specificity, replace it with invented specificity. Vague fiction is not safer if it feels evasive. Strong fictional details make the manuscript more original and more believable.

## Publication Boundary
After SB-010, the manuscript is safer for internal expansion but still not publication-facing until a later publication safety review confirms the changes are applied in the prose.

## Producer Decision
Fictionalization strategy: **approved for Pass 2 expansion controls**.
