# Character Composite Map

**Sprint:** SB-010  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Active fictionalization controls

## Purpose
Define how major Book 1 characters should remain composite, invented, and publication-safe during Pass 2 expansion.

## Core Rule
No character should map directly to a real person. Each must be a composite or invented figure with distinct fictional identity, motives, relationships, and biographical details.

## Jack Mercer
### Current Function
Primary protagonist, wounded veteran, technical expert, survivor-witness.

### Fictionalization Controls
- Preserve Jack as a fictional protagonist, not author-surrogate transcript.
- Add invented service, work, family, and geography details where needed.
- Use fictional technical projects and client contexts.
- Alter chronology from any real-world sequence.
- Keep physical limitation as part of character, but do not replicate medical history in exact detail.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Add one invented professional success unrelated to the main conflict.
- Add one invented personal preference or memory that has no real-world referent.
- Use fictional project names and invented client environments.

## Evelyn Blackwood
### Current Function
Initial framing pressure source and fictional antagonist catalyst.

### Fictionalization Controls
- Must remain composite.
- Must not replicate a real person's biography, appearance, work history, family structure, correspondence style, or exact behavioral sequence.
- Give her a distinct fictional social world and invented backstory.
- Make her motive architecture thematic: moral certainty, self-protection, social influence, and belief in her own righteousness.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Add invented biographical marker unrelated to any known real person.
- Create a distinct speech rhythm not borrowed from real correspondence.
- Avoid exact real-world message phrasing.
- Give her a fictional social role inside the story world.

## Marcus Vale
### Current Function
Lost ally and false-mercy channel.

### Composite Direction
Marcus should combine traits of multiple possible support figures and invented material.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Rename from Marcus if needed after name review.
- Add fictional history: storm-generator memory may remain if clearly invented or sufficiently transformed.
- Give him an invented career, family constraint, or social pressure that explains caution.

## Kendra Sloane
### Current Function
Quiet doubter and witness thread.

### Composite Direction
Kendra should be a fictional person with her own risk, not a convenient witness stand-in.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Give her an invented personal stake in staying silent.
- Give her an invented professional or community position.
- Make her late witness behavior emotionally specific.

## Lena Hart
### Current Function
Ambiguous care/harm figure.

### Composite Direction
Lena should represent the complexity of care under social pressure, not a direct person.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Give her a distinct relationship to Jack.
- Make the soup scene fictional and symbolic, not lifted from life.
- Add one prior act of ordinary kindness to complicate the later harm.

## Devin Cross
### Current Function
Information conduit and partial believer.

### Composite Direction
Devin should be a fictional bridge character with distinct voice.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Give him a specific invented work history with Jack.
- Make his call uncomfortable but motivated by partial loyalty.
- Use him to seed Mara Venn earlier.

## Harlan Pierce
### Current Function
Institutional-process figure.

### Composite Direction
Harlan should represent process logic, not a specific real professional.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Use a fictional institution and fictional role title.
- Give her careful language but not direct real-world phrasing.
- Avoid making her malicious; she should act through institutional incentives.

## Mara Venn
### Current Function
First true reader and bridge to Book 2.

### Composite Direction
Mara should be invented enough to sustain Book 2.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Seed Mara earlier through Devin or a fictional veteran-support network.
- Give her invented investigative background.
- Keep her cautious and limited.

## Widow Circle
### Current Function
Fictional antagonist belief network.

### Composite Direction
The Circle must remain symbolic and fictional. It should not read as a real group or a guide.

### Pass 2 Actions
- Use fictional symbolic motifs.
- Avoid operational details.
- Keep members varied, sincere, afraid, and socially motivated.
- Emphasize belief patterns over tactics.
