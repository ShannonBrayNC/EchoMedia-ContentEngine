# Chapter 12 — Echo Chamber

By the end of the week, Jack could tell who had heard something before they said hello.

It changed the timing of their eyes. A half-second shift. A glance to the side before contact. Voices lowered by a fraction, not out of secrecy, but out of the burden of being careful around a man who had become a story.

He saw it at the pharmacy. Heard it in a voicemail. Felt it when a message from an old project contact began with, `I hope you're doing okay, all things considered.`

All things considered.

The phrase exhausted him because it pretended to know the list.

Jack opened a new document and titled it `echo-map.md`. He did not like the name. It sounded melodramatic, like something from a conspiracy forum. He kept it because it was also accurate.

He made columns.

Person. Phrase. Known source. Possible source. First observed change. Evidence available. Context missing.

The rows filled faster than he wanted. Lena. Kendra. Marcus. Devin. Project lead. Neighbor. Unknown account manager. Three shared phrases. Two repeated ideas. One moral pattern: distance as care, caution as goodness, uncertainty as permission.

The screen blurred. Jack leaned back, closed his eyes, and listened to the house again. Refrigerator. Vent. A car passing too slowly or maybe at the speed cars always passed. His own breath, uneven but present.

The story had escaped its source. That was the Act II truth, though he would not have used those words. Evelyn did not need to be in every room. The room had learned her shape.

At 11:06 p.m., a new message appeared from a number he did not recognize.

`People know. Stop making this harder.`

No signature.

Jack's first reaction was heat. His second was procedure.

Screenshot. Export. Timestamp. Carrier metadata. Context note.

His hands shook while he did it. He let them shake. The shaking did not make the record less true.

After he saved the file, he stood in the hallway and said the sentence again, the one from the living room mirror.

"I know what happened."

This time the sentence was not enough.

So he added another.

"And I know what is happening now."

The echo did not stop. But for the first time, it had edges.
