# Chapter 21 — Old Wounds, New Language

The phrase was different, but the room inside Jack was the same.

`We just need to know everyone is safe.`

He read it in a follow-up message from Harlan and felt twenty years fold down to one point behind his ribs. Different building. Different chain of command. Different kind of pressure. Same grammar of suspicion. Same requirement that he become smaller so other people could call themselves careful.

Jack closed the laptop before he could answer.

The body did not understand calendar distance. It understood tone. It understood being watched for the wrong movement. It understood the long seconds after someone decided your explanation was part of the problem.

He stood in the kitchen and held the edge of the counter until the present returned by objects: mug, sink, towel, cracked tile near the stove. He named them under his breath.

Mug. Sink. Towel. Tile.

The old memory did not vanish. It moved back enough to stop breathing on his neck.

He opened a voice note because typing felt too narrow.

"This is not then," he said. His voice sounded rough. "This is now. Similar language. Different facts. Do not let the old wound write the timeline."

He played it back once and felt embarrassed by the need of it. Then he saved it because embarrassment was not evidence of uselessness.

Later, he added the Harlan message to the folder with a context note:

`Language activated prior injury. Separate emotional echo from current sequence.`

That distinction mattered. If he let the past flood the present, he would lose precision. If he denied the echo, he would lose honesty. The work was not to become untouched. The work was to remain accurate while touched by everything.

He thought of the man in Devin's sentence, the public double who managed optics and controlled narratives. That man would never need to whisper object names in a kitchen. That man did not have scars pulling tight under old words.

Maybe that was why the false version traveled so easily.

It was simpler than a human being.

Jack saved the note and backed it up with the rest.

The truth did not become weaker because it hurt.

But pain could distort the carrier. That was why he had to mark it, name it, and keep going.
