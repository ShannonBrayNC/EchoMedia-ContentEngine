# Chapter 28 — The Witness Thread

Kendra agreed to meet at a park because open air made fear feel less trapped.

She sat on the far end of the bench with both hands around a paper cup. Jack noticed that she had chosen a place where they could be seen but not overheard. Smart. Afraid. Both.

"I don't want my name in anything," she said before hello.

"It won't be," Jack said.

"I mean it."

"I know."

She looked at him then, really looked, and he saw the cost of the looking. She had been avoiding the full human shape of him because the story had made avoidance easier. Now he was there, injured shoulder held carefully, face drawn by too many nights, not the man from the echo and not untouched by it either.

"I should have said something sooner," she said.

Jack wanted to answer yes. He wanted to give the anger its due. Instead he said, "You noticed now. That matters."

She handed him a folded printout. The old thread. Three lines highlighted. Her own handwritten note at the bottom:

`This language was used before the later explanation.`

Jack held the paper carefully. A human hand had written that sentence. Not a log. Not a screenshot. A person.

"I can't do more," Kendra said.

"This is more than nothing."

"It doesn't feel like enough."

Jack looked across the park. A man threw a ball for a dog. A child dragged a stick along the fence. Life continued with the brutal innocence of things not under review.

"Enough might not be available," he said. "That doesn't make it worthless."

Kendra wiped at her eye quickly, angry at the tear before it fully arrived. "People are scared of being wrong about this kind of thing."

"People are also scared of being right too late."

She nodded once.

After she left, Jack stayed on the bench with the printout in his lap. The paper trembled slightly in the wind. He placed his hand over it so it would not lift away.

Witness had cost her. It had cost him. It would cost anyone who touched the truth before the room agreed to receive it.

That did not make witness heroic.

It made witness necessary.
