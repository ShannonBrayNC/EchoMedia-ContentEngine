# Chapter 14 — Risk Language

The meeting had four people in it and no one willing to say the word truth.

They said posture. Exposure. Safeguarding. Appropriate boundaries. They said temporary again, as if repetition could turn removal into kindness.

Jack kept his face still because he could feel every muscle asking to become evidence.

The compliance director, a woman named Harlan, spoke with the measured calm of someone trained to survive rooms like this by never creating a sentence that could be used against her. Jack almost respected it. Almost.

"This is not a conclusion," Harlan said. "It is a risk posture."

"A risk posture based on what?"

"Based on the information available."

"What information?"

Her eyes moved to the side. Not guilt. Calculation. "I am not able to discuss specifics."

Jack looked at the small video windows. One muted. One camera off. One man taking notes with his head tilted down so far Jack could see only forehead and glasses.

"Then I can't answer the specifics," Jack said.

"We are not asking you to answer anything at this stage."

That was the moment he understood the trap. They were not gathering truth. They were managing proximity to it.

His shoulder had begun to throb. He shifted slightly, a small adjustment, but Harlan's eyes flicked to the movement. Jack saw her see it. Saw the note it might become: agitated, visibly uncomfortable, defensive. The body could betray him in languages he did not speak.

He lowered his hand to the desk and kept his voice even. "I need the record to reflect that I asked for the basis of the action and was not provided it."

The note-taker finally looked up.

"We can include that you requested clarification," Harlan said.

"No," Jack said. "Include what I said."

For the first time, the meeting became truly quiet.

After it ended, Jack stayed seated until the screen went dark. He had won nothing. Maybe he had made things worse. But one sentence had gone into the room with its spine intact.

He opened his notes and wrote:

`They did not say truth. They said risk.`

Below it:

`When a system is afraid, it prefers categories to people.`

He saved the file, then made himself stand slowly enough that pain would not get the last word.
