# Chapter 9 — The First Lost Ally

Marcus had once driven forty minutes in a storm because Jack needed help lifting a generator he could no longer safely lift alone.

That was the memory Jack carried into the call. Marcus laughing in rain. Marcus saying, "You ask before you tear yourself apart, idiot." Marcus making help sound like an insult so Jack could accept it.

Now Marcus sounded like a man reading from a paper he hated.

"I just think," Marcus said, "until this gets sorted, maybe it's better if we keep some distance."

Jack had expected pain. He had not expected embarrassment. It rose hot and immediate, as if he had walked into the wrong room undressed.

"Did Evelyn talk to you?"

"I heard things."

"From her?"

"Jack."

There were whole verdicts inside the way he said the name.

Jack looked at the notebook beside him. It was open to a page of careful entries. Timestamps. Names. Phrases. He wanted to read them aloud, force Marcus to sit inside the sequence until the shape changed back into something human.

Instead he asked, "Do you believe I would do what they're implying?"

Marcus breathed out through his nose. "I don't know what they're implying. That's the problem."

It was not the problem. The problem was that Marcus had accepted enough of the fog to step away from the man inside it.

Jack thanked him. The word came out clean. Military clean. Professional clean. Clean enough to hide the damage.

After the call, Jack did not write immediately. He sat with both hands on the desk and let the loss become real. Some friendships ended with an argument. Some ended with a closed door. This one ended with a man trying to sound fair while choosing the safer side of uncertainty.

When he finally opened the file, he added:

`Marcus — distance requested. Reason stated: until sorted. Source unknown or unstated.`

The note was accurate. It was also obscene. It reduced a friendship to a line item because that was what the world had begun doing to him.

He wanted to delete it. He did not.

Records did not heal anything. They only kept wounds from being renamed as weather.
