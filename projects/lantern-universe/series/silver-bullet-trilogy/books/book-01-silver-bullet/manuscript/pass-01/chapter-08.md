# Chapter 8 — The Version He Hasn't Heard

Jack heard the new version from someone who thought they were helping.

It came from Devin, a former coworker who had never been good at keeping his face separate from his thoughts. Devin called after lunch and began with three disclaimers, which told Jack the truth before the words did.

"I don't want drama," Devin said. "And I don't know anything firsthand. But you should know what people think they're hearing."

Jack opened a blank note. "Say it exactly."

"Exactly?"

"As close as you can."

The version Devin repeated was ugly because it was simple. That was its power. It took Evelyn's careful implications and turned them into a story with clean edges: Jack as unstable, Jack as intimidating, Jack as someone people had been quietly worried about for a while. No dates. No sequence. No room for injury, grief, exhaustion, or context. Just a shape anyone could carry.

Jack wrote without looking at the keys.

When Devin finished, neither of them spoke for a few seconds.

"That's not what happened," Jack said.

"I figured it was more complicated."

More complicated. The phrase should have helped. Instead it sounded like a concession given from a safe distance.

After the call, Jack laid the versions side by side. Evelyn's message. Maren's wording. Kendra's warning. Devin's retelling. Each version was shorter than the one before it. Each one lost uncertainty and gained confidence. It reminded him of file compression: remove what the user might not notice, preserve what travels fastest.

He made a table.

Original fragment. Retold version. Missing context. Added implication.

The technical part of his mind steadied him. Systems failed in patterns. Stories did too.

By the fourth row, he saw the architecture. The story did not need proof. It needed repeatability. It needed phrases that sounded responsible. It needed people afraid of being the one who ignored a warning.

He saved the document as `version-drift.md`.

Then he read the title and almost laughed.

This was his life now: version control for a story trying to overwrite him.

At the bottom of the file he added one note for himself, not for any future reader:

`Do not argue with the shadow. Track the light source.`

It sounded like advice from someone calmer than he was. Maybe that was why he kept it.
