# Chapter 25 — Fracture Point

At three in the morning, Jack forgot why the truth mattered.

Not permanently. Not completely. Just long enough to frighten him.

He sat on the kitchen floor with his back against the cabinets, laptop open beside him, the blue light showing the cruel little order of his files. Context envelopes. Screenshots. Notes. Timelines. Witness fragments. A life arranged for review by people who might never care enough to read it.

The house hummed around him. Refrigerator. Vent. A pipe ticking somewhere in the wall. The same sounds from the good morning that should have held. They did not comfort him now. They only proved the world could continue while his place inside it collapsed.

He opened the folder and clicked through records without seeing them. Evelyn's message. The meeting summary. Kendra's late-night doubt. The formal letter. Marcus's offer. Each item true in pieces, insufficient alone, exhausting together.

What if nobody ever assembled it correctly?

The question did not arrive as panic. It arrived as math.

What if the story was already more useful than the truth? What if every correction only gave the false version another room to enter? What if the man in their story survived because he was simpler, and the real Jack, the injured, tired, stubborn human Jack, was too much context for anyone to hold?

He closed the laptop.

Darkness took the room.

For a while he did nothing. No notes. No screenshots. No disciplined breathing. He let the weight be weight. He let himself hate the work. He let himself feel the terrible pull of disappearing into whatever quiet the false mercy had promised.

Then his phone buzzed.

Kendra had sent one image: a screenshot of an old group thread. In it, Evelyn used the phrase `people need to be careful` two days before she claimed concern had begun.

Below it, Kendra had written:

`I found this. I didn't remember it until now.`

Jack stared until the words steadied.

One detail. Not enough to save him. Enough to refuse the dark.

He saved the image. Created a new folder: `act-v-active`. The title was absurd, but the sequence in his head had shifted. The detail belonged not to the collapse, but to whatever came after.

In the context note he wrote:

`Detail remained. External source. Prior language confirmed.`

Then, after a long pause:

`Truth did not win tonight. It survived.`
