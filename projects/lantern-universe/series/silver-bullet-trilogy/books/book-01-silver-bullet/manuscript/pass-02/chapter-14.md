# Chapter 14 — Risk Language

The Eastbridge meeting had four people in it and no one willing to use the word truth.

Harlan Pierce joined from a room with frosted glass behind her and a plant too healthy to be real. She introduced herself as a review coordinator, not a decision-maker, which meant she had power over language and distance from consequence. Two Northstar representatives joined with cameras off. Devin appeared for three seconds, looked miserable, then disappeared into mute.

Jack kept his camera on. Not because he wanted them to see him. Because turning it off felt like letting the story choose his shape.

"Mr. Mercer," Harlan said, "we appreciate your cooperation while we maintain an appropriate risk posture."

Risk posture. Jack wrote it down before answering.

"Based on what risk?"

Harlan's expression did not change. "Based on the information available at this stage."

"What information is available to me?"

"We are not prepared to discuss specifics in this forum."

Forum. Another word that made a room sound neutral.

Jack felt the shoulder tighten and shifted before the pain could pull his face out of alignment. Harlan's eyes moved, just slightly, to the side of her screen. He saw her notice. Saw the note that might be born from noticing.

He lowered his hand to the desk. "For the record, I'm managing a physical injury. If I adjust posture or pause, that should not be interpreted as emotional state."

The meeting went still in a way he could feel through the speakers.

One of the Northstar boxes came off mute. "That's noted."

"Please include my exact statement," Jack said.

Harlan nodded. "We can include that you requested accommodation for—"

"No," Jack said. Softly. Carefully. "Not accommodation. Context. My exact statement."

There it was: the edge. The small resistance that could later be called difficult. He knew it and made himself stop talking.

For the rest of the meeting, they discussed process. They did not ask for the sequence. They did not ask what Evelyn had written first. They did not ask when the phrase careful had begun moving through Grayline Road. The meeting was not designed to learn. It was designed to reduce exposure.

Afterward, Jack opened the context memo and wrote:

`They did not say truth. They said posture, exposure, appropriate, clarification, temporary.`

Then:

`When a system is uncertain, it often protects itself before it seeks context.`

He saved the file and sat in the office until the screen dimmed.

The machine did not hate him.

It did not need to.
