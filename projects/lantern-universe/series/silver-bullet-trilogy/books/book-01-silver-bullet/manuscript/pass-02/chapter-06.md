# Chapter 6 — The Shot Lands

The first practical consequence arrived without drama.

Jack opened CivicCloud at 8:03 the next morning and found the Halden Ridge workspace missing from his sidebar. For several seconds he simply looked at the blank space where the project should have been. Then he refreshed. Signed out. Signed back in. Checked the permissions view from the cached admin page. The role change was there, plain as a bruise.

`Access paused pending clarification.`

Paused. Not removed. Pending. Not decided. Clarification. Not truth.

He messaged the Northstar account lead.

`I no longer have workspace access for Halden Ridge. Was that intentional?`

The reply came seventeen minutes later.

`Temporary while Eastbridge completes its review. Giving everyone room to settle.`

Room. Settle. Everyone. The words had learned to travel from porches into systems.

Jack stood too fast. The shoulder locked before he made it halfway upright, and pain flashed white behind his eyes. He lowered himself back into the chair with one hand braced on the desk, breathing through his teeth until the room returned to corners and light.

Work had been the last place where facts still had priority. A broken permission chain could be traced. A bad token could be renewed. A migration risk could be named without anyone asking how naming it made them feel. Now the story had entered the workspace and changed his role before anyone had asked for the sequence.

By noon, two more doors had narrowed. A Northstar planning call disappeared from his calendar. The Switchyard Cafe text thread, where Devin usually complained about client dashboards, went silent after Jack asked whether anyone knew who from Eastbridge was reviewing the matter.

Nobody said Evelyn's name. Nobody needed to.

At 4:38, Jack compared Evelyn's message with the cached Northstar thread and found the first hard contradiction.

She had written as if concern had begun after the Switchyard conversation.

But two days before that conversation, she had already used the phrase `people need to be careful` in a Grayline-adjacent exchange Kendra had been part of.

Not rescue. Not a courtroom moment. Not enough to make anyone apologize. But a hard edge. The story required a sequence that the records did not support.

Jack created a folder called `context-packet-001` and saved three items: Evelyn's message, the earlier phrase, and the CivicCloud access notice. Then he wrote the contradiction in language dry enough to survive a hostile reader.

`The later explanation depends on concern beginning after the Switchyard conversation. Available record shows concern language before that conversation.`

He read it once. Then again.

Outside, Briar County evening went gold over Grayline Road. Somewhere a lawn mower started. Ordinary life continued with an innocence that felt almost offensive.

Jack backed up the folder.

The day had not broken by accident.

Something had been aimed. Now, at least, he had found the first mark on the wall.
