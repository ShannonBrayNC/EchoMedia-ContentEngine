# Chapter 17 — The Process Does Not Know Him

The Briar Civic Annex form had no field for what mattered.

Jack stood at a kiosk with a plastic stylus attached by a metal cord and stared at the dropdown menu. Work dispute. Community concern. Review request. Safety-related matter. Other.

Other was where systems placed the human remainder.

The intake coordinator, a patient woman with a silver badge that read `M. Ames`, waited beside him with a tablet. She had kind eyes and process language. Jack had learned those could coexist.

"Choose the closest option," she said.

"Closest to what?"

"To the purpose of today's filing."

He looked at the options again. His life had become all of them and none of them. The form wanted a container. The truth needed sequence.

He selected Other.

A text box opened.

`Brief description.`

Brief was another kind of verdict.

Jack typed one sentence, deleted it, typed another, deleted that too. Every version sounded either too small or too defensive. Behind him, someone laughed at a reception desk. A printer coughed out paper. The lobby smelled like carpet cleaner and old coffee.

Finally he wrote:

`Requesting preservation of context around a sequence of social and professional events currently under review.`

Ames read it over his shoulder and nodded as if the sentence had become acceptable by becoming vague.

"You can upload supporting documents after submission."

"They need context to make sense."

"There's an attachment section."

Jack almost smiled. The system believed truth was a file-size problem.

The upload portal accepted twelve files. He had more. It accepted only three category tags. He needed seven. It required a date for the initiating event. The point of the record was that the initiation had happened before the story admitted.

He chose the earliest documented phrase and added a note explaining why. The form accepted the date without accepting the meaning.

When he returned home, he wrote a context memo titled `process-limitations`.

`System accepted artifacts but not relational sequence. System requested a single initiating date where pattern depends on prior language, later framing, and social repetition.`

The sentence felt like a bridge built over a river nobody else could see.

He added another line:

`Process is not malicious. Process is narrow.`

That distinction mattered. If he made every narrow thing evil, he would lose accuracy. If he treated narrowness as harmless, he would lose himself.

The process did not know him.

So he had to make sure the record did not pretend it did.
