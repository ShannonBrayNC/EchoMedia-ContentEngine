# Chapter 8 — The Version He Hasn't Heard

Devin Cross told Jack the new version because guilt made him useful.

They met behind The Switchyard Cafe, where the patio faced the old rail spur and nobody from Northstar liked to sit because the trains still screamed through at odd hours. Devin arrived with two coffees, offered one, and then looked relieved when Jack took it. Small rituals mattered. A man handing you coffee still had to admit you were a person.

"I don't want to be in the middle," Devin said.

"People keep saying that from inside the middle."

Devin winced. "Fair."

The version he repeated was shorter than Evelyn's message and uglier because of it. Jack had been agitated. Jack had been controlling. Jack had made people uncomfortable before anyone knew how to name it. The words had lost source and gained certainty. A social story did not need citations if enough people carried it in the same tone.

Jack listened without interrupting. That cost him more than Devin knew.

When Devin finished, the train came through, shaking the patio fence and drowning the first sentence Jack tried to say. He waited until the sound passed.

"That's not the sequence," Jack said.

"I figured there was more."

"More isn't decoration. More is the difference between a map and a rumor."

Back home, Jack built a version-drift table. Evelyn's written message. Marcus's summary. Kendra's caution. Devin's portable version. Each version had fewer dates and stronger conclusions. The story was compressing itself for travel.

He marked the changes:

`concern` became `fear`

`uncomfortable` became `unsafe`

`context missing` became `Jack explaining too much`

`sequence record` became `controlling the narrative`

The last one stayed with him. Controlling the narrative. It was a phrase made to punish anyone trying to restore order after someone else had scrambled it.

Jack added a note under the table:

`Story compression favors certainty over accuracy.`

It sounded academic. He kept it because academic language sometimes kept his hands from shaking.

Later, Devin texted.

`I should've asked sooner.`

Jack did not know how to answer. Yes would punish. It's okay would lie. He typed nothing for several minutes, then wrote:

`Ask now. Remember exact wording when you can.`

The reply was a thumbs-up. Not enough. Something.

Jack saved it under `witness-possible/devin-cross`.

The story had versions now.

That meant it had a path.
