# Chapter 23 — The Room Turns Inside Out

The office had always been safe because it asked the least from him emotionally.

Machines did not lower their voices. Logs did not glance away. CivicCloud did not decide he was difficult because he explained too much. The office was where Jack went when human rooms became too slippery and he needed failures with timestamps.

Then the second Eastbridge notice arrived.

He opened it at the desk without thinking. That was the mistake. The document filled the center monitor with its white margins and careful language, and the room changed around it. The office did not become unfamiliar all at once. It became too familiar in the wrong direction. The chair remembered every hour he had spent building the context packet. The keyboard remembered the unknown message. The monitor remembered Evelyn's first line.

Jack pushed back from the desk and the chair struck the wall.

The sound cracked through the room.

He froze. Listened. No one else was there, but his body did not care. His heart moved as if the room had witnesses. His left shoulder tightened so hard the pain flashed down his arm.

He stepped into the hallway and left the notice open behind him.

The kitchen was dark except for the microwave clock. He stood there until the house sounds returned one at a time: refrigerator, vent, a branch ticking against the back window. The office glowed blue at the end of the hall, no longer a workspace but a place where the story had learned to wait for him.

Safe places did not vanish. They changed meaning.

After ten minutes, he went back. Not because he was ready. Because leaving the notice open gave it too much authority. He saved it, printed it, scanned it, and placed the original copy in the Eastbridge folder. His hands moved with the care of someone handling evidence from a scene nobody else would recognize.

Then he opened the sequence record and wrote:

`Office place-state changed after second Eastbridge notice. Workspace no longer feels neutral. Future behavior may shift to kitchen table / alternate work location.`

It looked strange and clinically useless. He kept it anyway.

Future Jack might need to remember why the office became hard to enter. A wound deserved continuity. Without continuity, people called it mood.

Before shutting down, Jack stood in the doorway and looked at the chair angled slightly from the wall.

A room could hold what happened inside it.

So could a man.

But holding was not the same thing as being unharmed.
