# Scene Density Targets

**Sprint:** SB-006A  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Active expansion plan

## Purpose
Define Pass 2 scene expansion targets so chapters grow through meaningful story density rather than filler.

## Current State
SB-006 contains a concise structural prose draft. Most chapters currently perform one or two major functions. Pass 2 should expand each chapter into fuller scene work while preserving pace.

## Target Scene Density

| Act | Chapters | Current Function | Pass 2 Target |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Act I — The Shot | 1-6 | baseline and first rupture | 2-3 full scenes per chapter |
| Act II — The Echo | 7-12 | social echo and relationship loss | 2-3 full scenes per chapter |
| Act III — The Machine | 13-19 | records, process, body cost | 2-4 full scenes per chapter |
| Act IV — The Fracture | 20-25 | identity pressure and low point | 2-3 full scenes per chapter |
| Act V — The Witness | 26-30 | witness payoff and handoff | 2-3 full scenes per chapter |

## Recommended Manuscript Growth

Pass 2 should expand chapters by adding:

- setup beat
- primary scene
- consequence beat
- continuity handoff

## Chapter Expansion Target Types

### Type A — Baseline Expansion
Used in early chapters to make later loss matter.

Required elements:

- ordinary routine
- social warmth or professional competence
- body limitation integrated naturally
- record habit foreshadowing

### Type B — Pressure Expansion
Used when social or institutional pressure increases.

Required elements:

- external event
- Jack's body/trauma response
- contextual ambiguity
- evidence or witness consequence

### Type C — Relationship Expansion
Used for Marcus, Kendra, Lena, Devin, Mara, and Evelyn-adjacent scenes.

Required elements:

- prior relationship context
- present pressure
- specific choice
- emotional consequence

### Type D — Record Expansion
Used when documents, messages, logs, screenshots, or summaries matter.

Required elements:

- artifact introduction
- Jack's interpretation
- missing context
- possible misreading
- preservation decision

### Type E — Witness Expansion
Used in Act V and key setup chapters.

Required elements:

- fragile truth detail
- human cost
- preservation method
- relationship consequence
- Book 2 continuity seed

## Expansion Limits

Do not add scenes that only repeat:

- Jack being isolated without new information
- people being careful without a different cost
- institutional language without a new consequence
- pain without plot function
- documents without context movement

## Acceptance Test
A Pass 2 expanded chapter is ready when it answers:

1. What changed externally?
2. What changed internally?
3. What did the scene cost Jack or another witness?
4. What truth was distorted or preserved?
5. What must carry forward?
