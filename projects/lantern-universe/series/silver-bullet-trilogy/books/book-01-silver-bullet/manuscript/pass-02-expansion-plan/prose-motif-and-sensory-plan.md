# Prose Motif and Sensory Plan

**Sprint:** SB-006A  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Active expansion plan

## Purpose
Define the recurring prose motifs and sensory continuity that Pass 2 should strengthen.

## Core Motifs

## 1. House Sounds
Recurring sounds anchor Jack's body in place:

- refrigerator hum
- vent tick
- pipe noise
- cars outside
- keyboard sound
- external drive clicks

### Function
House sounds mark shifts from safety to pressure to witness discipline.

## 2. Paper and Screens
Paper and screens represent the gap between record and truth.

- printed notices
- screenshots
- message previews
- meeting summaries
- logs
- context envelopes
- folders

### Function
The same object class can preserve or distort meaning depending on context.

## 3. Careful Language
Repeated social language carries the Widow Circle atmosphere.

- safe
- concern
- patterns
- space
- settle
- careful
- risk
- posture
- clarification

### Function
Soft language becomes pressure when repeated without context.

## 4. Body Timing
Jack's body changes timing and therefore changes how scenes are read.

- shoulder lock
- pain before movement
- fatigue after documentation
- tremor under stress
- breath control
- hunger and missed meals

### Function
Body continuity keeps trauma plot-active and dignified.

## 5. Sequence
Sequence is Jack's first defense against narrative collapse.

- timestamps
- ordered lists
- version drift
- timeline files
- contradiction chains
- evidence packets

### Function
Sequence becomes a witness practice.

## 6. Rooms Becoming Unsafe
Familiar places change emotional meaning.

- office
- kitchen
- porch
- vehicle
- public meeting space
- driveway

### Function
Place-state changes show the spread of the story into Jack's environment.

## Sensory Expansion Rules

1. Use sensory detail to reveal pressure, not decorate scenes.
2. Let recurring sounds change meaning over time.
3. Avoid overusing pain shorthand.
4. Ground intense scenes in concrete objects.
5. Use screen/paper contrast to reinforce record versus context.
6. Keep the prose restrained and precise.

## Act-Level Motif Deployment

| Act | Dominant Motifs |
|---|---|
| Act I | house sounds, routine, first messages, early careful language |
| Act II | social silence, repeated phrases, phone glow, context notes |
| Act III | paper, process language, screens, body timing, folders |
| Act IV | unsafe rooms, old language, dark screens, floor/grounding details |
| Act V | printouts, ledger fragments, human voice, shared record |

## Pass 2 Acceptance Test
A motif expansion passes if it:

- strengthens atmosphere
- tracks continuity
- avoids melodrama
- supports plot movement
- remains grounded in Jack's lived experience
