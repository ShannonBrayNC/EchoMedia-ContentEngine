# Evelyn Blackwood Composite Profile

**Sprint:** SB-013  
**Character:** Evelyn Blackwood  
**Status:** Composite profile refreshed  
**Use:** Internal development only

## Purpose
Define Evelyn Blackwood as an original fictional composite character with enough invented specificity to separate her from any real-world person while preserving her story function.

## Core Function
Evelyn is the first human face of narrative distortion in Book 1. She is not the entire antagonist system. She is the local catalyst whose language, certainty, and social gravity help reveal a wider pattern.

## Character Thesis
Evelyn believes she is protecting people from danger, but her certainty makes her careless with context. She does not see herself as cruel. She sees herself as the person willing to say what others avoid.

## Fictional Biography

- Full name: Evelyn Mara Blackwood
- Age range: early-to-mid forties
- Region: Briar County, fictional American Southeast
- Public role: community organizer and informal connector in Grayline Road social circles
- Professional background: former communications coordinator for small civic and nonprofit projects
- Current work: part-time consultant for local advocacy campaigns and community safety initiatives
- Social reputation: calm, articulate, reliable, hard to challenge without seeming unreasonable
- Private flaw: treats uncertainty as a moral failure in others
- Public strength: makes people feel heard before steering them toward her interpretation

## Invented Personal Texture

- Keeps handwritten index cards of phrases, names, and reminders.
- Prefers handwritten notes over calendar apps because paper feels deliberate.
- Volunteers at seasonal civic events but avoids roles with formal accountability.
- Collects old local newsletters and frames them as community memory.
- Uses tea, small gifts, and carefully phrased notes as social glue.
- Has a controlled, warm speaking style that becomes colder when challenged.

## Motive Architecture

Evelyn is driven by four overlapping motives:

1. **Protection** — she wants to believe she protects vulnerable people.
2. **Control of ambiguity** — she struggles to tolerate unresolved complexity.
3. **Social authorship** — she likes being the person who can name what is happening.
4. **Moral identity** — she needs her actions to feel like care.

## Relationship to Jack Mercer

Evelyn does not need to hate Jack. The stronger version is that she cannot tolerate a Jack who complicates the story she wants to tell about safety, concern, and responsibility.

She should read Jack's precision as control, his documentation as pressure, and his insistence on sequence as refusal to accept the moral shape she has assigned.

## Relationship to Widow Circle

Evelyn is not the whole Circle. She is a local expression of its belief pattern.

She may use Circle-like language before she fully understands how old or wide the pattern is. Her power comes from being socially legible, not from secret command.

## Fictionalization Controls

- Do not map Evelyn to a real person.
- Do not borrow real correspondence style.
- Do not replicate real biography, work history, family history, or exact event sequence.
- Do not make her a simple villain.
- Do not make her the sole explanation for the whole trilogy.
- Do not give her operational instructions or procedural methods.
