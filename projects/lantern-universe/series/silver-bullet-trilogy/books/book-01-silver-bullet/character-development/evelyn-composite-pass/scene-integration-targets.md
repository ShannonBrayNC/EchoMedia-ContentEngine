# Evelyn Scene Integration Targets

**Sprint:** SB-013  
**Character:** Evelyn Blackwood  
**Status:** Approved for internal revision planning

## Purpose
Identify where future revisions should integrate Evelyn more strongly without breaking Book 1 structure or fictionalization controls.

## Integration Strategy
Evelyn should appear through a mix of direct scene presence, remembered social context, messages, and echo-through-others. She should not dominate Book 1. Jack's witness arc remains the center.

## Target Integration Points

## Chapter 1 — Baseline Reference
### Current Function
Jack's ordinary world and technical competence.

### Evelyn Addition
Add a light indirect reference to Evelyn as a known Grayline organizer or civic connector before she becomes pressure.

### Goal
Make her later influence feel socially grounded.

## Chapter 3 — First Major Framing
### Current Function
Evelyn's message changes the room.

### Evelyn Addition
Preserve the message but add one sensory or memory detail tied to Evelyn's fictional communication style, such as index cards, civic notes, or careful handwritten reminders.

### Goal
Make the message feel authored by a distinct fictional person.

## Chapter 5 — Circle at the Edge
### Current Function
Repeated concern language appears socially.

### Evelyn Addition
Add a brief pre-existing civic-event memory where Evelyn used similar language in a harmless-seeming context.

### Goal
Show how her language can become socially portable.

## Chapter 8 — Version Drift
### Current Function
Devin repeats a shorter version of the story.

### Evelyn Addition
Let Devin identify one phrase as sounding like Evelyn without implying direct command.

### Goal
Show voice trace without making her overpowered.

## Chapter 14 — Risk Language
### Current Function
Process language becomes formal pressure.

### Evelyn Addition
Do not add direct Evelyn presence. Instead show that institutional language echoes social language without proving coordination.

### Goal
Preserve ambiguity and plausibility.

## Chapter 21 — Old Wounds, New Language
### Current Function
Repeated wording activates older injury.

### Evelyn Addition
Use a phrase family that belongs to Evelyn's fictional voice but appears through another source.

### Goal
Show echo without over-explaining.

## Chapter 27 — Timeline of the Lie
### Current Function
Jack reconstructs sequence.

### Evelyn Addition
Add one line in the timeline noting when Evelyn's public-facing language first appeared versus when the later account claims concern began.

### Goal
Strengthen sequence logic.

## Chapter 30 — Book 2 Handoff
### Current Function
Pattern beyond Jack opens.

### Evelyn Addition
Do not over-focus on Evelyn. The ending should widen beyond her.

### Goal
Preserve Book 2 expansion.

## Revision Acceptance Test
Evelyn integration succeeds if:

1. She feels more fictional and embodied.
2. Jack remains the center of Book 1.
3. The Widow Circle remains larger than Evelyn.
4. No scene reads like a real-world accusation.
5. Her voice is distinct without being theatrical.
6. Public-facing safety is improved.

## Producer Decision
Scene integration targets: **approved for future revision and safety scan**.
