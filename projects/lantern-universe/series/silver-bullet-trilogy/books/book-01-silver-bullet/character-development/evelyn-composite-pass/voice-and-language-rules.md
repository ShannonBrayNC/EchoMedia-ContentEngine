# Evelyn Voice and Language Rules

**Sprint:** SB-013  
**Character:** Evelyn Blackwood  
**Status:** Approved for internal revision planning

## Purpose
Define Evelyn's fictional voice so her dialogue, messages, and social presence become distinct while remaining original and composite-safe.

## Voice Thesis
Evelyn speaks as if care is a civic duty and uncertainty is something responsible people should resolve quickly. Her voice is warm enough to invite trust and controlled enough to limit disagreement.

## Core Voice Qualities

| Quality | Description |
|---|---|
| Measured | She rarely sounds rushed. Her pacing suggests control. |
| Concerned | She frames positions through care, repair, and safety. |
| Indirect | She implies more than she states. |
| Polished | Her sentences feel edited before they are spoken. |
| Corrective | She gently revises other people's phrasing toward her frame. |
| Socially fluent | She knows which words will sound reasonable in a group. |

## Preferred Fictional Vocabulary

Use selectively:

- repair
- careful
- community
- context, but in a narrowed way
- patterns
- responsibility
- space
- safe
- impact
- stewardship
- thoughtful
- trust
- concern

## Voice Pattern Examples

Use original wording only. Do not reuse real correspondence.

### Pattern: Soft Frame
Evelyn does not say someone is wrong. She says the room needs a careful understanding.

### Pattern: Moral Contrast
She pairs one positive word with one negative word so others know which side sounds responsible.

Examples of contrast pairs:

- repair versus control
- care versus escalation
- safety versus defensiveness
- listening versus managing

### Pattern: Question With Built-In Shape
She asks questions that imply the answer's moral boundary.

### Pattern: Group Responsibility
She uses community language to make her interpretation feel collective.

## Dialogue Rules

1. Do not make Evelyn rant.
2. Do not make her cartoonishly cold.
3. Do not make her use legalistic or real-world traceable language.
4. Do not let her explain the whole antagonist system.
5. Let her control tone more than facts.
6. Let other characters adopt softened versions of her phrases.

## Message / Artifact Rules

Any Evelyn-authored note or message should be:

- short enough to travel
- polished enough to sound reasonable
- ambiguous enough to be hard to answer directly
- original fiction
- free of real-world metadata or exact dates

## Public-Safety Rule
If a line sounds like it could be a real copied message, rewrite it until it is clearly original fiction.
