# Evelyn Antagonist Embodiment Plan

**Sprint:** SB-013  
**Character:** Evelyn Blackwood  
**Status:** Approved for internal revision planning

## Purpose
Move Evelyn from mostly off-page pressure into a more embodied fictional presence while preserving composite safety.

## Embodiment Goal
Evelyn should feel like a real fictional person, not only a message sender or thematic symbol. Her scenes should reveal how she turns uncertainty into moral certainty through voice, social presence, and selective framing.

## Embodiment Principles

## 1. She Should Be Plausibly Trusted
Evelyn must be socially credible. People listen to her because she seems careful, calm, and protective.

Scene signals:

- measured pauses
- precise word choice
- soft correction of others
- selective warmth
- apparent patience

## 2. She Should Believe Her Own Framing
Evelyn is most effective if she is not performing evil. She believes she is naming danger responsibly.

Scene signals:

- sincere concern language
- confidence in her interpretation
- discomfort with unresolved sequence
- tendency to treat doubt as moral hesitation

## 3. She Should Avoid Direct Claims When Implication Works Better
Her power is not bluntness. It is arrangement.

Scene signals:

- phrases that invite others to complete the meaning
- questions that contain conclusions
- careful contrast between repair and control
- indirect framing that sounds reasonable

## 4. She Should Have Human Texture
Add invented, non-plot-specific texture to make her original fiction.

Scene signals:

- handwritten index cards
- local newsletter habit
- tea or paper-note motif
- civic-event memory
- polished but slightly old-fashioned communication style

## 5. She Should Be One Expression of a Wider Pattern
Do not make her the entire antagonist system.

Scene signals:

- language that echoes others without proving command
- social circles that reinforce her interpretation
- moments where she is also shaped by the belief pattern

## Recommended Embodiment Scenes

| Scene Type | Placement | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-rupture civic scene | Act I, before or near Chapter 3 | Show Evelyn as trusted and socially calm before Jack fully sees the danger. |
| Indirect social framing scene | Act I or Act II | Show Evelyn using concern language in person without making blunt claims. |
| Challenge-response scene | Act II or III | Show how Evelyn cools when someone asks for sequence. |
| Private self-justification beat | Act III or IV | Show that Evelyn believes she is protecting people. Keep it short and controlled. |
| Echo scene through others | Any act | Show her social presence without direct appearance. |

## What to Avoid

- A monologue explaining her plan.
- A direct confession.
- A scene where she becomes cartoonishly cruel.
- A scene that maps to a real person or real conversation.
- A scene that teaches how to socially manipulate people.

## Producer Decision
Embodiment plan: **approved for future revision; public-facing use still requires SB-014 scan**.
