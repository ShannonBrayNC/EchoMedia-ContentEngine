# Silver Bullet Book 1 Act Breakdown

**Sprint:** SB-004  
**Status:** Active architecture  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet

## Purpose
Define act-level story movement for the 30-chapter Book 1 architecture.

## Act I — The Shot
**Chapters:** 1-6  
**Primary question:** What happens when the first story about Jack becomes more powerful than Jack's own explanation?

### Act Function
- Establish Jack's life, competence, wounds, and fragile recovery.
- Establish social world before distortion.
- Introduce first wrong notes.
- Fire the Silver Bullet by the end of the act.

### Required Movement
Jack begins believing truth will be enough. By the end of Act I, he realizes truth is slower than accusation.

### Exit Condition
The false story has entered the world and cannot simply be recalled.

## Act II — The Echo
**Chapters:** 7-12  
**Primary question:** How does the story travel when nobody will say it plainly?

### Act Function
- Show silence and distance replacing warmth.
- Let the Widow Circle become atmosphere before explanation.
- Introduce portable false framing.
- Remove Jack's sense that private truth can protect public identity.

### Required Movement
Jack moves from defending facts to trying to understand what version of events others believe.

### Exit Condition
The story exists independently of its source.

## Act III — The Machine
**Chapters:** 13-19  
**Primary question:** What happens when process starts preserving the wrong version?

### Act Function
- Move from social pressure to record pressure.
- Show institutional caution and risk language.
- Make documentation both necessary and costly.
- Show how context loss becomes durable.

### Required Movement
Jack realizes the problem is no longer just people. It is people plus records plus procedures plus caution.

### Exit Condition
The official/social record is now dangerous enough to reshape Jack's future.

## Act IV — The Fracture
**Chapters:** 20-25  
**Primary question:** Who is Jack when the world keeps answering a false version of him?

### Act Function
- Deepen psychological horror.
- Force Jack to face identity displacement.
- Introduce or elevate a doubter/witness thread.
- Offer Jack a false version of relief through surrender or disappearance.

### Required Movement
Jack moves from defense into near-collapse, then finds one surviving detail that can still carry truth.

### Exit Condition
Jack is not saved, but something true survives.

## Act V — The Witness
**Chapters:** 26-30  
**Primary question:** Can truth survive even when it does not immediately win?

### Act Function
- Reassemble context.
- Convert trauma survival into witness practice.
- Show the shape of the attack without fully resolving the trilogy.
- Leave Book 2 open through pattern expansion.

### Required Movement
Jack stops asking only to be believed and starts preserving truth so it cannot be erased.

### Exit Condition
Jack is not cleared cleanly, but he is not erased. The trilogy advances from personal case to larger pattern.

## Trilogy Handoff
Book 1 hands Book 2 these open threads:

- The false story had architecture.
- The Widow Circle is larger than one person.
- Jack's case is not isolated.
- Lantern themes are becoming visible.
- Witness preservation is now a survival discipline.
