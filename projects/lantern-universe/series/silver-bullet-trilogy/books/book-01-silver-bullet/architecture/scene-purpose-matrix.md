# Silver Bullet Book 1 Scene Purpose Matrix

**Sprint:** SB-004  
**Status:** Active architecture  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet

## Purpose
Give the next sprint, SB-005 Scene Cards, a required matrix for every scene. No scene should exist only because it is interesting. Each scene must carry story load.

## Required Scene Fields
Each scene card must define:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `scene_id` | Stable scene identifier. |
| `chapter` | Chapter number. |
| `act` | Act placement. |
| `location` | Story location or setting class. |
| `pov` | Point of view character. |
| `plot_function` | What changes externally. |
| `character_function` | What changes internally. |
| `trauma_pressure` | Active pressure from SB-003. |
| `antagonist_pressure` | Active Widow Circle/Evelyn pressure from SB-002. |
| `narrative_corruption` | What truth is distorted or threatened. |
| `evidence_thread` | What record/detail/context survives. |
| `witness_thread` | What human witness detail survives. |
| `continuity_impact` | What must carry forward. |
| `producer_review_flags` | Review concerns. |

## Scene Type Library

### Baseline Scene
Shows what normal life costs Jack before the attack escalates.

### Signal Scene
A small cue tells Jack something is wrong before he can prove it.

### Distortion Scene
Context is stripped, simplified, or repeated in a harmful form.

### Social Loss Scene
A relationship changes under the weight of the story.

### Record Scene
A message, file, report, note, screenshot, or other record becomes important.

### Institutional Scene
A system responds to risk, process, or category rather than human context.

### Body Cost Scene
Jack's physical limitation, fatigue, or trauma response changes what happens.

### Doubter Scene
A character sees contradiction but does not yet fully act on it.

### Witness Scene
A detail, memory, timeline, or testimony is preserved against collapse.

### Counter-Record Scene
A record or witness thread gains enough structure to resist erasure.

## Act Matrix

| Act | Dominant Scene Types | Avoid Overusing |
|---|---|---|
| I | Baseline, Signal, Distortion | Too much exposition. |
| II | Social Loss, Distortion, Record | Repeating the same social beat. |
| III | Institutional, Record, Body Cost | Making institutions cartoonish. |
| IV | Body Cost, Doubter, Social Loss | Pure despair without plot movement. |
| V | Witness, Counter-Record, Doubter | Too-clean vindication. |

## Scene Validation Rule
A scene passes architecture review if it answers all four questions:

1. What changes after the scene?
2. What does it cost Jack?
3. What truth is threatened or preserved?
4. What must carry forward?

## Producer Review Rule
Any scene touching intense trauma, fictionalized composites, institutional parallels, public reputation, or medical/psychological framing must carry a Producer Review flag.
