# Public Positioning Risk Scan

**Sprint:** SB-014  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Result:** blocked-until-positioning-package

## Purpose
Review risk created by future public descriptions, excerpts, blurbs, metadata, packaging, and sales language.

## Scan Findings

| Positioning Area | Result | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Genre label | Needs controlled language | Use fictional psychological thriller / grounded psychological horror. |
| Memoir implication | Block | Do not position as memoir, true story, true crime, or direct autobiographical account. |
| Character framing | Needs care | Characters must be described as fictional composites. |
| Antagonist framing | Needs care | Avoid language implying identifiable real-world accusation. |
| Veteran themes | Needs care | Frame as fictional veteran psychological pressure, not personal case history. |
| Technology themes | Pass with note | Position as record/context thriller element, not proof of real events. |
| Public excerpts | Block | No excerpt should be released before SB-016 sign-off. |

## Required Public Positioning Rules

Any future external-facing language must clearly state or imply:

- novel
- fiction
- invented setting
- composite characters
- psychological thriller / horror
- not memoir
- not true crime
- not a direct account of real people or institutions

## Positioning Phrases to Avoid

- based on my true story
- names changed
- what really happened
- exposing real people
- true account
- real case
- documentary novel
- revenge story

## Safe Direction
A safer positioning direction is:

`A fictional psychological thriller about a wounded technical expert whose life is reshaped by social narrative distortion, missing context, and the human cost of becoming a witness to his own erasure.`

## Current Gate Result
Public positioning remains blocked until SB-015 creates a controlled fiction-first positioning package.

## Producer Decision
Public positioning risk scan: **blocked pending SB-015**.
