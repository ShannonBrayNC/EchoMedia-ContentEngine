## Strengths

- Chapter 16 successfully shows physical pain changing perception and procedural risk.
- Chapter 21 separates old wounds from current sequence, preserving Jack's reliability.
- Chapter 25 creates a low point without stripping Jack of agency.
- The recurring record/context motif supports trauma continuity.
- The manuscript keeps Jack capable and technically precise while under pressure.

## Notes for Pass 2

1. Expand sensory continuity carefully: house sounds, pain signals, room temperature, light, screen glow, paper texture.
2. Add moments where Jack rests, eats, or fails to do either; body continuity should carry across chapters.
3. Avoid repetitive shorthand for pain; vary physical presentation.
4. Add one trusted veteran-adjacent or support-network scene only if it advances plot.
5. Keep dark scenes restrained and purposeful.
6. Preserve the difference between trauma response and unreliability.
