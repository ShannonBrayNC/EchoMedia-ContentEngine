# SB-007 Fictionalization Review

**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Manuscript Input:** SB-006 Pass 1  
**Review Result:** revise-before-publication-facing-use

## Scope
Review fictionalization, composite-character safety, reputation-sensitive themes, and separation from identifiable real-world people, cases, institutions, or events.

## Review Standard
Silver Bullet may draw on emotional truth and social themes, but it must remain fiction. Characters, institutions, settings, and incidents should be composite, transformed, and non-identifiable before publication-facing use.

## Required Fictionalization Controls for Pass 2

1. Do not map Evelyn directly to a real person.
2. Do not use real case numbers, real institutions, real office names, or real direct event chronology.
3. Give primary characters fully invented identifiers, biographical details, and relationship histories.
4. Use fictional place names or composite geography when possible.
5. Use fictional institutional names and invented workflows.
6. Avoid direct replication of real correspondence language.
7. Keep Widow Circle symbolic and thematic rather than operational.
8. Maintain Producer Review for every expansion that adds specificity.
