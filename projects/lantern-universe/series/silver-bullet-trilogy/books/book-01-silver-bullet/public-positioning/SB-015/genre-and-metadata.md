# Genre and Metadata Guidance

**Sprint:** SB-015  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Metadata guidance complete

## Purpose
Define safe genre, category, and metadata direction for later marketing or packaging work.

## Title
**Silver Bullet**

## Series
**Silver Bullet Trilogy**

## Series Order
Book 1 of 3

## Recommended Genre Labels

Primary labels:

- Psychological Thriller
- Psychological Horror
- Literary Suspense

Secondary labels:

- Techno-Social Thriller
- Veteran Psychological Fiction
- Systems Thriller
- Conspiracy-Adjacent Fiction

## Short Category Line
A fictional psychological thriller about narrative distortion, damaged records, and the human cost of becoming a witness to the truth.

## Metadata Description Boundaries
Metadata should:

- identify the book as fiction
- use invented setting language
- avoid real-world identifiers
- emphasize composite characters
- focus on themes, stakes, and story
- avoid documentary framing

## Safe Setting Language
Use:

- fictional Briar County
- Grayline Road
- Northstar Systems Group
- Eastbridge Review Office
- CivicCloud Workspace

Avoid:

- real cities as anchoring claims
- real companies
- real agencies
- exact local institutions
- case-like labels

## Audience Positioning
Potential readers:

- psychological thriller readers
- grounded horror readers
- readers interested in unreliable records and social pressure
- readers of systems-driven suspense
- readers interested in veteran-centered fictional protagonists
