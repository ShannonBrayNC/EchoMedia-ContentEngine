# Prohibited Language

**Sprint:** SB-015  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Controls complete

## Purpose
Define language that must not be used in future public-facing Book 1 materials.

## Prohibited Positioning
Do not describe Book 1 as:

- memoir
- true crime
- documentary account
- direct autobiography
- real case account
- expose
- revenge story
- names-changed account
- factual reconstruction

## Prohibited Claim Patterns
Avoid phrases that imply the book depicts real people or real institutions.

Do not use:

- based on what really happened
- the truth behind real events
- exposing real people
- real names changed
- from actual records
- documentary novel
- a true account
- my real case
- what they did

## Prohibited Antagonist Framing
Do not frame any character as a direct stand-in for an identifiable real person.

Avoid:

- real villain language
- direct accusation framing
- revenge framing
- public callout language
- language suggesting readers should identify real analogues

## Prohibited Institution Framing
Do not imply that fictional entities are real companies, agencies, courts, or employers.

Avoid:

- direct institutional comparison
- real customer reference
- real workplace reference
- real proceeding reference
- real case reference

## Safer Replacement Strategy
Replace documentary-sounding language with fiction-first story language.

Use:

- fictional
- invented
- composite
- psychological thriller
- grounded horror
- narrative distortion
- witness
- context
- reputation fracture

## Gate Rule
Any future copy containing prohibited language fails the positioning review and must be rewritten before sign-off.
