# SB-015 Positioning Strategy

**Sprint:** SB-015  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet  
**Status:** Strategy complete

## Purpose
Define how Book 1 should be described in any future external-facing context after final sign-off.

## Positioning Principle
Silver Bullet must be positioned as original fiction: a grounded psychological thriller / horror novel set in a fictional world with composite characters and invented institutions.

## Primary Positioning
**Silver Bullet** is a fictional psychological thriller about a wounded technical expert whose life is distorted by social narrative pressure, missing context, and the human cost of preserving truth when records alone are not enough.

## Genre Stack

Primary:

- psychological thriller
- grounded psychological horror

Secondary:

- literary suspense
- techno-social thriller
- veteran-centered fiction
- systems-and-records thriller

Avoid positioning it as:

- memoir
- true crime
- documentary fiction
- legal expose
- revenge story
- direct autobiographical account

## Positioning Pillars

## 1. Fiction First
All descriptions should make clear that Book 1 is a novel with invented setting and composite characters.

## 2. Human Witness
The story is about context, witness, memory, and the limits of records.

## 3. Grounded Horror
The horror comes from social distortion, isolation, and misread records rather than supernatural spectacle.

## 4. Technical Precision
Technology and documentation matter, but they do not save truth by themselves.

## 5. Trilogy Handoff
Book 1 should be positioned as the beginning of a larger Silver Bullet Trilogy arc.

## Gate Rule
This strategy may be used for planning. External release of copy requires SB-016 final producer sign-off.
