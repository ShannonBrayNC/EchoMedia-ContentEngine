# Living Anchor Resolution

**Sprint:** SB-009  
**Book:** Book 03 — The Last Witness  
**Status:** Concept locked

## Purpose
Define how The Last Witness resolves the Living Anchor concept within the Silver Bullet Trilogy without violating Canon Bible rules.

## Locked Canon Rule
The Living Anchor is human. Technology cannot become the Living Anchor.

## Book 3 Resolution
Book 3 resolves the Living Anchor not as a title Jack wins, but as a truth he finally understands: records can preserve evidence, but only human witness can preserve context, meaning, cost, and moral responsibility.

## Core Resolution Statement
The Living Anchor is not one person holding all truth alone. It is the human act of carrying truth with context when systems, records, and social pressure fail.

## Jack's Relationship to the Concept
Jack begins the trilogy as a man trying to preserve his own context. By Book 3, he understands that truth cannot survive if every record depends on one wounded witness.

His final movement is from:

- isolated witness

to:

- catalyst for distributed witness.

## Technology's Role
Technology may assist by preserving:

- files
- timestamps
- messages
- logs
- contradiction chains
- ledger fragments
- witness packages

Technology cannot preserve:

- courage
- context
- moral responsibility
- lived cost
- human judgment
- the decision to testify

## Final Living Anchor Test
Book 3 should ask:

If every record can be bent, who remains willing to carry truth accurately?

The answer should not be a machine. The answer should be human witnesses choosing to carry separate pieces together.

## Resolution Mechanics

1. Jack sees the ledger become vulnerable to distortion.
2. He realizes controlling the whole record makes him the single failure point.
3. He releases context to trusted witnesses rather than hoarding it.
4. Prior targets begin carrying their own fragments.
5. The truth survives because it becomes distributed human memory, not because one archive becomes perfect.

## Lantern Connection
Lantern mythology may become visible, but it should not take over the emotional climax. Lantern should clarify the pattern; Jack and the witnesses should carry the human resolution.

## What to Avoid

- A software platform becoming the Living Anchor.
- Jack being declared mythologically chosen in a supernatural way.
- An exposition-heavy explanation of Lantern replacing emotional resolution.
- A perfect archive solving the trilogy.
- Witness becoming a title without cost.

## Producer Decision
Living Anchor resolution: **approved for future architecture development**.
