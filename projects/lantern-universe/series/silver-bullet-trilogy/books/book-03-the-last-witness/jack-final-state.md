# Jack Mercer Final State

**Sprint:** SB-009  
**Book:** Book 03 — The Last Witness  
**Status:** Concept locked

## Purpose
Define Jack Mercer's intended end-state at the close of the Silver Bullet Trilogy.

## Starting Point from Book 1
Jack begins as a wounded but functioning veteran and technical expert whose life is destabilized by a targeted narrative collapse. By the end of Book 1, he has preserved enough context to avoid erasure but has not been publicly restored.

## Middle Point from Book 2
By the end of The Echo Ledger, Jack understands that his case was not isolated. He becomes the reluctant keeper of a partial ledger of prior targets and damaged witnesses.

## Final Book 3 Question
Can Jack preserve truth without becoming the single vulnerable point around which all truth depends?

## Final State Target
Jack ends the trilogy as a human witness who has learned to distribute truth rather than control it alone.

He is not cured. He is not untouched. He is not returned to the exact life he had before. But he is no longer defined by the false story, and he is no longer isolated as the only carrier of context.

## Final State Dimensions

### Identity
Jack's identity is internally restored. He knows who he is even if public restoration remains imperfect.

### Reputation
Jack receives meaningful restoration, but not magical universal vindication. Some people understand. Some records are corrected. Some wounds remain.

### Body
Physical limitations remain part of continuity. The ending must not pretend pain disappears because truth survives.

### Trust
Jack does not become naive again. He regains selective trust through earned witness relationships.

### Work
Jack's technical identity is transformed. He no longer uses technical skill only to fix systems; he uses it to protect context while recognizing technology's limits.

### Witness Role
Jack refuses to be the only witness. His final strength is not carrying everything alone but enabling distributed witness.

### Moral Position
Jack refuses both revenge and surrender. His final act should preserve truth without becoming the thing that harmed him.

## Final Emotional Note
The end should feel like hard-won breath, not clean triumph.

Jack survives with his name still attached to the truth. More importantly, the truth survives without needing Jack to stand alone forever.

## What Must Not Happen

- Jack is instantly healed.
- Everyone apologizes.
- Every record is corrected.
- The world becomes fair overnight.
- Jack becomes a flawless hero.
- Jack becomes only a symbol and stops being human.
