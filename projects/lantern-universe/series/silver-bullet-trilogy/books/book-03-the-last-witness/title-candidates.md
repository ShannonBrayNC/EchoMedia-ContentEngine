# Book 3 Title Candidates

**Sprint:** SB-009  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy  
**Selected Title:** The Last Witness

## Selection Criteria
Book 3 title needed to communicate:

- finality
- human witness
- truth survival
- identity restoration
- cost of memory
- emotional and moral climax
- trilogy closure without over-explaining Lantern mythology

## Candidate List

| Rank | Title | Assessment |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **The Last Witness** | Selected. Human, direct, final, emotionally clear, and aligned with Jack's end-state. |
| 2 | The Living Anchor | Strong mythology title, but too explicit and risks turning the trilogy toward exposition. |
| 3 | The Final Record | Strong record motif, but less human than the trilogy requires. |
| 4 | The Witness Protocol | Good Lantern alignment, but overlaps too strongly with anthology/case-file language. |
| 5 | The Human Record | Strong thematic fit, but less thriller-forward. |
| 6 | The Last Ledger | Good Book 2 continuity, but too derivative of The Echo Ledger. |
| 7 | The Truth That Remains | Strong literary title, but softer than the trilogy's grounded thriller/horror tone. |
| 8 | No More Silence | Direct but too generic and slogan-like. |

## Locked Title
# The Last Witness

## Rationale
The title works because Book 3 is not about a machine, file, archive, or institution saving truth. It is about human witness: costly, fragile, distributed, and morally necessary.

The word "last" creates finality but should not mean Jack is literally the only witness. The trilogy resolution should reveal that the danger lies in making one wounded person carry truth alone. Jack may become the last witness willing to stand before others finally choose to carry truth with him.

## Trilogy Title Pattern

- **Silver Bullet** — the attack.
- **The Echo Ledger** — the pattern.
- **The Last Witness** — the human cost and final stand.

## Producer Note
Title may be revisited only if Book 3 architecture determines the ending requires a stronger Lantern-specific title.
