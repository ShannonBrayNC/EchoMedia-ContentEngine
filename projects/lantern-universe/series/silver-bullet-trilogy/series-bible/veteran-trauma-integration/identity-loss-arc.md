# Identity Loss Arc

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active fiction design model  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Define how Silver Bullet Book 1 attacks Jack Mercer's identity and how the rewrite should track that loss without stripping him of agency.

## Core Identity
Jack's identity is built around service, competence, honor, endurance, protection, technical ability, and survival.

## Core Fear
The Silver Bullet does not merely threaten Jack's reputation. It threatens to replace his identity with a public story he cannot control.

## Arc Movement

## Stage 1 — I Know Who I Am
Jack begins wounded but still anchored by self-knowledge. He knows his history, values, skills, and limits.

## Stage 2 — They Are Seeing Someone Else
The accusation creates a double: a version of Jack others begin responding to.

## Stage 3 — Every Defense Changes Shape
Jack learns that his attempts to explain himself can be interpreted through suspicion.

## Stage 4 — The Mirror Breaks
Jack begins to feel the gap between who he is and who the world has decided he might be.

## Stage 5 — Witness Reassembly
Jack does not recover the old self untouched. He reassembles identity through witness, context, endurance, and chosen truth.

## Plot Requirements
Each stage should affect visible choices:

- what Jack says
- what he withholds
- where he goes
- whom he trusts
- what he documents
- what he avoids
- when he pushes back
- when he freezes

## Relationship Effects
Identity loss should affect:

- family bonds
- professional identity
- veteran pride
- friendship trust
- relationship to institutions
- relationship to his own body

## Living Anchor Alignment
Jack becomes more aligned with the Living Anchor concept when he stops trying only to be believed and begins preserving truth so it can survive beyond the moment.

## Dignity Rule
The story may break Jack's public identity, but it must not erase his inner personhood.
