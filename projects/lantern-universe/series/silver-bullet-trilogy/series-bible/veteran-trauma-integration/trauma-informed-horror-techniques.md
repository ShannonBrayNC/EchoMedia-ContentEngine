# Trauma-Informed Horror Techniques

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active fiction craft guide  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Define horror techniques that portray veteran trauma with dignity while increasing dread, ambiguity, and emotional force.

## Core Rule
The horror should come from lived pressure, not spectacle. Trauma should intensify the reader's understanding of Jack's reality rather than exploit his pain.

## Technique 1: Threat Scanning
Jack notices exits, tone shifts, body position, background noise, pauses, and institutional language before he notices his own fear.

### Use
Build tension through what Jack cannot stop noticing.

### Avoid
Do not make threat scanning look like superpower perception. It should cost him energy.

## Technique 2: Context Collapse
A detail Jack knows has context is seen by others without that context.

### Use
Create dread when evidence, tone, or behavior can be misread.

### Avoid
Do not rely on contrived misunderstandings. The distortion should feel plausible.

## Technique 3: Safe Place Failure
A place that should feel safe becomes emotionally unsafe because the false story has entered it.

### Use
Home, office, vehicle, VA setting, courthouse, inbox, phone, or familiar room can become charged with dread.

### Avoid
Do not make every space equally threatening. Contrast matters.

## Technique 4: Body-First Reaction
Jack's body reacts before he can assemble language.

### Use
Show pain, tension, breath, startle, freezing, fatigue, or sensory overload shaping decisions.

### Avoid
Do not reduce trauma to flashbacks only.

## Technique 5: Record Horror
A record exists, but it is incomplete, contextless, or emotionally false.

### Use
Tie this to Lantern's larger question: what happens when records survive but truth does not?

### Avoid
Do not make records magically evil. The horror is plausibility.

## Technique 6: Social Silence
People do not accuse Jack directly. They grow careful around him.

### Use
Make silence more frightening than confrontation.

### Avoid
Do not over-explain every social cue.

## Technique 7: Witness Fragments
Even in collapse, Jack preserves fragments: times, words, smells, sounds, messages, pain, contradictions.

### Use
Foreshadow Living Anchor alignment.

### Avoid
Do not turn witness preservation into a clean solution. It should be costly.

## Scene Rule
Each horror beat should carry both external pressure and internal cost.

## Dignity Rule
The reader should leave each trauma-heavy scene understanding Jack more deeply, not merely watching him suffer.
