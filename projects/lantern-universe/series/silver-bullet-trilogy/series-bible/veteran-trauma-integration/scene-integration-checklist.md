# Veteran Trauma Scene Integration Checklist

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active rewrite checklist  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Give writers and reviewers a practical checklist for integrating veteran trauma into Silver Bullet scenes with dignity, plot relevance, and continuity.

## Scene-Level Questions

## 1. External Pressure
What is happening outside Jack?

- accusation
- silence
- institutional process
- social distance
- record/context problem
- physical limitation
- relationship fracture

## 2. Internal Pressure
What trauma pressure is active inside Jack?

- hypervigilance
- avoidance
- anger
- numbness
- shame
- distrust
- exhaustion
- body pain
- memory pressure
- sleep disruption

## 3. Behavior Change
How does trauma change what Jack does?

- delays response
- documents excessively
- withdraws
- scans the room
- over-explains
- refuses help
- asks for proof
- changes route
- avoids a person or place
- freezes before action

## 4. Cost
What does this scene cost Jack?

- energy
- trust
- credibility
- relationship warmth
- physical pain
- professional confidence
- emotional control
- sense of safety

## 5. Context Preservation
What truth, memory, evidence, or detail survives the scene?

- timestamp
- phrase
- witness
- contradiction
- body memory
- message
- sensory detail
- institutional record

## 6. Reader Effect
What should the reader feel?

- dread
- claustrophobia
- grief
- anger
- moral outrage
- uncertainty
- recognition
- hard-earned hope

## 7. Living Anchor Movement
Does the scene move Jack toward witness preservation?

- yes, by preserving context
- yes, by refusing the false story
- yes, by recognizing narrative corruption
- no, but the scene creates the wound that later requires witness

## 8. Dignity Check
Does the scene understand Jack more deeply, or only hurt him?

If the scene only hurts him, revise.

## 9. Producer Review Trigger
Flag the scene for Producer Review if it includes:

- intense trauma depiction
- real-person-adjacent material
- legal or reputational sensitivity
- self-harm ideation or crisis material
- medical/psychological framing
- direct institutional analogue

## Acceptance Test
The scene passes if trauma changes action, creates cost, preserves context, and maintains Jack's dignity.
