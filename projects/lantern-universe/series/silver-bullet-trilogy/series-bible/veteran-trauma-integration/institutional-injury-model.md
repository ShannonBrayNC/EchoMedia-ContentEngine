# Institutional Injury Model

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active fiction design model  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Define how institutions create psychological pressure in Silver Bullet without making every institution cartoonishly evil.

## Core Principle
Institutional horror is strongest when systems behave plausibly, defensively, and coldly while still producing harm.

## Institutional Injury
Institutional injury occurs when a person turns to a system expecting clarity, fairness, or protection and instead receives process, suspicion, silence, or context loss.

## Story Domains

### Employment
Professional identity becomes fragile when suspicion enters workplace judgment.

### Legal Process
Formal language and procedural distance make Jack feel reduced to risk and allegation.

### Medical / Psychological Framing
Records and labels may capture fragments of distress without capturing the pressure that created them.

### Benefits and Support Systems
Help systems may still require Jack to explain himself while exhausted, injured, and under suspicion.

### Digital Records
Messages, screenshots, logs, and forms can preserve pieces of truth while stripping emotional context.

### Community Institutions
Friend groups, veteran networks, churches, clubs, or local organizations can become risk-avoidant and silent.

## Plot Function
Institutions should create hard choices:

- speak and risk being misread
- stay silent and risk the false story hardening
- document everything and look obsessive
- trust process and risk context loss
- avoid process and look guilty

## Horror Function
The terror comes from the realization that no single villain needs to control the system. A corrupted narrative can travel through cautious people and ordinary paperwork.

## Writing Rule
Each institutional scene should include:

- what Jack needs
- what the institution sees
- what context is missing
- what record survives
- what emotional cost increases

## Producer Review Note
Institutional scenes should remain fictionalized and avoid direct claims about identifiable real-world people, offices, or cases unless separately reviewed.
