# Trauma Continuity Impact Map

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active continuity tool  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Ensure trauma consequences persist across chapters, books, and future Lantern Universe continuity.

## Core Rule
A major wound must leave continuity. If the story causes psychological, social, institutional, professional, physical, or reputational harm, later chapters must account for it.

## Continuity Domains

## Physical Continuity
Track injury, pain, fatigue, medication effects, mobility limits, and body-based constraints.

## Psychological Continuity
Track hypervigilance, avoidance, sleep disruption, anger, numbness, shame, distrust, and memory pressure.

## Social Continuity
Track who pulls away, who stays, who goes silent, who misreads Jack, and who later becomes a witness.

## Institutional Continuity
Track records, reports, meetings, access changes, official language, and context loss.

## Professional Continuity
Track reputation, clearance/professional trust, employment risk, technical identity, and future work ability.

## Evidence Continuity
Track what survives: messages, screenshots, timelines, witnesses, contradictions, logs, and sensory details.

## Living Anchor Continuity
Track moments where Jack preserves truth despite pressure.

## Rewrite Rule
Do not reset Jack between chapters. Recovery, collapse, anger, exhaustion, and resilience should all have memory.

## Trilogy Rule
Book 2 and Book 3 should inherit scars from Book 1. Jack's later strength should not erase the cost of surviving the Silver Bullet.

## Producer Review Note
Any continuity item touching real-person-adjacent material, mental health crisis, legal reputation risk, or medical framing requires Producer Review before publication.
