# SB-003 Veteran Trauma Research Notes

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Research complete  
**Scope:** Fictional veteran trauma integration for Silver Bullet Book 1

## Purpose
Ground Silver Bullet's veteran trauma layer in trauma-informed storytelling without turning the manuscript into clinical advice or diagnosis.

## Source Orientation
This sprint used high-level reference grounding from:

- VA National Center for PTSD: PTSD basics, common post-trauma reactions, and functional impact.
- National Institute of Mental Health: PTSD as trauma response that can interfere with work, relationships, sleep, and daily life.
- SAMHSA trauma-informed principles: safety, trust, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and cultural/historical awareness.

## Creative Boundary
This sprint is for fiction craft only. It does not diagnose characters, prescribe care, or provide mental health guidance.

## Relevant Trauma-Informed Concepts

### Persistence Beyond Immediate Danger
PTSD-related stress can continue even when the person is not currently in danger. In fiction, this allows scenes where Jack's body reacts to social or institutional cues before his conscious mind fully understands why.

### Daily Life Interference
Trauma can disrupt relationships, work, sleep, focus, and ordinary functioning. In Silver Bullet, that means trauma must affect plot choices, not simply appear in exposition.

### Hypervigilance and Arousal
Jack may scan environments, over-read social cues, react strongly to ambiguous threat, or become exhausted by constant alertness.

### Avoidance
Jack may avoid calls, rooms, people, records, or conversations that feel likely to trap him inside the false story.

### Mood and Cognition Pressure
Shame, anger, numbness, guilt, distrust, memory pressure, and hopelessness may distort choices without making Jack unreliable as a human being.

### Trauma-Informed Writing
Scenes should preserve dignity, agency, choice, and context. The story can be dark without turning trauma into spectacle.

## Silver Bullet Application
The Silver Bullet attack should aggravate existing wounds by combining:

- social isolation
- institutional coldness
- reputational loss
- body limitation
- loss of professional identity
- fear that every response can be misread
- collapse of trusted systems

## Required Story Test
Every trauma element must answer:

- What does this change in Jack's behavior?
- What does it cost him?
- What does it reveal about the system around him?
- What context must the reader preserve?
- How does this move Jack toward witness rather than collapse?
