# Veteran Trauma Model

**Sprint:** SB-003  
**Status:** Active fiction design model  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Define how veteran trauma operates inside Silver Bullet Book 1 as story pressure, not exposition.

## Core Rule
Trauma must change plot behavior. If a trauma element can be removed without changing Jack Mercer's decisions, relationships, pacing, or danger, it is not integrated deeply enough.

## Trauma Domains

## 1. Body Memory
Jack's body reacts before his conscious mind can explain the reaction.

### Story Use
Use physical signals such as exhaustion, pain, guarded movement, startle, scanning, and sleep disruption to shape scene pacing.

### Plot Impact
Body memory limits what Jack can do, when he can act, and how others misread him.

## 2. Trust Damage
Jack's ability to rely on people, systems, and records is damaged.

### Story Use
Show hesitation before calls, meetings, disclosures, and requests for help.

### Plot Impact
Jack delays, over-documents, withdraws, or chooses harder solo paths because trust now feels dangerous.

## 3. Identity Collapse
The attack strikes the identity Jack built around service, competence, honor, protection, and usefulness.

### Story Use
Make the accusation feel like a theft of self, not only a threat to reputation.

### Plot Impact
Jack's choices are shaped by the terror of becoming the false story.

## 4. Institutional Injury
Systems that should clarify truth instead preserve fragments, categories, and risk labels.

### Story Use
Every institutional interaction should feel plausible but emotionally costly.

### Plot Impact
Jack learns that the record can survive without the truth.

## 5. Isolation Spiral
The social world becomes quieter, colder, and harder to read.

### Story Use
Silences, delayed responses, careful language, and avoided eye contact become horror atmosphere.

### Plot Impact
Jack loses support exactly when support would help him preserve context.

## 6. Witness Adaptation
Jack begins converting survival habits into witness habits.

### Story Use
Documentation, memory anchoring, timeline reconstruction, and context preservation become part of his character arc.

### Plot Impact
Jack moves toward Living Anchor alignment without becoming mythological or superhuman.

## Rewrite Requirement
Each major act should contain at least one scene where trauma changes what Jack does, not just how he feels.

## Dignity Rule
Jack is wounded, not weak. Trauma complicates his choices; it does not erase his agency.
