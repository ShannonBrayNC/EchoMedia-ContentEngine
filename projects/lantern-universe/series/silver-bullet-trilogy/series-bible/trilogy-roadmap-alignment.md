# Silver Bullet Trilogy Roadmap Alignment

**Sprint:** SB-001  
**Status:** Active roadmap alignment  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Ensure Book 1 rewrite choices support the full trilogy rather than solving every mystery or exhausting the premise too early.

## Trilogy Function
Silver Bullet should progress from personal destruction to systemic revelation to truth-preservation confrontation.

## Book 1 — The Shot
### Function
Ground the horror in Jack Mercer's targeted collapse.

### Core Question
Can a man preserve truth when everyone around him begins acting on the false version?

### Ending Requirement
Jack must survive with enough context preserved to become a credible witness, but not with all wounds healed or all systems corrected.

## Book 2 — The Echo
### Function
Expand from Jack's case into a larger pattern.

### Core Question
How many lives have already been rewritten by the same narrative machinery?

### Setup from Book 1
Book 1 should leave:

- unresolved Widow Circle signals
- preserved evidence fragments
- hints of prior targets
- institutional patterns that feel too consistent to be coincidence
- a stronger Lantern connection

## Book 3 — The Anchor
### Function
Confront the deepest form of narrative corruption and lock Jack's relationship to witness preservation.

### Core Question
What does truth cost when systems prefer the corrupted story?

### Setup from Book 1
Book 1 should foreshadow:

- Living Anchor themes
- witness protocols
- the limits of records without context
- Jack's refusal to surrender memory
- the human cost of preserving truth

## Book 1 Rewrite Boundaries
Book 1 should not:

- fully explain Lantern
- fully defeat the Widow Circle
- make Jack cleanly victorious
- turn the story into supernatural exposition
- resolve all institutional consequences

Book 1 should:

- make Jack's personal collapse unforgettable
- reveal the shape of the Silver Bullet attack model
- preserve a counter-narrative thread
- introduce the emotional logic of Living Anchor mythology
- create momentum for Books 2 and 3

## Trilogy Continuity Rule
Every major Book 1 rewrite must be checked against Books 2 and 3 potential. If a rewrite closes too many doors, it should be revised.
