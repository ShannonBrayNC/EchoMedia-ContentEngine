# Widow Circle Narrative Threat Model

**Sprint:** SB-001  
**Status:** Active rewrite tool  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet

## Purpose
This document defines the Widow Circle's narrative function for Silver Bullet Book 1. It is a fictional story-design document, not a real-world operating guide.

## Core Function
The Widow Circle turns moral certainty into narrative pressure. Its horror comes from ordinary people treating suspicion as proof because the group's belief system rewards certainty over context.

## Story Role
The Widow Circle should make Jack feel surrounded by people who do not need evidence because they believe their certainty is evidence.

## Narrative Behaviors
In the manuscript, the Widow Circle should function through:

- repeated phrases that make members feel righteous
- emotional certainty replacing factual complexity
- social distance that appears polite but becomes suffocating
- institutional fear triggered by the appearance of risk
- selective interpretation of Jack's reactions
- group reinforcement when doubts appear

## Horror Rule
The Widow Circle is frightening because its members believe they are doing good. The story should not reduce them to cartoon villains.

## Escalation Role by Act

### Act 1
The Circle is mostly invisible. Jack sees only hints: tone shifts, private conversations, a sudden change in how people look at him.

### Act 2
The Circle becomes socially active. Jack discovers that a simplified version of the story is spreading without his participation.

### Act 3
The Circle's narrative begins influencing systems. People responsible for decisions do not need to be members; they only need to fear being seen as insufficiently cautious.

### Act 4
The Circle becomes psychological atmosphere. Jack feels watched, interpreted, and pre-condemned.

### Act 5
The Circle is not fully defeated. Instead, Jack preserves enough context to show that the story was shaped, amplified, and rewarded.
