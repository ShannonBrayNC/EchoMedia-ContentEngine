# Widow Circle Narrative Pressure Model

**Sprint:** SB-002  
**Status:** Active fictional antagonist design  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
Define how the Widow Circle creates pressure inside the story without presenting real-world procedures. This model is for narrative design, pacing, and horror atmosphere.

## Core Principle
The Circle does not need to control every event. It only needs to make the false story feel socially safer than the complicated truth.

## Pressure Types

### Atmosphere Pressure
Jack senses changed rooms, shortened conversations, and faces that have already decided something. Nothing explicit needs to happen for the reader to feel dread.

### Language Pressure
Members reuse phrases that make uncertainty sound irresponsible. Repetition makes the group's worldview feel normal.

### Concern Pressure
Cruelty is disguised as care. Characters act as if distance, exclusion, and suspicion are forms of protection.

### Interpretation Pressure
Every action Jack takes is interpreted through the Circle's preferred story. Calm becomes coldness. Anger becomes danger. Silence becomes guilt. Evidence becomes manipulation.

### Institutional Pressure
The Circle does not need to own institutions. It benefits when institutions react defensively to social risk.

### Memory Pressure
Jack begins questioning whether others remember him as he was or only through the Circle's story.

## Act-Level Use

### Act 1
Pressure is implied through tone shifts and social signals.

### Act 2
Pressure becomes visible through repeated framing and social distance.

### Act 3
Pressure shapes decisions by people who may not even belong to the Circle.

### Act 4
Pressure becomes psychological atmosphere. Jack feels interpreted before he speaks.

### Act 5
Pressure is partially named. Jack begins preserving context against the Circle's simplified story.

## Horror Rule
The pressure should feel plausible. A reader should recognize the emotional pattern without needing the group to behave like a cartoon conspiracy.

## Rewrite Use
For each major Widow Circle scene, identify:

- what pressure type is active
- how it changes Jack's options
- what context is lost
- what emotional cost increases
- what witness detail survives

## Producer Review Note
This model is fictional and non-instructional. It exists to support horror atmosphere and character pressure inside a novel.
