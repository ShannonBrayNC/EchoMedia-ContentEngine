# Fictional Act Tension Map

**Sprint:** SB-002  
**Status:** Active fiction design note  
**Series:** Silver Bullet Trilogy

## Purpose
This file provides a safe, fiction-only tension map for the antagonist group in Silver Bullet. It is for novel structure, mood, and character psychology only.

## Boundary
This is not a real-world model. It does not describe instructions, tactics, or procedures.

## Act Progression

## Act 1 — Unease
The protagonist senses that ordinary social space has changed. The story should feel quiet, plausible, and uncomfortable.

## Act 2 — Isolation
The protagonist experiences emotional distance from people and places that once felt familiar. The scene work should emphasize silence, hesitation, and uncertainty.

## Act 3 — Weight
The story around the protagonist begins to feel harder to move. The reader should feel that misunderstanding has become heavier than fact.

## Act 4 — Fracture
The protagonist feels the psychological cost of being seen through a distorted story. This is the deepest horror movement of the book.

## Act 5 — Witness
The protagonist preserves context, memory, and testimony. The resolution should feel hard-earned rather than clean.

## Writer Check
Each scene using the antagonist group should answer:

- What emotional pressure is present?
- What does the protagonist lose or preserve?
- What does the reader understand more clearly?
- How does this support the next act?

## Result
The antagonist group should deepen the novel's atmosphere while remaining clearly fictional and non-instructional.
