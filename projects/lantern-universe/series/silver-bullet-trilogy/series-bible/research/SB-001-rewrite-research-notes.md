# SB-001 Rewrite Research Notes

**Sprint:** SB-001  
**Status:** Research complete  
**Scope:** Silver Bullet Book 1 rewrite blueprint

## Research Basis
This sprint uses existing Lantern Universe canon and registry work:

- Silver Bullet is grounded veteran psychological horror / techno-thriller.
- Jack Mercer is the fictionalized veteran protagonist and must remain a composite character.
- Evelyn Blackwood is a fictionalized composite antagonist.
- Widow Circle is a fictional collective antagonist and belief network.
- The Living Anchor is human, not technology.
- Narrative corruption is the process by which a false story begins to function as reality.
- Producer Review is required for sensitive, canon-changing, likeness-sensitive, legal/reputation-sensitive, or trauma-heavy work.

## Current Creative Diagnosis
Book 1 needs to move from "mystery with veteran flavor" into "veteran psychological horror with mystery mechanics." The threat should not feel like background conflict. It should feel like a targeted collapse of identity, systems, relationships, and reality.

## Required Story Direction

### Deeper Veteran Trauma
Veteran trauma must affect plot choices, pacing, trust, investigation, isolation, and Jack's physical/emotional limits.

### Targeted Silver Bullet Mechanic
The Silver Bullet must be deliberate enough to feel designed. It is not a misunderstanding; it is a narrative attack that turns social and institutional systems against Jack.

### Widow Circle Escalation
The Widow Circle must feel like a belief network, not a loose group of gossiping antagonists. Its members should believe they are morally justified.

### Narrative Corruption
False narrative should spread faster than truth. The horror is that the world begins reacting to the story before facts can catch up.

### Living Anchor Alignment
Jack's survival arc should align with the Living Anchor concept: human witness, preserved memory, context, and refusal to let corrupted systems become final truth.

## Research Constraint
All Silver Bullet material must remain clearly fictionalized, composite, and story-focused. Sensitive real-world themes should be handled through fictional narrative design, not direct accusation or one-to-one mapping.
