# Silver Bullet Trauma Escalation Matrix

**Sprint:** SB-001  
**Status:** Active rewrite tool  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet

## Purpose
This matrix ensures veteran trauma is plot-active rather than decorative. Trauma must change decisions, pacing, trust, perception, investigation behavior, and relationships.

## Core Principle
Trauma is not backstory. Trauma is a pressure system inside the story.

## Trauma Layers

| Layer | Narrative Effect | Plot Impact | Rewrite Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hypervigilance | Jack notices too much and cannot filter threat. | He catches details others miss but exhausts himself. | Acts 1-5. |
| Isolation | Jack withdraws before others can abandon him. | Evidence and support become harder to gather. | Acts 2-4. |
| Purpose Collapse | Professional identity and veteran pride are attacked. | Jack's sense of usefulness fractures. | Acts 2-3. |
| Institutional Betrayal | Systems he expected to be fair respond coldly. | He stops trusting official channels. | Acts 3-4. |
| Memory Pressure | Stress makes recall feel fragile. | Jack fears his own uncertainty can be used against him. | Act 4. |
| Body Limitations | Physical pain and limitations shape choices. | He cannot pursue every lead or confrontation. | Acts 1-5. |
| Reputation Wound | Others see accusation before they see the man. | Jack's social reality changes before facts do. | Acts 2-5. |
| Witness Awakening | Jack preserves context despite collapse. | Trauma becomes testimony, not defeat. | Act 5. |

## Act-Level Trauma Progression

### Act 1 — Controlled Strain
Jack appears functional but already carries old injuries, guarded trust, and survival habits.

### Act 2 — Social Withdrawal
Jack begins reading distance, silence, and changed tone as threat signals. Some of this is accurate; some is trauma amplification.

### Act 3 — Systemic Triggering
Institutional contact becomes psychologically dangerous because every interaction can become a record stripped of context.

### Act 4 — Fracture
Jack's body and mind begin reacting faster than his conscious reasoning. The narrative should make the reader feel how survival responses can be misread as guilt, instability, or aggression.

### Act 5 — Witness Integration
Jack does not heal neatly. He learns to preserve testimony and context while still carrying the wound.

## Rewrite Rules

1. Do not use trauma as decoration.
2. Do not make Jack invulnerable because he is a veteran.
3. Do not reduce PTSD to flashbacks only.
4. Show trauma through behavior, perception, pacing, avoidance, evidence habits, and trust damage.
5. Preserve dignity: Jack is wounded, not weak.
6. Trauma should complicate truth-seeking, not replace it.

## Acceptance Test
For each major scene, identify:

- what trauma pressure is active
- how it changes Jack's choice
- what it costs him
- how it affects the reader's sense of reality
- what witness/context survives the scene
