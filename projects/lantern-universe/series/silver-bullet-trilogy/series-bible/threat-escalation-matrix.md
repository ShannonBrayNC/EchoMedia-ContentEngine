# Silver Bullet Threat Escalation Matrix

**Sprint:** SB-001  
**Status:** Active rewrite tool  
**Book:** Book 01 — Silver Bullet

## Purpose
This matrix defines fictional narrative escalation for Book 1. It is not a real-world playbook. Its purpose is to help the manuscript show how narrative corruption raises stakes through story pressure, not procedural instruction.

## Escalation Principle
The Silver Bullet should escalate from a single claim into a world that behaves as if the claim is already true.

## Threat Layers

| Layer | Story Pressure | Horror Function | Rewrite Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Personal | Jack is forced to defend his own memory and character. | Identity destabilization. | Early chapters. |
| Social | People begin distancing without confrontation. | Isolation and invisibility. | Act 2. |
| Professional | Work reputation and clearance/professional trust are threatened. | Purpose collapse. | Acts 2-3. |
| Institutional | Systems begin recording or acting on suspicion. | The false narrative becomes durable. | Act 3. |
| Financial | Stability begins to fail. | Survival pressure. | Acts 3-4. |
| Legal/Reputational | Jack must respond carefully because every response can be reframed. | Speech becomes dangerous. | Acts 3-4. |
| Psychological | Hypervigilance, sleep loss, and trauma responses intensify. | Reality instability. | Act 4. |
| Mythic/Foreshadowing | Jack preserves context when systems fail. | Living Anchor alignment. | Act 5. |

## Act-Level Threat Model

### Act 1 — Accusation
Threat is immediate but localized. Jack still believes truth will matter quickly.

### Act 2 — Spread
Threat becomes social. Jack realizes people are reacting to a version of the story he has not heard.

### Act 3 — System Conversion
Threat becomes institutional. Records, processes, and gatekeepers begin behaving as if suspicion equals fact.

### Act 4 — Internal Collapse
Threat becomes embodied. Jack's own trauma responses become part of the danger because others can misread survival behavior.

### Act 5 — Witness Counterforce
Threat remains unresolved, but Jack begins preserving context, testimony, and evidence as a human witness.

## Rewrite Rule
No escalation should exist only to make the plot louder. Every escalation must either:

- reveal character
- deepen trauma impact
- increase narrative corruption
- force Jack to make a costly choice
- preserve a future witness/context thread

## Producer Review Note
Because this matrix handles sensitive themes, it must remain fictional, non-instructional, and focused on narrative effect rather than real-world tactics.
