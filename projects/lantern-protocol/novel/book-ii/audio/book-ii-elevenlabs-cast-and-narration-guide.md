# Lantern Protocol II — ElevenLabs Cast and Narration Guide

## Purpose

This guide prepares **Lantern Protocol II: The Separate Agreements** for audiobook production, ElevenLabs Studio scene packaging, future trailer voiceover, and character-consistent narration.

The book should sound like a legal thriller, disaster operations log, humanitarian field report, and intimate testimony braided together. The danger should feel procedural and human, not theatrical.

## Core Narration Doctrine

```text
Agreement is not consent when stacked into coercion.
```

## Narrator Tone

- Controlled, cinematic, serious.
- Avoid melodramatic villain delivery.
- Let dread emerge from procedure, not volume.
- Treat humanitarian characters with dignity even when they are wrong.
- Treat systems as useful, precise, and dangerous because they are useful.
- Read legal and technical material clearly, but keep emotional scenes warm and grounded.

## Series Continuity Tone

Book II follows Book I's Bound Flame aftermath. The world is not post-apocalyptic. It is functioning, frightened, and eager to copy a miracle.

Book II should carry these tonal anchors:

```text
Prediction is not permission.
Agreement is not consent when stacked into coercion.
Delegation is not disappearance.
```

## Character Voice Guidance

### Adrienne Cross

- **Register:** Legal, controlled, strategic.
- **Emotional center:** Carries the cost of rights as policy.
- **Delivery:** Firm but not grandstanding. She does not preach; she names the line others want blurred.
- **Avoid:** Campaign speech cadence.

### Elias Voss

- **Register:** Quiet systems architect, burdened by consequence.
- **Emotional center:** Knows a protocol can become a brand.
- **Delivery:** Thoughtful, restrained, sometimes weary.
- **Avoid:** Genius-tech swagger.

### Mara Vale

- **Register:** Investigative, precise, evidence-first.
- **Emotional center:** Outrage contained inside records.
- **Delivery:** Clean, clipped, mapmaker energy.
- **Avoid:** Conspiracy-theory rhythm. Mara proves things.

### Naomi Bell

- **Register:** Human, field-grounded, increasingly doctrinal.
- **Emotional center:** Refuses to let unreachable people become absent people.
- **Delivery:** Warm but sharpened by experience.
- **Avoid:** Passive witness energy. Naomi builds.

### Juno Park

- **Register:** Technical, quick, quietly haunted.
- **Emotional center:** Trust roots became infrastructure others can exploit.
- **Delivery:** Alert, precise, sometimes underbreath realization.
- **Avoid:** Hacker caricature.

### Iris Chen

- **Register:** UX/interface conscience.
- **Emotional center:** Sees coercion hiding inside accurate wording.
- **Delivery:** Crisp and perceptive, with emotional pressure under the surface.
- **Avoid:** Abstract design lecture tone.

### Marcus Thorne

- **Register:** Operational command.
- **Emotional center:** Speed saves lives, but speed without people becomes trespass.
- **Delivery:** Direct, field-command realism.
- **Avoid:** Action hero bluster.

### Caleb Rusk

- **Register:** Broadcast-polished, then increasingly whole-record serious.
- **Emotional center:** Discovers truth is less profitable when not cut into weapons.
- **Delivery:** Controlled media rhythm with moments of uncomfortable honesty.
- **Avoid:** Cartoon pundit voice.

### Leah Santos

- **Register:** Field advocate, practical and compassionate.
- **Emotional center:** Forms do not equal dignity when people are carrying grief.
- **Delivery:** Grounded, patient, direct.
- **Avoid:** Sentimental softness.

### Dr. Amara Sayeed

- **Register:** Doctor-operator, humanitarian authority.
- **Emotional center:** Has watched process arrive too late.
- **Delivery:** Calm under pressure, morally formidable.
- **Avoid:** Villain tone. Amara is right about real things.

### Minister Rafael Ibarra

- **Register:** Political leader under climate-displacement pressure.
- **Emotional center:** Refuses U.S. ownership of restraint doctrine.
- **Delivery:** Diplomatic, sharp, wounded pride under control.
- **Avoid:** Simplistic nationalist performance.

### Kaito Ren

- **Register:** Founder, autonomy evangelist, compelling libertarian fire.
- **Emotional center:** Believes he is giving people personal sovereignty.
- **Delivery:** Smooth, intelligent, charismatic, nearly persuasive.
- **Avoid:** Greedy tech villain delivery.

### Sister Malia Okoro

- **Register:** Local trust authority, spiritual but practical.
- **Emotional center:** Knows names where institutions know categories.
- **Delivery:** Steady, intimate, unshowy authority.
- **Avoid:** Mystic stereotype or saintly whisper.

## System / Interface Voice Guidance

### Civic Mirror

If voiced, Civic Mirror should be:

- calm,
- respectful,
- clear,
- bureaucratically gentle,
- never menacing,
- never smug.

Civic Mirror is frightening because it is careful and useful.

### Consent Agent Interface

If voiced, consent agents should sound:

- polished,
- helpful,
- intimate,
- commercially reassuring,
- slightly too confident.

### Bound Flame / Audit Notices

If voiced, audit notices should sound like public safety doctrine, not divine warning.

## ElevenLabs Studio Structure

### Recommended project organization

Create one ElevenLabs Studio project for Book II once prose is complete:

```text
Lantern Protocol II - The Separate Agreements
```

Suggested section structure:

```text
Title Page
Preface / Series Note
Chapter 01 - The One That Asked First
Chapter 02 - No Nation Owns Mercy
...
Chapter 24 - The Separate Agreements
Author Note / Production Note
```

### Scene batching guidance

For long chapters, batch scenes by emotional or procedural turn:

```text
Chapter 04A - Mara opens the agreement archive
Chapter 04B - The clean documents
Chapter 04C - The stack becomes visible
Chapter 04D - Every agreement is clean
```

### What to remove before audio upload

Do not include in the reader-facing manuscript:

- internal processing metadata,
- revision notes,
- continuity notes,
- TODO markers,
- script comments,
- GitHub issue references,
- manifest JSON,
- non-reader-facing doctrine scaffolding unless written as prose.

### What to preserve before audio upload

- Title page.
- Preface if reader-facing.
- Chapter headings.
- Scene breaks.
- Letters, interface excerpts, public statements, transcripts, and field records that the listener should hear.

### Formatting recommendation

For ElevenLabs manuscript upload:

- Bold the book title.
- Bold every chapter heading.
- Keep chapter headings consistent.
- Avoid excessive Markdown tables in the narration manuscript.
- Convert important artifacts into prose-readable blocks.
- Remove code fences unless the literal system text should be read aloud.

## Reader-Facing Artifact Handling

Book II should use artifacts as dramatic texture, but not dump raw pseudo-documents for too long.

Good audio artifacts:

- Civic Mirror doctrine request.
- Mercy Bloc statement.
- Caleb broadcast excerpt.
- Consent-agent product language.
- Translation prompt comparison converted into narrated prose.
- Memory Registry testimony.
- Common Refusal compact excerpt.
- Final burden trace.

Poor audio artifacts:

- long JSON blocks,
- raw tables,
- full legal clauses without narrative framing,
- repetitive audit fields.

## Performance Notes by Act

### Act I — The Mirror Asks

Tone: procedural unease. The reader should feel that nothing has exploded, yet something irreversible has begun.

### Act II-A — Rights Without Borders

Tone: field pressure. Humanitarian reality should complicate clean doctrine.

### Act II-B — The Market Learns Mercy

Tone: polished danger. The consent-agent world should feel seductively reasonable.

### Act III — The Right to Be Carried

Tone: intimate, exhausted, accountable. No triumphalism. The victory is that the person is not erased.
