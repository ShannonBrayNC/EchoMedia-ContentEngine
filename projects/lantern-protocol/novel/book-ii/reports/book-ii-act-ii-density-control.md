Issue: #120

Purpose: Keep Chapters 7-18 from becoming procedural-heavy by assigning each chapter one emotional burden, one physical setting anchor, one human-facing consequence, one doctrine move, and one clear cut-line.

## Act II Through-Line

1. Chapters 7-9 keep Mercy Bloc and public authority in direct conflict while preserving both sides as morally serious.
2. Chapters 10-13 move consent pressure into language, records, and delegation.
3. Chapters 14-18 widen the fight into ideology, markets, testimony, ultimatum, and civic acceleration.
4. Caleb's whole-record arc should not drift into a side quest. It should pressure Chapters 12, 14, 16, and 18 by making every faction afraid of selective memory.
5. Consent Markets must enter early enough to remain intertwined with humanitarian pressure, not arrive as a separate tech subplot.

## Artifact Throttle

- Default: one reader-facing artifact per chapter.
- Maximum: two only when the second is short and emotionally necessary.
- If a chapter needs more than two artifacts, move extras to internal continuity notes.
- Raw tables should become prose, field notes, testimony, interface prompts, or short memos before manuscript export.

## Human-Facing Consequence Gate

Every Act II chapter must answer this before drafting is considered complete:

```text
Who pays the price in body, time, safety, family stability, access, memory, reputation, or future choice?
```

If the answer is only an institution, policy, or system, the chapter is too abstract.

## Cut Rules

Cut or move material when:

- a scene explains more than it changes;
- two doctrine ideas compete for the chapter center;
- an artifact repeats what dialogue already proves;
- faction language replaces human stakes;
- a system becomes more vivid than the person affected by it;
- Caleb comments from the side instead of forcing a choice;
- Mercy Bloc becomes either savior-only or villain-only;
- Kaito's market argument becomes silly instead of seductive;
- translation scenes explain localization instead of dramatizing misunderstanding.
