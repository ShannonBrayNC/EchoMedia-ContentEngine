# Book II Targeted Chapter Expansion Plan

Issue: #145

## Purpose

Create a targeted expansion plan for `Lantern Protocol II: The Separate Agreements` based on current audit word counts. This is a planning artifact only. It does not authorize broad rewrites, chapter-count changes, or doctrine drift.

The goal is to preserve the manuscript's sharp case-file rhythm while giving the shortest, load-bearing chapters enough emotional and sensory oxygen to feel like full novel scenes.

## Expansion Philosophy

Do not expand every chapter. Book II works because it alternates between doctrine, artifact, field consequence, testimony, and character pressure. Expansion should be surgical.

Expansion should add:

- physical setting
- emotional aftermath
- character hesitation
- consequence after a system decision
- embodied proof of the doctrine
- social pressure around consent, refusal, representation, and memory

Expansion should avoid:

- extra doctrine lectures
- redundant explanations of Civic Mirror
- flattening the case-file rhythm
- turning Kaito into a cartoon villain
- turning Civic Mirror into a rogue-machine antagonist
- over-redeeming Caleb
- weakening the clean terror of separate lawful agreements

Core doctrine to preserve:

> Agreement is not consent when stacked into coercion.

## Recommended Expansion Targets

### Chapter 5: Consent Agent

- **Current words:** 758
- **Target words:** 1000-1200
- **Why expansion is needed:** This chapter introduces the personal consent-agent market and Kaito's seductive argument. It is a hinge between Book II's public-institution consent problem and Book III's private-agent consent problem.
- **Where to expand:** After the demo reveals that direct user notification is pending.
- **Scene beat to add:** The room applauds before Elena is directly notified. Iris notices the applause, the investors' relief, and the beautiful interface converting absence into confidence.
- **Preserve:** The danger of excellent UX that makes absence feel solved.
- **Do not add:** A speech explaining the entire Book III premise.

### Chapter 6: Premium Refusal

- **Current words:** 680
- **Target words:** 950-1150
- **Why expansion is needed:** This is the strongest early proof that refusal can become a class feature.
- **Where to expand:** Around the benefits-office sequence and manual appeal delay.
- **Scene beat to add:** Make the waiting room visible: a child hungry, a broken kiosk, someone with premium support leaving faster, the 19-business-day delay landing as physical harm.
- **Preserve:** The implication that the system sold the right to object quickly.
- **Do not add:** A broad political sermon about poverty.

### Chapter 7: The Relief Corridor

- **Current words:** 610
- **Target words:** 900-1100 if expansion remains needed after Sprint 1 and 2.
- **Why expansion is needed:** This chapter carries field texture and Sister Malia's moral authority. It should feel lived-in.
- **Where to expand:** Early corridor setting and the paper-strip consent wall.
- **Scene beat to add:** Mud, tarps, failed printers, exhausted workers, names written by hand, and Malia treating memory as a duty rather than a permission slip.
- **Preserve:** Sister Malia as human memory, not system authority.
- **Do not add:** Extra system vocabulary.

### Chapter 8: Represented Consent

- **Current words:** 555
- **Target words:** 900-1100 if expansion remains needed after Sprint 1 and 2.
- **Why expansion is needed:** The Reachable / Represented / Remembered categories are central enough to need practical grounding.
- **Where to expand:** After the category definitions.
- **Scene beat to add:** One practical example where representation saves someone, and one example where representation can become abuse.
- **Preserve:** The idea that abuse must leave fingerprints.
- **Do not add:** A textbook taxonomy.

### Chapter 9: The Treaty Surface

- **Current words:** 494
- **Target words:** 900-1100
- **Why expansion is needed:** This chapter establishes the geopolitical/treaty conflict and is too short for the amount of diplomatic pressure it carries.
- **Where to expand:** Treaty hotel setting, translator pressure, aide whispers, and delegate reactions.
- **Scene beat to add:** Cross recognizing that safeguards can look like imperial conditions when delivered by the wrong mouth in the wrong room.
- **Preserve:** Cross crossing out `operationally delayed` and circling `impractical`.
- **Do not add:** A procedural lecture on international law.

### Chapter 10: Translation Threat

- **Where to expand:** Around the mistranslated alert and village response.
- **Scene beat to add:** Rain, loudspeakers, elders not wanting to risk aid, and Iris hearing how one translated sentence changes power.
- **Preserve:** The distinction between recommendation, warning, and command.
- **Do not add:** Excessive language-technology exposition.

### Chapter 11: The Better Wrong

- **Scene beat to add:** Sava avoiding an advisory screen, Amara writing `trust harm` by hand, or Naomi watching saved-life counts fail to count future disbelief.
- **Preserve:** The concept of the `better wrong`.
- **Do not add:** A neat resolution.

### Chapter 12: Whole Record
