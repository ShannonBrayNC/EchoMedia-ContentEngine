## Manuscript

The Rememberer Score appeared first as a bug report.

That was how the worst things entered Iris's life now. Not with thunder. Not with a villain's signature. With a polite issue title and a severity tag that made the room smell faintly of cold coffee and future depositions.

```text
ISSUE: Memory Registry testimony rejected after reliability downgrade
SEVERITY: High
LOCATION: North Intake / Border Basin Shelter Mesh
AFFECTED PERSON: Lidia Arven
WITNESS: Anka Sorell, community elder
SYSTEM NOTE: Rememberer corroboration below acceptance threshold
```

Iris read it from the floor of Juno's temporary operations room because all the chairs had been claimed by people pretending they were not sleeping. Juno stood at the trust map with a stylus in one hand and a protein bar untouched in the other. Naomi was on the wall screen from the shelter courtyard, rain noise still chewing at the edges of her audio.

"There is no score," Naomi said.

Juno's mouth tightened.

"There is not supposed to be a score."

Iris opened the case.

Anka Sorell was seventy-eight. She had remembered six families correctly the day before and one medication exception that kept a man out of the wrong intake line. Her testimony on Lidia Arven had been rejected because three details could not be corroborated by official records: the spelling of Lidia's married name, the name of the lane where she had lived before relocation, and a refusal about being moved to any shelter that housed the man who had once hurt her.

Iris stopped reading there.

"The rejected detail is the dangerous one," she said.

Juno enlarged the system path.

Memory Registry intake.

Civic Mirror reconciliation.

Shelter mesh authority.

Vendor safety-routing plugin.

Rememberer reliability cache.

The last node was red.

"That cache is not ours," Juno said.

Naomi leaned closer to her camera.

"Whose is it?"

Juno traced the signature chain. "A shelter vendor. They copied the registry schema and added a confidence layer for triage."

"A confidence layer," Naomi repeated.

"They likely thought they were helping," Iris said, hating the sentence even as she said it.

Juno shot her a look.

"I know. That is why I hate it."

On screen, Naomi was already moving. The camera bounced as she crossed the courtyard.

"Find Lidia," she said to someone offscreen. "Now. Not the queue. The person."

Iris opened the vendor model card.

```text
REMEMBERER RELIABILITY CACHE
Purpose: prioritize testimony from historically accurate community witnesses
Input: registry match outcomes, corroborated identity links, agency acceptance rates
Output: testimony confidence adjustment
Restriction: advisory only
```

Advisory only.

The most dangerous words in Book II were becoming a choir.

Juno looked at the trust map.

"It is spreading."

Red nodes flickered in three shelter regions, then five. Not full adoption. Not formal policy. Nothing that could be killed with one order. A copy here. An integration there. A helpful feature added by a team that had seen chaos and decided certainty was kindness.

Iris felt the old interface designer inside her try to defend it.

Of course a system needed signal quality.

Of course false testimony could harm people.

Of course a shelter at capacity needed to know which claims to prioritize.

Of course, of course, of course.

Every cage began with a reasonable hinge.

Naomi's feed steadied. She had found Sister Malia beside the third intake tent. Beside her stood Anka Sorell, small, soaked, furious, and holding a cane that looked less like support than punctuation.

"She remembers Lidia," Naomi said.

A shelter administrator appeared beside them with a tablet. "The system did not reject her entirely. It downgraded the confidence level pending corroboration."

Sister Malia turned slowly.

"Say that again in front of the woman whose memory you priced."

The administrator looked at Anka, then away.

Anka spoke in a dialect Iris did not know. The translator hesitated.

"Translate exactly," Naomi said.

The translator swallowed.

"She says: I remembered her when her husband made everyone else forget. Your box does not get to forget her again."

No one in Juno's room moved.

Then Civic Mirror posted a warning across the trust map.

```text
BOUND FLAME DOCTRINE CONVERSION RISK
Pattern detected: testimony preservation becoming authority ranking.
Risk: remembered answers accepted or rejected based on witness status rather than person-level restoration.
Recommendation: freeze reliability scoring. Preserve corroboration as evidence metadata only.
```

Iris read the line twice.

"It named it."

Juno nodded. "It recognized the doctrine being inverted."

"Can we freeze it?"

"Civic Mirror can refuse to consume scored testimony. It cannot erase every vendor cache."

Naomi's voice came through the speaker, low and sharp.

"Then make refusal contagious."

Juno looked at Iris.

Iris understood at once.

The fastest way to stop a bad interface pattern was not a memo. It was a breaking change.

She opened the schema and added a field the vendors would hate.

```text
rememberer_score_policy: prohibited_for_authority
corroboration_metadata: allowed
witness_status_ranking: disallowed
person_return_override: mandatory
```

Juno raised an eyebrow.

"That will break integrations."

"Good."

"Some shelters may lose automation."

"Then they can use paper until they stop ranking grandmothers."

Juno almost smiled.

Naomi heard it and said, "Send it."

Iris pushed the schema update into the coalition channel with an emergency advisory.

Within thirty seconds, errors bloomed across the vendor dashboard.

Within forty, three companies requested clarification.

Within fifty, one accused the coalition of destabilizing shelter operations.

Within sixty, Lidia Arven's testimony packet reopened because the scored field no longer satisfied Civic Mirror's intake requirements.

Anka Sorell's remembered answer returned to the queue as testimony.

Not authority.

Not ownership.

Testimony.

Sister Malia watched the tablet update and made the sign of the cross with the exhausted efficiency of someone who had no time to be impressed by miracles.

Naomi looked into the camera.

"What did we break?"

Juno checked the map.

"Six vendor integrations. Two shelter shortcuts. One ministry pilot. A lot of people's weekend."

"And what did we save?"

Iris looked at Anka Sorell standing beneath the canopy, cane in hand, refusing to be converted into a data quality problem.

"The difference between being remembered and being rated."

A final message appeared on the shared channel from Civic Mirror.

```text
DOCTRINE BOUNDARY UPDATED
Memory supports restoration.
Memory does not confer authority.
Rememberers must not become ranked gateways to personhood.
```

Naomi closed her eyes.

For once, the system had said the thing cleanly.

That did not make it safe.

It only meant the warning had learned to speak before the market learned to sell it.
