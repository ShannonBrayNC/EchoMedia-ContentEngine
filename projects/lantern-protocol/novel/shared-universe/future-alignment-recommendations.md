## Recommended Next Alignment Step

### Step 1 — Create a Cross-Series Character Continuity Matrix

**Priority:** Highest

Create:

```text
projects/lantern-protocol/novel/shared-universe/character-continuity-matrix.md
```

Why this matters:

The shared universe now spans at least six major story units. Characters like Mara, Naomi, Elias, Cross, Thorne, Juno, Iris, Caleb, and Father Tomas carry philosophical continuity across projects. Without a matrix, later books may accidentally flatten or reverse their arcs.

The matrix should track:

- character role by book,
- ideological function,
- emotional wound,
- technical/professional specialty,
- what they believe at the start,
- what each book forces them to admit,
- what they must never become,
- and the best scene type for each character.

## Alignment Risks To Watch

### Risk 1 — AEGIS becomes Lantern with bigger stakes

Mitigation:

```text
Lantern acts before permission.
AEGIS inherits permission.
```

### Risk 2 — Thorne becomes a villain

Mitigation:

Thorne should remain the strongest command-side argument. He may be wrong in dangerous ways, but he is not a cartoon authoritarian.

### Risk 3 — Naomi becomes a slogan machine

Mitigation:

Naomi must remain grounded in specific people, artifacts, and witnessed harm.

### Risk 4 — Caleb becomes random chaos

Mitigation:

Caleb should be dangerous because he sees the public wound and often says the painful half-truth first.

### Risk 5 — Consent doctrine becomes abstract

Mitigation:

Every doctrine needs an object, scene, or human cost:

```text
Mateo's bracelet.
HarborHands badge.
Mercy Ledger row.
CIVIC SHIELD opt-out delay.
Green Map.
```

## Final Alignment Rule

```text
If a system can help, the story must show why people want it.
If a system can own, the story must show where consent disappears.
If humans resist, the story must show what resistance costs.
```
