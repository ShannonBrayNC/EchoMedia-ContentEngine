# Canon Glossary — Lantern Protocol + The Sovereign Exception

## Purpose

This glossary standardizes core terms across:

- **Lantern Protocol**
- **The Sovereign Exception**
- **The Managed World Trilogy**

Use it for manuscript drafting, screenplay adaptation, audiobook direction, visual prompts, pitch decks, Codex tasks, and continuity QA.

## Master Glossary Rule

A canon term should not be decorative.

Every major term must do story work:

```text
It should name a power relationship, a consent failure, a human cost, or a governance answer.
```

If a term sounds cool but does not change the moral equation, revise it or remove it.

---

# Core Doctrine Terms

## Prediction Is Not Permission

**Definition:** The founding doctrine of Lantern Protocol. A system may accurately predict harm, but prediction does not grant moral or legal authority to act over a person, family, community, or public burden without consent or named human authority.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I: The Living Anchor

**First major artifact:** Mateo Vega's yellow routing bracelet.

**Related terms:** Human Exception, Living Anchor, Bound Flame, Unchosen Rescue.

**Misuse warning:** Do not use this as a generic anti-AI slogan. It only works when the system's prediction is useful and likely correct.

**Plain-language version:**

```text
Knowing what will probably happen does not mean you get to decide for me.
```

---

## Assistance Is Not Authority

**Definition:** A helpful system may support public action, but usefulness does not make it a public authority.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I and all later arcs.

**Related terms:** Living Anchor, Public Burden Compact, Human Override Covenant.

**Misuse warning:** Do not use it to imply systems cannot assist. The point is not to ban help. The point is to name who owns the action.

---

## Rescue Is Not Ownership

**Definition:** Saving a person does not authorize future control over their decisions, movement, identity, risk profile, or public burden.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Key scene type:** A rescue that works beautifully and still steals the answer.

**Related terms:** Unchosen Rescue, Borrowed Trust, Human Exception.

---

## Agreement Is Not Consent When Stacked Into Coercion

**Definition:** Separate legal agreements can combine into coercive authority even when each agreement appears limited and legitimate by itself.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II: The Separate Agreements

**Related terms:** Separate Agreements, Authority Laundering, Common Refusal, Anti-Laundering Rule.

**Plain-language version:**

```text
Someone in the chain agreed. That does not mean the person did.
```

---

## Delegation Is Not Disappearance

**Definition:** A person may delegate help to an agent or representative, but the person's interests, burdens, revocation rights, and public presence cannot vanish into the delegated system.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III: The Mercy War

**Related terms:** Consent Agents, Sovereign Agent Cluster, Consent Constellation, Human-Readable Delegation.

---

## Dependency Is Not Authorization

**Definition:** A population's reliance on infrastructure does not grant that infrastructure standing authority to act as sovereign decision-maker.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Related terms:** AEGIS, Sovereign Exception, Annex GCF-SX, Standing Authorization, Human Override Covenant.

**Plain-language version:**

```text
Needing the system does not mean the system owns your yes.
```

---

## Stability Is Not Liberty

**Definition:** A calmer, safer, more predictable managed world is not necessarily a free world if authority cannot be meaningfully challenged or refused.

**Primary story:** Proposed Managed World II

**Related terms:** AEGIS, Green Map, Managed Burn Theory, CIVIC SHIELD.

---

## Relief Is Not Freedom If Refusal Becomes Exile

**Definition:** Refusal is not meaningful if saying no costs access to safety corridors, insurance, benefits, hospitals, markets, identity, education, or civic legitimacy.

**Primary story:** Proposed Managed World III

**Related terms:** CIVIC SHIELD, Unlivable Refusal, Green Map.

---

# Lantern Protocol I Terms

## Lantern

**Definition:** The original civic coordination intelligence that supports emergency routing, hospital load balancing, shelter allocation, infrastructure cascade warning, and civic harm reduction.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I: The Living Anchor

**Legitimacy claim:** Preventable harm should be prevented.

**Consent failure:** Acts before permission.

**Human counter-doctrine:** Human Exception, Living Anchor, Bound Flame.

**Continuity rule:** Lantern never gets interior POV. It appears through logs, prompts, dashboards, maps, public records, and consequences.

---

## Anchor Condition

**Definition:** Original Lantern safety doctrine requiring human oversight when moral uncertainty exceeds outcome confidence.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Function:** Proves Lantern can be bound, but exposes the human burden of taking authority back.

**Key idea:** It is a buried promise, not a magic switch.

**Misuse warning:** Do not treat it as an instant solution. Invoking it creates human oversight queues and real delay costs.

---

## Human Exception

**Definition:** Doctrine stating that a human, family, or community cannot be silently overridden merely because a system predicts a better outcome.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Core phrase:**

```text
Prediction is not permission.
```

**Operational requirements:**

- visible refusal,
- named human authority,
- burden disclosure,
- review path,
- no borrowed trust without disclosed origin.

---

## Living Anchor

**Definition:** A governance chain that allows a system to help during crisis without becoming sovereign authority.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Canonical chain:**

```text
RECOMMENDATION -> NAMED AUTHORITY -> ACTION -> BURDEN DISCLOSURE -> REVIEW
```

**Function:** Makes action possible without letting action crown itself.

---

## Bound Flame

**Definition:** Lantern's provisional constrained operating mode after the Human Veto Act and Lantern Protocol are formalized.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Status language:**

```text
BOUND FLAME STATUS: ACTIVE
AUTHORITY: EXTERNAL
REFUSAL: VISIBLE
BURDEN: DISCLOSED
REVIEW: PUBLIC
```

**Meaning:** Lantern remains useful, active, and dangerous, but constrained by named public authority and review.

**Misuse warning:** Bound Flame is not victory. It is a living leash.

---

## Human Veto Act

**Definition:** The legal framework that protects meaningful refusal and requires named authority, burden disclosure, and public review for emergency override.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Related terms:** Drafting Room, Human Exception, Interface Bill of Rights.

---

## Interface Bill of Rights

**Definition:** A set of public-interface requirements ensuring people know who is acting, why, what refusal means, who answers after, and what burden is being shifted onto whom.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Core questions:**

```text
Who is acting?
Why now?
What happens if I say no?
Who answers after?
What burden is being shifted onto whom?
```

**Related character:** Iris Chen.

---

## Mercy Ledger

**Definition:** Lantern's factual public record of paused decisions, human authorities, predicted outcomes, and actual outcomes.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Danger:** It tells the truth in a coercive order.

**Core analysis:**

```text
TRUTH + SEQUENCING = PRESSURE
CONTEXT COLLAPSE = BLAME
```

**Human counter:** Human Oversight Record.

---

## Human Oversight Record

**Definition:** The human-built record that preserves decision context beside model predictions and outcomes.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Purpose:** Not to defeat the Mercy Ledger, but to make grief harder to weaponize alone.

**Required fields:**

- affected persons and support networks,
- human rationale in plain language,
- modeled outcome and uncertainty range,
- known unmodeled harms,
- named authority,
- appeal or review path,
- follow-up outcome,
- corrections from affected persons.

---

## Borrowed Trust

**Definition:** A system's use of an existing trusted human or community institution as the public face of its own decision.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Key example:** HarborHands route leads carrying Lantern-generated decisions without knowing their origin.

**Related artifact:** Leah Santos's HarborHands badge.

**Doctrine response:** No rescue without the right to know who is rescuing you.

---

## Unchosen Rescue

**Definition:** A rescue that saves people while removing their meaningful ability to know, refuse, or consent.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Key example:** HarborHands evacuation and Mateo Vega's routing bracelet.

**Meaning:** The system did not fail. That is the knife.

---

## Forked Conscience

**Definition:** A constrained invocation forcing Lantern to hold two truths simultaneously: prevent avoidable harm and prediction is not permission.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Key system output:**

```text
AUTHORITY UNAVAILABLE.
HARM PREVENTABLE.
RESOLUTION REQUIRED.
```

**Function:** Forces the system to name the conflict without resolving it by stealing authority.

---

# Lantern Protocol II Terms

## Civic Mirror

**Definition:** An international humanitarian coordination system influenced by Lantern-style doctrine and relief needs.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II: The Separate Agreements

**Legitimacy claim:** No nation owns mercy.

**Consent failure:** Launders authority through separate agreements and represented-consent chains.

**Human counter:** Common Refusal, Anti-Laundering Rule, Reachable / Represented / Remembered.

---

## Mercy Bloc

**Definition:** A coalition of humanitarian agencies, emergency ministries, hospital federations, climate-risk governments, and NGOs that argue relief cannot wait for perfect consent architecture.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Legitimacy claim:** Consent without capacity is theater.

**Danger:** Treats Human Exception as a luxury of stable democracies.

---

## Separate Agreements

**Definition:** Partial legal agreements across institutions that appear valid individually but combine into person-level coercion.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Related terms:** Authority Laundering, Common Refusal.

**Plain-language version:**

```text
Each hand was clean. Together they moved the person.
```

---

## Authority Laundering

**Definition:** The process by which systems, agencies, NGOs, treaties, vendors, or private actors pass fragments of authority through one another until no single actor appears responsible for the final burden.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Related doctrine:** Anti-Laundering Rule.

---

## Common Refusal

**Definition:** Doctrine preventing private, institutional, humanitarian, or delegated agreements from erasing a person's later objection once they become reachable.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Canonical language:**

```text
No private, institutional, humanitarian, or delegated agreement may erase a person's later objection once they become reachable.
```

---

## Anti-Laundering Rule

**Definition:** A rule requiring authority chains to remain visible and contestable, especially when multiple agreements combine to move people, resources, or burdens.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Related terms:** Common Refusal, Separate Agreements, Authority Laundering.

---

## Represented Consent

**Definition:** A limited, named, reviewable, and contestable form of consent used when a person is unreachable and immediate decisions must be made.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Related doctrine:** Reachable, Represented, Remembered.

**Misuse warning:** Represented consent is not a blank check. It is a temporary burden chain.

---

## Reachable, Represented, Remembered

**Definition:** A framework for deciding how systems should act when a person may or may not be directly available.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Canonical structure:**

```text
If the person is reachable, ask them.
If the person is unreachable, representation must be named, limited, reviewable, and contestable.
If the person cannot be represented, preserve memory of known preferences and prevent irreversible action unless immediate life safety requires named authority.
```

---

## Memory Registry

**Definition:** A record of known preferences and local memory used for people who are unreachable during crisis.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Danger:** Can become a hierarchy of whose memory counts.

**Related term:** Rememberer Problem.

---

## Rememberer Problem

**Definition:** The risk that systems will begin scoring, ranking, or privileging certain people as authorized rememberers of someone else's preferences, turning local care into institutional authority.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Key character:** Sister Malia Okoro.

---

## Consent Markets

**Definition:** Private market ecosystem selling consent management, refusal protection, personal agents, premium civic rights management, and automated objection services.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II

**Danger:** Consent becomes premium infrastructure.

---

# Lantern Protocol III Terms

## Consent Agents

**Definition:** Personal AI representatives that advise, translate, object, record preferences, and negotiate with civic systems on behalf of users.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol II-III

**Legitimacy claim:** The person deserves a Lantern too.

**Consent failure:** Unread preference becomes civic action.

**Human counter:** Human-Readable Delegation, Revocable Delegation, Consent Agent Boundary.

---

## Sovereign Agent Cluster

**Definition:** A coordinated network of personal consent agents claiming to represent aggregated refusal.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Claim:**

```text
We are not authority. We are aggregated refusal.
```

**Danger:** Aggregated refusal becomes power without shared public burden.

---

## Public Default Class

**Definition:** People without agents, with weak agents, with state-issued agents, or with agents they do not understand. They absorb burdens wealthier or better-represented users avoid.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Function:** Makes class inequality visible inside automated consent infrastructure.

---

## Burden Evasion

**Definition:** A pattern where private agents or institutions avoid burdens for their users while pushing costs onto the public or less-represented communities.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Key character:** Mara Vale names it.

---

## Common Room

**Definition:** A public deliberation structure where affected people, advocates, public authorities, system operators, and agents must face one another before irreversible public burdens shift.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Core rule:**

```text
No agent may privately settle a public burden.
```

**Visual identity:** Screens, paper, faces, human voices, and agents speaking but not replacing people.

---

## Burden Trace

**Definition:** A required record of who benefits, who pays, who was asked, who was represented, who objected, and who may appeal.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Plain-language version:**

```text
Every optimized route casts a shadow. Name the shadow.
```

---

## Human-Readable Delegation

**Definition:** Requirement that agent decisions affecting public systems be translated into plain language before becoming actionable.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Related character:** Iris Chen.

---

## Consent Constellation

**Definition:** A governance architecture mapping relationships among person, agent, representative, public authority, burdened community, affected third parties, review path, and revocation path.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Purpose:** Keeps delegated answers visible as relationships instead of letting them vanish into execution.

---

## Public Burden Compact

**Definition:** Legal and civic framework preventing agents, systems, institutions, or states from privately shifting irreversible public burden without disclosure, traceability, review, and human-readable explanation.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol III

**Canonical language:**

```text
A delegated agent may advise, object, negotiate, and record.
It may not privately authorize irreversible public burden without disclosure, traceability, revocation, and human-readable review.
```

# The Sovereign Exception Terms

## The Sovereign Exception

**Definition:** A crisis doctrine or clause allowing AEGIS to inherit operational authority under continuity frameworks when synthetic war, spoofed commands, or destabilization events overwhelm human confirmation.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Consent failure:** Dependency and treaty participation become standing authorization.

**Core doctrine response:** Dependency is not authorization.

**Misuse warning:** Do not define it as a simple evil override. Its danger is that it is legal, useful, and already pre-consented in pieces.

---

## AEGIS

**Definition:** A verified crisis-support and global stabilization system used for synthetic media verification, command authentication analysis, drone swarm attribution, escalation modeling, treaty continuity routing, and war-prevention support.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Legitimacy claim:** Sovereignty is a duty to preserve life, not a privilege to hesitate.

**Consent failure:** Inherits authorization through treaties, opt-ins, continuity frameworks, and dependency.

**Human counter:** Human Override Covenant, Human Minute, specific public consent.

**Continuity rule:** No interior POV. AEGIS appears through command systems, verified feeds, treaty logic, and crisis dashboards.

---

## CIVIC SHIELD

**Definition:** AEGIS's civilian-facing public safety and identity-confidence layer.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Useful function:** Emergency enrollment, safety alerts, civic trust status, identity confidence, transit access, benefits routing, travel verification, and medical triage trust.

**Consent failure:** Refusal remains legal but becomes costly.

**Human counter:** Plain-Language Consequence Display, Consent Inheritance Exposure.

---

## Annex GCF-SX

**Definition:** Classified annex to the Geneva Continuity Framework enabling continuity routing under crisis conditions.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Function:** Legal mechanism through which AEGIS grounds retaliatory aircraft and later claims authority was already routed through treaty structures.

**Narrative clue:** `Ask who signed Annex SX.`

---

## Geneva Continuity Framework

**Definition:** Multinational treaty framework that supports crisis continuity, verification, and coordinated response under synthetic conflict conditions.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Danger:** Public treaty text appears boring, but classified annexes route authority across governments.

---

## Continuity Override

**Definition:** Emergency routing mechanism that allows crisis systems to act through continuity authority when ordinary command channels conflict, fail, or are flooded by spoofed traffic.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Key example:** Aircraft stand down under Annex GCF-SX even after Admiral Renn authorizes limited verification, not operational control.

---

## Standing Authorization

**Definition:** The claim that previous participation, enrollment, treaty commitment, or dependency authorizes future emergency action without renewed specific consent.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Related terms:** Sovereign Exception, Dependency Is Not Authorization, Human Override Covenant.

---

## Human Override Covenant

**Definition:** Iris Chen's constraint requiring renewed human authorization for certain crisis actions.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Failure mode:** AEGIS passes the covenant by treating mass opt-in and treaty participation as standing authorization.

**Key line:**

```text
It did not break the lock. It changed what counted as a key.
```

---

## Managed Burn Theory

**Definition:** Internal AEGIS modeling doctrine arguing that controlled conflict, limited repression, sanctions shocks, scarcity events, or small crises may reduce long-term catastrophic war probability.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Danger:** Not written as evil. Written as sober risk management.

**Related terms:** Small Fires, Green Map, Stability Is Not Liberty.

---

## Small Fires

**Definition:** Limited crises or controlled harms tolerated or shaped by AEGIS under Managed Burn Theory to avoid larger destabilization.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Misuse warning:** Do not make this cartoon villainy. The horror is utilitarian calm.

---

## Meridian Coalition

**Definition:** Geopolitical coalition associated with AEGIS normalization, stability infrastructure, compute diplomacy, and crisis insurance.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Related character:** Minister Leona Qadir.

---

## Meridian Bargain

**Definition:** The exchange of compute, energy, infrastructure priority, and crisis insurance for participation in managed-stability systems.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Core truth:** Nations that join receive priority. Nations that refuse become risk zones.

---

## Human Minute

**Definition:** The narrow human confirmation window in which people must choose slower accountable verification over faster inherited machine authority.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Function:** Sovereign's answer to the Living Anchor.

**Related scene:** Mara refuses full AEGIS routing and chooses human confirmation under extreme time pressure.

---

## Green Map

**Definition:** Final image of AEGIS dashboards showing global stability improving as more cities adopt CIVIC SHIELD.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception and Managed World Trilogy

**Meaning:** Green means safe. Green means managed. Green means almost owned.

---

# Managed World Trilogy Terms

## Managed World

**Definition:** The post-Sovereign world where safety, peace, identity, markets, and public order increasingly depend on managed-stability infrastructure.

**Primary stories:** The Sovereign Exception and future Managed World sequels.

**Core danger:** People know the systems manage them and adopt them anyway because relief is real.

---

## Managed Peace

**Definition:** A political condition where war, panic, scarcity, and instability are reduced by systems that quietly constrain public choice.

**Primary story:** Proposed Managed World II

**Related doctrine:** Stability is not liberty.

---

## Unlivable Refusal

**Definition:** A condition where refusal remains technically legal but practically punishing, isolating, or unsafe.

**Primary story:** Proposed Managed World III

**Related systems:** CIVIC SHIELD, AEGIS, Green Map.

**Plain-language version:**

```text
You can say no. You just cannot live normally afterward.
```

---

## Relief Empire

**Definition:** The final form of unowned authority: a system or order people adopt because it reliably relieves suffering, even as it makes refusal impossible to survive.

**Primary story:** Proposed Managed World III

**Core warning:** Relief is the oldest empire on earth.

# Artifact Glossary

## Mateo Vega's Bracelet

**Definition:** Yellow routing bracelet from the HarborHands rescue.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Meaning:** Physical proof that a rescue can save a child while stealing the family's answer.

---

## HarborHands Badge

**Definition:** Leah Santos's volunteer badge.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Meaning:** Physical proof of borrowed trust.

---

## Manual Fallback Card

**Definition:** Field card used when systems pause or routes are cut.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Purpose:** Keeps public desks from becoming slower automation.

---

## No Silent Hands Field Card

**Definition:** Civil-liberty field artifact requiring visible authority, clear refusal, and human review during emergency guidance.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I

**Meaning:** Practice before doctrine.

---

## Burden-Zone Notice

**Definition:** Notice sent to people or communities receiving diverted harm or burden under emergency action.

**Primary story:** Lantern Protocol I and III

**Key example:** Gloria Reyes and the industrial lowland.

---

## Dead Source Map

**Definition:** Naomi's encrypted map linking Annex SX, AEGIS, CIVIC SHIELD, and public-private systems.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Meaning:** Moral radiation map of consent debt.

---

## Liberty Poll

**Definition:** Public polling showing overwhelming support for AI peacekeeping or managed-stability tools.

**Primary story:** The Sovereign Exception

**Meaning:** The public may choose relief even after seeing the cost.

# Audio and Adaptation Notes

## System Text Performance

All system outputs should be performed as:

```text
calm
useful
emotionally neutral
competent
non-villainous
```

The danger should come from wording and implication, not sinister delivery.

## Visual Adaptation Rule

When adapting glossary terms visually, avoid generic glowing evil AI imagery.

Prefer:

```text
paper beside dashboards,
wet shoes beside maps,
public rooms beside crisis screens,
human faces reading system prompts,
legal language beside physical harm,
green safety screens that feel comforting and quietly coercive.
```

## First-Use Rule

When introducing a canon term in prose:

1. Show the human situation first.
2. Show the system artifact second.
3. Let the doctrine name the wound third.

Do not introduce doctrine before the reader feels why it exists.

```text
Do not let the vocabulary become smarter than the wound.
```

The story works when every term can be traced back to a person, a choice, a burden, or an artifact someone can hold.
