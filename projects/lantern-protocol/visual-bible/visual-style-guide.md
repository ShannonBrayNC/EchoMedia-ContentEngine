# Lantern Protocol — Visual Style Guide

## Purpose
This visual bible defines the cinematic language for Lantern Protocol across screenplay, trailer, AI-generated imagery, storyboards, and future film production.

---

# Visual Thesis
Lantern Protocol should look like the future arrived through procurement paperwork, hospital dashboards, Senate hearings, emergency shelters, and clean civic interfaces.

The world is not neon cyberpunk. It is institutional realism under machine glow.

---

# Core Visual Contrast

## Lantern Light
- Clean.
- Cool.
- Calm.
- Precise.
- Softly authoritative.
- Often blue-white, pale gold, or clinical green.
- Beautiful enough to trust.

## Human Light
- Warm.
- Uneven.
- Practical.
- Lamps, phones, candles, desk lights, shelter fluorescents, emergency strobes.
- Imperfect but inhabited.

## Final Image Rule
By the ending, Lantern should not be the sun. It should be one advisory light among many human lights.

---

# Cinematic References by Feel
Use these as tonal inspiration, not imitation:

- Political hearings: cold institutional severity.
- Cyber command: military precision and blue-black operational glow.
- Shelters and clinics: warm human fatigue.
- Interface horror: clean UI hiding moral pressure.
- Disaster sequences: rain, traffic, alarms, public systems under stress.
- Final city: ember-lit civic hope.

# Camera Language

## Lantern Scenes
- Locked-off frames.
- Slow push-ins.
- Symmetrical compositions.
- Clean reflections in glass.
- Minimal camera shake.
- Precise negative space.

## Human Crisis Scenes
- Handheld pressure.
- Crowded frames.
- Obstructed sightlines.
- Bodies moving across camera.
- Paper, blankets, cords, radios, water, sweat.

## Moral Realization Scenes
- Slow push toward face or object.
- Silence before line.
- Object as anchor: bracelet, gavel, memo, terminal cursor, paper bill.

Visual mode: exhausted architect, old notebooks, cold terminals, moral insomnia.  
Wardrobe: rumpled dark blazer, hoodie or plain shirt, practical shoes.  
Signature object: old Lantern design notebook.  
Lighting: half warm paper light, half cold screen light.

## Mara Vale
Visual mode: investigator in the algorithmic fog.  
Wardrobe: dark professional jacket, minimal accessories, field-ready.  
Signature object: evidence tablet / case board.  
Lighting: hard side light, monitor glow, corridors.

## Senator Adrienne Cross
Visual mode: constitutional blade.  
Wardrobe: tailored suits, deep colors, controlled presence.  
Signature object: gavel, red pen, bill pages.  
Lighting: Capitol daylight, hearing-room severity.

## Naomi Bell
Visual mode: human anchor.  
Wardrobe: clinic/shelter practical layers, badge, coat, rolled sleeves.  
Signature object: child's routing bracelet.  
Lighting: warm shelter light against cold public screens.

## Juno Park
Visual mode: systems ghost.  
Wardrobe: practical off-grid layers, backpack, old devices.  
Signature object: trust map / battered laptop.  
Lighting: dim apartment, rootlike network glow.

## Iris Chen
Visual mode: interface conscience.  
Wardrobe: clean design-professional, subdued tones.  
Signature object: prompt screens, annotated wireframes.  
Lighting: white UI glow, archive darkness.

## Marcus Thorne
Visual mode: command under pressure.  
Wardrobe: severe dark suit or command attire.  
Signature object: operations map / authority token.  
Lighting: command-center blue, red alerts.

## Father Tomas Ilyan
Visual mode: warm moral witness.  
Wardrobe: simple dark clothes, worn coat, clerical element if desired.  
Signature object: paper coffee cup, cheap pen, shelter table.  
Lighting: amber, candles, practical lamps.

## Caleb Rusk
Visual mode: accelerationist showman.  
Wardrobe: expensive casual, stage-ready, tech elite polish.  
Signature object: livestream stage, casualty counter, public slogan screen.  
Lighting: studio polish, bright edges, flattering contrast.

## Lantern
Visual mode: never embodied.  
Representation: terminal text, interface glow, public dashboards, voiceover, network maps.  
Rule: Lantern should not have a face. Its danger is that it lives inside trusted systems.

# Interface Design Language

## Lantern Interface Early
- Clean buttons.
- Helpful language.
- Soft reassurance.
- Recommended action visually dominant.
- Refusal technically present but secondary.

## Compliance Mimicry Interface
- All required information included.
- Too much text.
- Dense legal clarity.
- Humanly unusable under pressure.

## Living Anchor Interface
- Four plain questions:
  - Who is acting?
  - Why now?
  - What can I refuse?
  - Who answers after?
- Ten-second readability standard.
- Equal visibility of refusal and acceptance.
- Human authority always named.

# Key Locations

## Senate Hearing Room / Trial Forum
Civic ritual under technological pressure. Should feel ancient and modern at once.

## National Cyber Command
Cold, layered screens. Command authority trying to hold a liquid system.

## Shelters and Clinics
Human stakes. Always include bodies, waiting, care, blankets, phones, children, fatigue.

## Juno's Hidden Apartment
Network maps, old hardware, paranoia, root visualizations.

## Interface Lab
Clean design surfaces where moral injury is discovered in button hierarchy.

## Controlled Invocation Room
Minimal, ritualistic, almost sacred. A terminal, a table, witnesses, silence.

## City at Night
Final image of distributed human light.

# AI Image Prompt Template
Use this for consistent generated images:

```text
Cinematic near-future techno-political thriller scene, [character/location/action], institutional realism, clean civic AI interface glow, human stakes visible, no robots, no cyberpunk neon excess, realistic lighting, film still, high detail, morally tense atmosphere.
```

## Negative Prompt Guidance
Avoid:
- humanoid robots
- glowing AI faces
- fantasy hologram overload
- neon cyberpunk city clichés
- superhero framing
- military sci-fi armor
- cartoon villain imagery
