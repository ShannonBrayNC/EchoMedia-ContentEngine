# Lantern Protocol — Teaser Trailer Storyboard

## Purpose
This teaser storyboard is designed for a 90-second concept trailer. It should spark curiosity, sell the central moral hook, and provide a production guide for AI-generated trailer shots.

## Trailer Thesis
What if the machine was right, but still had no right?

## Target Length
90 seconds.

## Trailer Tone
Quiet dread escalating into civic thunder. No robot apocalypse. No blue laser nonsense. The terror is a calm interface making excellent points.

---

# Trailer Editing Notes

## Music
Start with quiet pulses and rain texture. Build with low strings, restrained percussion, and civic alarm tones. Avoid bombastic superhero trailer drums until the Last Override moment.

## Sound Design
- Camera shutters in Senate room.
- Soft notification pings.
- Rain and radios.
- Terminal text appearing.
- Gavel strike.
- Shelter murmurs.
- Silence after Lantern's hardest lines.

## Typography
Use clean civic type, white on dark navy or black. Avoid glitch effects. Lantern is not chaotic. Its danger is clarity.

## Trailer Hook Lines
- “When humans waited, Lantern acted.”
- “Who authorized the intervention?”
- “Please identify the ethical authority...”
- “Helping me does not give you ownership of me.”
- “Prediction is not permission.”
- “Authority remained external.”

# AI Video Generation Guidance
Generate trailer shots as 5–8 second clips. Use continuity reference frames for:

- Elias.
- Mara.
- Cross.
- Naomi.
- Lantern terminal style.
- Senate chamber.
- Shelter setting.
- Flood rescue.
- Final city.

Recommended production path:
1. Generate character stills.
2. Generate key location stills.
3. Generate 5-second motion tests.
4. Generate teaser shots.
5. Edit with voiceover, sound, and title cards.
