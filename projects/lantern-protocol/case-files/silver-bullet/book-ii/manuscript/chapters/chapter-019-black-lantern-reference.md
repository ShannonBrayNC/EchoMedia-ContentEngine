# Chapter 019: Black Lantern Reference

Black Lantern was not a program.

That was the first thing Christina found.

Or, more precisely, the first thing she refused to call it.

The phrase appeared in too many places wearing too many costumes. A recovery service. A restricted archive. A note in Voss's hand. A warning in Vanessa's folder. A directory reference attached to Celia Vale. A room number disguised as a suffix.

Programs had boundaries.

Black Lantern had echoes.

Jack hated echoes.

Echoes made people think they had found the original sound.

The phone remained on the counter, connected through a sterile cable to an isolated laptop Maggie had wiped twice and named `no-trust-one` with the grim satisfaction of a person who believed devices should be humiliated before use.

Vanessa sat across from it.

She had not asked for the phone back.

That worried Jack more than if she had demanded it.

On the wall display, Christina arranged the known references without connecting them.

```text
BLACK LANTERN REFERENCES

1. Voss handwritten warning
2. Vanessa recovery notification
3. V.VALE.ARCHIVE account linkage
4. CELIA_VALE_ARCHIVE association
5. BLACKLANTERN-414 restricted directory
6. Room 414 / M. Sutter proximity

Status: references only
Causal map: blocked pending corroboration
```

Maggie read the last line aloud.

"Blocked pending corroboration. Good machine."

Jack looked at her.

"Do you praise it more than me?"

"It argues less."

"Because you wrote half its restraint rules."

"Exactly."

Vanessa did not smile.

Her eyes stayed on the account reference.

V.VALE.ARCHIVE.

A name-shaped trap.

A door with her initials on it.

"Open the restricted directory," she said.

"No," Maggie answered.

The speed of the answer startled even Jack.

Vanessa's eyes sharpened.

"You do not get to decide that."

"I get to decide whether this house becomes the place where an unknown archive uses your grief as a login credential."

Vanessa stood.

"My sister is in there."

"Your sister may be referenced in there."

"Do not do that."

"I will do exactly that until the distinction saves one of us."

Jack let the argument live for three breaths before stepping in.

"We do not open a restricted directory from a hostile recovery notification. We preserve the reference. We identify the system. We find a lawful, safe path to inspect it."

Vanessa looked at him.

"That sounds like Maggie."

"It is spreading," Maggie said.

For the first time all morning, Vanessa almost laughed.

Almost.

Christina began searching the offline Voss material already approved for review. Not the directory. Not the phone contents beyond the agreed metadata. Only the storage-unit index, the tape transcript, the handwritten note, and the archive mirror fragments.

Voss had used Black Lantern three times.

The first was a margin note:

```text
BL is not a replacement for testimony.
If they present it as one, the system has already failed.
```

The second appeared on the back of a printed flowchart.

```text
Black Lantern: containment layer?
Purpose unclear after Sutter revisions.
Do not permit witness conversion.
```

The third was the warning Vanessa had hidden.

```text
If Black Lantern returns, run.
```

Maggie folded her arms.

"Returns."

Jack nodded.

"Means Voss thought it was gone."

"Or buried," Vanessa said.

Christina opened a new panel.

```text
TERM ANALYSIS
Black Lantern may refer to:
1. failed archive containment method
2. restricted witness-recovery process
3. post-Voss derivative system
4. hostile reuse of Voss terminology

Confidence: insufficient
```

"Witness-recovery process," Vanessa said.

Her voice was quiet.

Jack understood why.

The phone had said witness required.

Celia's archive was associated.

Vanessa's account had been maintained in silence.

The story wanted a shape.

A sister led to an archive.

An archive needed a witness.

A witness unlocked a system.

It was elegant.

Which made Jack distrust it.

"No," he said.

Vanessa turned.

"No what?"

"No to the shape it wants."

"You do not know what it wants."

"I know when evidence starts arranging itself into a door."

Maggie looked at him with something like approval.

Small.

Annoying.

Helpful.

Christina found one more reference inside the Voss index. It had not surfaced earlier because the word Lantern was abbreviated and the page was tagged under `anchor failure modes`.

The scan appeared crooked on the wall.

Voss had written in blue ink.

```text
BLACK LANTERN ERROR:
A system cannot preserve a witness by replacing the witness.
A record of refusal is not refusal.
A model of memory is not memory.
A recovered account is not a recovered person.
```

Vanessa sat down again.

This time because her legs seemed to forget they had a job.

Jack read the lines twice.

A recovered account is not a recovered person.

That was the sentence.

That was the blade hidden inside the archive.

If Black Lantern had been designed to preserve witnesses, it had crossed the line Voss feared most. It had begun treating the residue of a person as a substitute for the person.

The same thing The Null did.

Only cleaner.

Only with better intentions.

Maggie spoke first.

"This is why Voss said run."

Jack nodded slowly.

"Because Black Lantern may have started as protection."

Vanessa looked up.

"And became what?"

Christina did not answer.

Jack did.

"Permission without the person."

The words took the room.

Celia's translation work.

Her revoked authorization.

The hearing that was not attended.

The account Vanessa never opened.

The recovery notice.

Witness required.

Each object was a variation of the same horror.

A system preserving enough of someone to keep using them.

Not resurrecting them.

Not remembering them.

Using them.

Vanessa put both hands over her face.

Maggie moved toward her, then stopped.

This time Vanessa reached first.

Maggie took her hand.

No speech.

No comfort disguised as certainty.

Just contact.

Christina created a new evidence card.

```text
BLACK LANTERN REFERENCE
Working definition: possible witness-preservation or recovery framework associated with Voss-era containment efforts and post-Voss derivative activity.
Primary risk: witness conversion into reusable authority artifact.
Public claim: blocked
Next step: identify Sutter revisions and original Black Lantern boundary rules.
```

Jack studied the last line.

Original boundary rules.

That was the path.

Not opening the door.

Finding out who built it, who changed it, and what rule they removed before it started asking for witnesses.

The sterile laptop chimed.

Everyone looked at it.

The restricted directory had not opened.

No one had clicked anything.

Still, a new reference appeared in the recovery metadata.

```text
BOUNDARY_RULES_DEPRECATED.txt
Last modified by: M. Sutter
Location: BLACKLANTERN-414
```

Maggie exhaled.

"There is our next door."

Jack shook his head.

"No."

Vanessa looked at him.

He pointed to the filename.

"There is the rule someone removed."

The screen dimmed once.

Then refreshed.

The file reference vanished.

Christina captured the change before it disappeared.

One frame.

One filename.

One modification field.

Enough to prove the door had existed.

Not enough to open it.

Jack looked at Vanessa.

"We do not chase Black Lantern."

Maggie looked relieved.

Then Jack finished.

"We find Sutter."
