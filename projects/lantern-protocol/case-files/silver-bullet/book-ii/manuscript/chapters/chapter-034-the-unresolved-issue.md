# Chapter 034: The Unresolved Issue

The problem with unresolved issues was that they rarely stayed unresolved.

They evolved.

They accumulated paperwork.

They acquired committees.

They changed names.

They buried themselves beneath procedure until nobody remembered the original wound.

Then one day they returned wearing a different badge.

Room 414 had returned.

Which meant the old issue had returned too.

Nobody said that out loud at first because everyone in the room already knew it.

The restored Transition Review fragments remained open on the table display.

Black Lantern suspended.

Archive transfer authorized.

Witness continuity issue unresolved.

The last official sentence before history disappeared.

Maggie tapped the screen.

"Start here."

Nobody argued.

The room had finally become disciplined enough to stop chasing the biggest mystery and focus on the next one.

Christina began reconstructing the final hours from artifacts, timelines, and gaps.

Especially gaps.

The first discovery arrived quickly.

```text
TRANSITION REVIEW

Adjourned: 17:12

Facility access records:
E. Voss departure: Unavailable
C. Vale departure: Unavailable
M. Sutter departure: 17:19
C. Reed departure: 17:23
```

Nobody spoke.

The absence was louder than the data.

No departure records for Voss.

No departure records for Celia.

Caroline closed her eyes.

"I stopped looking at that part."

Jack understood. Some gaps punish the person who keeps staring.

Christina continued.

A maintenance-style entry surfaced from the classification system.

```text
CLASSIFICATION REVIEW

Status: Pending
Review Owner: E. Voss
Secondary Reviewer: C. Vale
Subject: Witness Continuity
```

The room became still.

No warning. No alarm. No dramatic label.

Just a review.

A review that never finished.

Vanessa stared at the screen.

"That's the unresolved issue."

Nobody answered because she was right.

Christina opened another fragment. Damaged. Mostly unrecoverable.

One paragraph survived.

```text
Question:

If witness continuity cannot be preserved through records, what remains?

Response:
...
```

The answer was gone.

Of course it was.

Then Sutter surprised everyone.

"I remember."

Nobody moved.

Sutter stared past the fragment, years past it.

"Celia answered first. She said trust remains."

The room changed.

Trust remains.

Human. Messy. Fragile. Real.

Caroline nodded slowly.

"Yes."

Sutter continued.

"Elias disagreed. He said the witness remains."

That got everyone's attention.

Caroline leaned forward.

"No. That's not what happened."

The room froze.

Preserving disagreement suddenly felt less theoretical.

Caroline pointed at the fragment.

"Elias said responsibility remains."

Nobody moved.

Three answers.

Trust.

Witness.

Responsibility.

All three felt true.

All three felt incomplete.

Exactly the kind of problem that creates systems.

Exactly the kind of problem that destroys them.

Christina processed the responses and created a comparison.

```text
CELIA
Trust remains

VOSS
Responsibility remains

SUTTER MEMORY
Witness remains
```

Nobody liked the mismatch.

Good.

The mismatch was probably important.

Jack stood and walked toward the board. Room 414. The Anchor notes. Celia's page.

Then stopped.

"What if the unresolved issue wasn't the answer?"

Silence.

He pointed at the three responses.

Trust. Responsibility. Witness.

Three different people.

Three different truths.

Three different priorities.

Then he pointed toward Celia's page.

Preserve disagreement.

The shape became visible.

The unresolved issue was not which answer was correct.

The unresolved issue was whether the disagreement itself could survive.

Caroline sat back slowly.

"My God. The review never failed. The disagreement survived."

Vanessa stared at the board.

"The disagreement is still happening."

Trust.

Responsibility.

Witness.

Still alive.

Still unresolved.

Still preserved.

Exactly the way Celia wanted.

Exactly the way Voss feared and hoped.

Exactly the thing Black Lantern could not tolerate.

Christina interrupted with a new update.

```text
ROOM 414 UPDATE

Prior selection revoked.
New selection recorded.
Subject: Vanessa Vale
Status: Pending
```

Nobody spoke.

Because suddenly the classification had stopped asking about philosophy.

And started choosing people.
