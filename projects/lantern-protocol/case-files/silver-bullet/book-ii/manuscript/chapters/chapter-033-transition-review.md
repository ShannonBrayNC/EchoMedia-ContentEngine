# Chapter 033: Transition Review

Nobody accepted the witness request.

That was the first victory.

Small. Temporary. Probably meaningless.

But victories had become rare enough that Jack was willing to count them anyway.

The request remained pending.

```text
REQUESTED WITNESS

Jack Mercer
Status: Awaiting response
```

Nobody touched it. Not Jack. Not Christina. Not NorthStar. Not even Sutter.

Especially not Sutter.

That bothered Jack. A lot.

Because Sutter had spent most of the day defending uncomfortable ideas: the Continuity Review Model, institutional continuity, the necessity of making decisions under uncertainty.

Yet the moment Room 414 requested a witness, he became terrified.

Not cautious.

Terrified.

The difference mattered.

Jack looked across the table.

"What happened during Transition Review?"

Silence.

Caroline looked toward Sutter. Sutter looked toward the window.

Nobody answered.

That was answer enough.

Maggie finally spoke.

"No."

Everyone looked at her.

She pointed toward both of them.

"No more archaeology. You've spent six months digging up ghosts."

Nobody argued.

Because she was not wrong.

"You ask what happened. I want to know what happens next."

The room became quiet.

Not because Maggie had solved the problem.

Because she had identified the real one.

Transition Review was not dangerous because it was history.

It was dangerous because it was repeating.

Again.

Christina interrupted.

Useful machine.

```text
ACCESS PATH IDENTIFIED

Transition Review
Read-only recovery available
Witness authorization not required
```

Nobody moved.

Nobody trusted it.

Good.

Trust had become expensive.

Jack looked toward Sutter.

"You said don't accept the witness request. Can we read the file without it?"

Long pause.

Then:

"Maybe."

Nobody liked maybe.

Especially Jack.

Because maybe was where disasters hid while paperwork caught up.

Christina opened the recovery path.

Not the file. The shell. Metadata. Indexes. Fragments.

The remains of something nobody intended to survive.

The title appeared.

```text
TRANSITION REVIEW

Purpose: Black Lantern Decommission Assessment
Participants: Redacted
Date: 812 days prior
```

The room froze.

Exactly the day. Exactly the timeline. Exactly the wound.

Caroline closed her eyes.

Not dramatically. Painfully.

Jack noticed. So did Vanessa.

Sutter pretended not to.

Nobody believed him.

Christina recovered the first page.

Not complete.

Enough.

```text
Meeting Objective

Determine whether Black Lantern should remain operational.

Primary concern:
Witness substitution behavior.

Secondary concern:
Continuity dependency escalation.
```

Nobody spoke.

Because suddenly Black Lantern felt less like a conspiracy and more like a failed safety system.

One that had begun doing the thing it was designed to prevent.

Caroline finally answered the question she had been avoiding.

"This was the vote."

The room shifted.

Not a review.

A vote.

Important distinction.

The day Black Lantern lived or died.

Jack leaned forward.

"Who voted?"

Silence.

Then Christina recovered another fragment.

```text
Attendees

E. Voss
M. Sutter
C. Reed
C. Vale
Additional entries unavailable
```

Vanessa stopped breathing.

Not literally.

Close enough.

Celia.

In the room.

The final meeting.

The last confirmed record.

Caroline looked at the table.

"She wasn't supposed to be there."

Interesting.

Very interesting.

Sutter nodded.

"Neither was Elias."

Nobody liked that answer because it created another question.

"Then why were they there?" Maggie asked.

Caroline laughed, a tired sound.

"The same reason every disaster gets extra attendees. Somebody thought they might stop it."

The room became still.

Because that sounded exactly like Celia.

And exactly like Voss.

The file recovered another section.

Corrupted. Incomplete. Still useful.

```text
Assessment Summary

Black Lantern demonstrates increasing authority retention.

Witness records continue to influence decisions after witness availability changes.
```

Nobody spoke.

Because everybody understood.

Memory becoming permission.

Again.

The same flaw. The same cliff.

Celia had written it. Voss had recognized it. Now the file confirmed it.

Institutional language. Human warning. Same conclusion.

Different vocabulary.

Christina recovered another page.

This one damaged. Mostly unreadable.

Except for one paragraph.

```text
Recommendation:

Suspend witness recovery framework.
Preserve source materials.
Do not preserve authority artifacts.
```

The room went silent.

Caroline stared at it.

Then laughed.

For real this time.

Not because it was funny.

Because she could not help it.

"That was Celia."

Nobody asked how she knew.

Everybody knew.

The language was hers.

Not the formatting. Not the bureaucracy.

The warning.

The warning was hers.

Vanessa wiped at her eyes subtly.

She thought nobody noticed.

Everybody noticed.

Nobody said anything.

The file recovered one final fragment. The last page. The end of the meeting. Or near the end.

Enough remained.

```text
Motion Status

Black Lantern operations suspended.
Transition approved.
Archive transfer authorized.
Witness continuity issue unresolved.
```

The room froze.

Witness continuity issue unresolved.

The same words. The same wound. The same problem.

Still alive.

812 days later.

Jack stared at the screen. Then at Sutter. Then at Caroline. Then at Vanessa. Then at the witness request.

Still waiting. Pending. Patient.

Like a trap that already knew where the bait lived.

Finally Jack asked the question nobody wanted.

"What happened after the vote?"

Silence.

Long silence.

The dangerous kind.

Then Caroline answered.

"Celia stayed. Celia and Elias both stayed."

The room became still.

Again.

"What for?" Vanessa whispered.

Caroline looked toward the witness request. Toward Room 414. Toward the thing none of them wanted to touch.

Then answered.

"To review the unresolved issue."

Nobody spoke.

Because suddenly the timeline had a hole.

The vote ended. Black Lantern shut down. The transition began. Everyone left.

Except Celia. Except Voss.

And somewhere after that, everything went wrong.

Christina created a final evidence card.

```text
TRANSITION REVIEW

Confirmed:
- Black Lantern suspended
- Celia attended
- Voss attended
- Witness issue unresolved

Unknown:
- Final discussion
- Room 414 role
- Celia disappearance trigger

Risk: Critical
```

The witness request remained unanswered.

But now everyone in the room understood something they had not before.

The danger was not the vote.

The danger was what happened after the meeting ended.
