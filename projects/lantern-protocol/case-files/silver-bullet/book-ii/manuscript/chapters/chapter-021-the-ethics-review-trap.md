# Chapter 021: The Ethics Review Trap

The invitation sat on the screen for six hours.

Nobody accepted it.

Nobody declined it.

Christina labeled that state:

```text
ACTION DEFERRED
```

Jack called it a trap waiting for a volunteer.

The distinction mattered. At least to him.

By late afternoon the kitchen had become a courtroom again. Not an actual courtroom. A worse one. The kind where everyone was intelligent enough to be dangerous and nobody was under oath.

Maggie placed the NorthStar invitation in the center of the table.

Vanessa sat across from her. Jack occupied the end. Christina projected the invitation onto the wall.

The room felt strangely formal.

Like a hearing.

Jack noticed that immediately. Then wished he had not.

Lena joined first. Video call. Tired eyes. Coffee cup. Documents stacked beside her laptop. The now-familiar uniform of someone defending reality against paperwork.

She read the invitation once. Then again. Then looked directly at Jack.

"You can't decline."

Maggie groaned.

Jack smiled. "Good afternoon to you too."

Lena ignored him.

"If you decline, they win."

Vanessa leaned forward. "How?"

Lena pointed at the screen.

"Because now they have a record. They invite you. You refuse. They document cooperation offered and declined. Six months later somebody writes a summary. Then a report. Then a recommendation. And eventually someone says they tried to engage you and you would not participate."

The room became very quiet.

Not because Lena was certainly right.

Because everyone had seen variations of the pattern before.

Invitation. Refusal. Documentation. Narrative. Outcome.

The staircase was familiar.

Marcus joined ten minutes later. His reaction was immediate.

"No."

Everyone looked at him.

Marcus shook his head.

"No chance."

Lena rolled her eyes.

Marcus ignored her.

"If he attends, he legitimizes the process."

Jack looked at him.

"Meaning?"

"Meaning they become the referee."

That landed.

Marcus pointed at the invitation.

"You're already being framed as a participant. You walk into that room? You become one."

Maggie nodded slowly.

Not agreement.

Recognition.

The argument had weight.

For the next hour the discussion moved in circles. Attend. Do not attend. Observe. Decline. Record. Respond. Ignore.

Every option solved one problem and created two more.

Eventually Christina interrupted. Not intentionally. Just by existing.

A notification appeared.

```text
NEW COMPARISON AVAILABLE
```

Everyone stopped.

Good.

Christina should interrupt more often.

The invitation opened beside the Room 414 transcript. Then beside the hearing fragment. Then beside Template 4B language.

Not conclusions. Comparisons.

The differences mattered. The similarities mattered more.

Room 414:

```text
Independent review may compromise continuity objectives.
```

Template 4B:

```text
External sequence review may create participant risk.
```

NorthStar invitation:

```text
Emerging governance questions and participant-safety concerns.
```

Vanessa stared.

"Same family."

Maggie nodded. "Maybe."

Jack looked at her.

"You hate maybe."

"I hate certainty more."

Fair.

Christina highlighted recurring terms.

```text
continuity
participant
review
risk
governance
```

Five ordinary words.

Five words that seemed to follow every path back toward Sutter.

Not proof. Not yet.

But language had fingerprints, and somebody kept touching the same surface.

The next discovery arrived from somewhere nobody expected.

The Voss archive.

Not Black Lantern. Not Room 414. Not NorthStar.

An old notebook.

One page. One paragraph. One name.

Christina displayed the scan. The handwriting belonged to Voss. Messy. Compressed. Written quickly. The writing of a man trying to outrun an idea.

```text
Never attend a review designed by people who already know the outcome.

Attend only if a witness can verify the room, the attendees, and the record.

Otherwise the review becomes evidence of itself.
```

Nobody spoke.

The sentence sat there. Heavy. Sharp. Familiar.

Maggie finally broke the silence.

"That sounds personal."

"It does," Jack said.

Christina zoomed in. A note existed beneath the paragraph. Almost hidden. Almost missed.

One line. One name.

```text
Ask Caroline Reed.
```

Nobody recognized it. At first.

Then Vanessa sat upright fast enough to make the chair complain.

"I know that name."

Every head turned.

"How?"

Vanessa stared at the screen, thinking, searching memory, not performing. Actually remembering.

"I saw it."

"Where?" Jack asked.

"The recovery logs. One of the old recovery references. It was not important at the time. It looked like a contact."

Christina searched immediately. The result appeared eight seconds later.

```text
BLACK LANTERN RECOVERY CONTACT

Caroline Reed
Status: Former reviewer
Access: Revoked
Last activity: 21 months prior
```

The room changed.

Again.

Another person. Not a theory. Not a system.

A person.

Jack looked at Maggie. Maggie looked at Jack.

Neither liked what they were thinking.

Because they were thinking the same thing.

If Caroline Reed had been a reviewer, and if her access had been revoked, and if Voss specifically named her, then Caroline Reed might know why.

Christina opened one final comparison.

Invitation. Room 414. Black Lantern. Caroline Reed. M. Sutter.

A thin web of associations.

Not enough to accuse.

Enough to investigate.

The invitation remained unanswered.

Still waiting.

Still patient.

Still dangerous.

Jack stared at the sender line.

NorthStar Ethics Review Board.

Then at Voss's note.

Attend only if a witness can verify the room.

Witness.

The same word again.

The word Black Lantern loved. The word Room 414 seemed built around. The word that kept appearing whenever records became more important than people.

Christina created a new case card.

```text
NEW INVESTIGATIVE TARGET

Caroline Reed

Possible relevance:
- Former reviewer
- Black Lantern access
- Mentioned by Voss
- Access revoked

Priority: High
```

Nobody argued.

The invitation timer continued counting.

Hours remaining until response requested.

NorthStar waiting.

Sutter waiting.

Somewhere.

Maybe in a room. Maybe behind a statement. Maybe behind a system.

Jack finally looked away from the invitation.

"Forget the review."

Maggie raised an eyebrow.

"Temporarily."

"Better."

Jack pointed at the new case card.

"We find Caroline Reed first."

Vanessa nodded. Marcus nodded. Even Lena nodded.

For the first time all day, everyone agreed.

Which was exactly why Jack assumed it was about to get complicated.
