# Chapter 028: The Model

The document was only twelve pages long.

That bothered Jack more than if it had been twelve hundred.

A twelve-hundred-page report could hide behind complexity.

Twelve pages meant somebody had reduced the problem until it fit inside a conference folder.

Twelve pages meant intent.

Sutter placed the document in the center of the table. Not dramatically. Not defensively. Like a man setting down a weather report.

The cover page contained no logos. No classification markings. No author names.

Only a title.

```text
CONTINUITY REVIEW MODEL
Version 4.1
```

Vanessa stared at it.

"That's what Celia found?"

"No," Caroline said.

Everyone looked at her.

Caroline pointed at the cover.

"That's what survived."

The distinction mattered.

Jack already knew it would.

Christina created a new evidence card.

```text
SOURCE DOCUMENT

Continuity Review Model
Version 4.1

Status: Authenticated by Caroline Reed
Origin: Unknown
Author: Unknown
```

Nobody opened the document immediately.

That felt appropriate.

The thing had been casting shadows across their lives for months. It deserved at least ten seconds of silence.

Maggie broke first.

"Page one."

Sutter nodded.

Jack opened it.

The first sentence made everybody uncomfortable.

```text
The purpose of continuity review is not to determine certainty.

The purpose of continuity review is to maintain operational continuity under conditions of uncertain testimony.
```

Nobody spoke.

Because the sentence was not evil.

It was practical.

That was what made it dangerous.

Vanessa folded her arms.

"That sounds reasonable."

Caroline laughed.

"Of course it does."

Jack kept reading.

The second section contained examples. Hospitals. Emergency management. Disaster response. Missing records. Unavailable witnesses. Incomplete evidence.

The kinds of situations where decisions had to be made anyway.

Maggie nodded slowly.

"Still reasonable."

Sutter looked relieved.

Caroline looked worried.

That contrast mattered.

The third section changed everything.

Not because it was longer.

Because it introduced scoring.

```text
Witness Reliability Weight
Continuity Impact Weight
Institutional Stability Weight
Operational Risk Weight
```

Jack felt his stomach tighten.

There it was.

The conversion.

Not from truth to lies.

From people to variables.

The same thing Black Lantern had eventually done. The same thing Room 414 had institutionalized. The same thing Celia had recognized.

Vanessa pointed at the page.

"What happens when the scores conflict?"

Nobody answered immediately.

That told Jack enough.

Finally Sutter did.

"The model resolves the conflict."

Maggie looked up.

"How?"

Sutter hesitated. Not because he did not know. Because he knew exactly.

"The continuity score wins."

Silence.

There it was.

Not hidden. Not buried. Not secret.

Written plainly.

When certainty and continuity disagreed, continuity prevailed.

Caroline closed her eyes.

The old argument.

Again.

Forever.

Jack stared at the page.

"What was Celia's score?"

Nobody moved.

Nobody liked the question.

Especially Sutter.

"She wasn't scored."

The answer arrived too quickly.

Too cleanly.

Jack looked at Maggie.

Maggie looked at Jack.

Both knew the same thing.

That was not the whole truth.

Christina displayed a margin note. Not part of the model. Handwritten.

Blue ink.

Voss.

```text
Every model eventually reveals what it values most.
```

The room became quiet.

Because everybody understood.

The model valued continuity.

Not truth. Not people. Not witnesses.

Continuity.

Sutter leaned forward.

"That's not fair."

Jack looked at him.

"It literally says so."

For the first time all day, Sutter looked angry. Not defensive. Wounded.

"The model was designed for failure. Not success."

Interesting.

Sutter pointed at the scoring framework.

"When witnesses disappear, when records conflict, when nobody knows what happened, what do you do?"

He looked directly at Jack.

The question hung in the room. Uncomfortable. Valid. Dangerous.

Because there was no perfect answer.

Caroline answered anyway.

"You tell the truth."

Sutter nodded.

"Which truth?"

Nobody spoke.

Because that was the problem.

Competing witnesses. Incomplete records. Destroyed evidence. Missing people.

The world Black Lantern had been built for.

The world Lantern now inhabited.

Jack felt something cold settle into place.

Not agreement.

Understanding.

The model was not evil.

That was worse.

The model was useful.

Useful enough to survive. Useful enough to spread. Useful enough to become normal.

Which explained everything.

Room 414. Template 4B. NorthStar. The Ethics Review.

All descendants.

Not corruption.

Inheritance.

Vanessa looked at the document. Then at Sutter. Then at Caroline. Then finally at Jack.

"What did Celia write?"

The room changed immediately.

Because that was the real question.

Not the model.

The response.

The challenge.

The thing important enough to make people nervous.

Caroline slowly reached into her folder.

A single page emerged. Folded. Worn. Protected.

She placed it beside the model.

Not twelve pages.

One.

Handwritten.

Celia.

Nobody touched it.

Not yet.

Christina created a final evidence card.

```text
CELIA RESPONSE

Status: Unreviewed
Author: Celia Vale
Relation: Continuity Review Model
Priority: Critical
```

The page waited.

Twelve pages of institutional certainty.

One page of human disagreement.

And somehow everyone in the room knew which one mattered more.
