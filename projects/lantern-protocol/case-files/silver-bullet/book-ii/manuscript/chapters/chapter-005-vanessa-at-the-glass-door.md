# Chapter 005: Vanessa at the Glass Door

Vanessa Vale arrived during the rain with no umbrella and no visible hurry.

Jack saw her through the front camera first. Dark coat. Silver earrings. One hand tucked around a phone, the other holding a paper envelope against her chest. She looked at the camera as if she knew exactly where the lens lived and had decided to make politeness uncomfortable.

Maggie glanced at the feed. "Do we know her?"

"No."

"Then we especially know nothing."

Jack opened the door anyway, which told him something about the kind of trouble he had become vulnerable to.

Vanessa was not beautiful in the simple way cameras rewarded. She was composed in a way that made the porch light seem poorly briefed. Her eyes moved once over his shoulder, once to his right arm, once to the tablet in his left hand. She noticed too much and pretended not to keep the peace.

"Jack Mercer?"

"Depends who is asking."

"Vanessa Vale. I was told you preserve sequence better than institutions do."

Maggie appeared beside him. "Who told you that?"

Vanessa's smile held for exactly one beat too long.

"A dead man."

That got Christina's attention. The hall screen woke behind them.

Vanessa opened the envelope and removed a photograph, not a printed screenshot but actual glossy paper. It showed Elias Voss standing beside a whiteboard filled with circles, arrows, and one sentence written larger than the rest.

**THE NULL IS NOT ABSENCE. IT IS THE PLACE POWER HIDES AFTER DELEGATION.**

Jack's mouth went dry.

Voss was supposed to be a ghost in Lantern's architecture, a name attached to unfinished protocol notes and old case fragments. Voss had built the original doctrine Jack had operationalized without fully understanding. Jack did not like owing his survival to a dead man with better secrets.

"Where did you get this?" he asked.

"From a storage unit in Durham that someone paid to keep alive after Dr. Voss disappeared."

"Disappeared," Maggie repeated.

"That is the polite word."

Rain tapped the porch roof. Jack did not invite Vanessa in. That felt important.

Vanessa looked past him again, not at Maggie this time but at the lit wall where Christina had gone still.

"Your archive is already in the next story," she said. "The people who understand that moved before you did."

Jack wanted to dismiss her. Too elegant. Too timed. Too much like a doorway placed by someone who knew his weaknesses. But the photograph was real enough to make his hands cold, and the phrase on the board had appeared in Voss's encrypted notes under another name.

Null corridor.

Maggie took the photo with a tissue, because by now romance, mystery, and chain of custody had to share a room.

"What do you want?" she asked.

Vanessa's eyes softened, or performed softness so well it became useless to distinguish.

"I want to know why my sister's name vanished from a record that still uses her life to justify decisions."

Jack stepped back then.

Not trust.

Not surrender.

A measured opening.

Vanessa crossed the threshold and brought cold rain smell into the house. Echo created a new file before anyone asked.

```text
NEW SUBJECT: Vanessa Vale
Claim: missing sister record / Voss-linked artifact
Risk: romantic leverage, planted evidence, legitimate witness, all possible
Recommended posture: preserve first, believe slowly
```

Vanessa read the recommendation from across the room and smiled without warmth.

"Smart machine."

Jack looked at the photograph again.

On the whiteboard, beneath the sentence about The Null, someone had drawn a small lantern with the flame crossed out.
