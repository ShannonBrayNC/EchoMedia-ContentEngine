# Chapter 024: Forty-Eight Hours

The timer appeared at 7:43 p.m.

Nobody created it.

That was what bothered Jack.

Christina simply surfaced it from the invitation metadata.

A countdown already existed.

NorthStar had built it.

Someone expected an answer.

```text
ETHICS REVIEW

Response Requested:
47:59:58
47:59:57
47:59:56
```

The seconds continued falling. Patient. Mechanical. Certain.

Everything Jack distrusted.

Maggie looked at the display. Then at Jack. Then back at the display.

"Turn it off."

Christina complied immediately.

The timer vanished.

The pressure did not.

Vanessa remained seated beneath the evidence board. The words still hung there.

**Celia Anchor**

Nobody had solved them.

Nobody had disproven them.

The phrase had become furniture.

Dangerous furniture.

Jack poured coffee.

Nobody wanted coffee.

Everyone accepted coffee.

That felt about right.

The house had become something strange during the last two weeks. Not headquarters. Not a command center. Not a war room.

A witness room.

The thought arrived unexpectedly.

Jack hated it immediately.

Christina interrupted.

Again.

Useful machine.

```text
NEW EVENT

NorthStar Ethics Review
Status: Updated
```

The invitation changed.

Not dramatically.

One new attachment.

```text
ATTENDEE LIST
```

Nobody liked that.

Because everybody opened it.

Three names appeared.

```text
M. Sutter
Caroline Reed
Jack Mercer
```

Silence.

Vanessa stood first.

"No."

Maggie stood second.

"Absolutely not."

Jack stared at the screen.

His own name already existed.

Again.

Room 414.

The hearing that wasn't there.

The false transcript.

Now another attendance list.

Different building. Different review. Same pattern.

The room became quiet enough to hear the refrigerator again.

Caroline answered on the first call this time.

That was worse.

Jack put her on speaker.

No introductions. No small talk. No pretending any of this was normal.

"Did you agree to attend?"

Caroline laughed once.

"No."

Vanessa closed her eyes.

"You were listed."

"I know."

The room froze.

Jack felt something settle into place.

Not understanding. Recognition.

Caroline continued.

"They listed me three days ago. I declined. Then they listed me anyway."

There it was.

Not proof.

Not yet.

Pattern.

The same thing Room 414 had done. The same thing Black Lantern had done. The same thing Celia's authorization dispute had done.

Participation after refusal.

Jack looked toward the wall. Toward the Anchor notes. Toward the Living Anchor fragments. Toward Voss.

The same wound.

Again.

Caroline's voice sharpened.

"Listen carefully. M. Sutter believes institutions fail because witnesses disappear. He thinks systems need continuity. He is not entirely wrong."

That was the hardest part.

The villains who frightened Jack most were always right about something.

Caroline continued.

"The problem is what comes next. If continuity becomes more important than consent, people become components."

Nobody moved.

Nobody even breathed correctly.

People become components.

Celia. Thomas Vale. Jack. Caroline.

Every road pointed to the same cliff.

Maggie finally spoke.

"What happens if Jack attends?"

Silence.

Then:

"I don't know."

Nobody liked that answer.

Because Caroline never sounded uncertain.

"The review isn't normal," Caroline said.

"How?" Jack asked.

Caroline hesitated for the first time.

"Because NorthStar should not know about the Anchor question."

The room changed.

Jack felt it immediately.

"What do you mean?"

Caroline answered quietly.

"I never told them. I never told anyone."

The silence became physical.

The Anchor discussion happened here. Inside this house. Inside Lantern. Inside trusted systems.

And now NorthStar apparently knew it existed.

Vanessa looked toward her phone immediately.

Maggie noticed. Jack noticed both.

Nobody said anything.

Christina did.

```text
NEW MESSAGE

Sender: M. Sutter
```

The room froze.

Not NorthStar. Not Ethics Review Board. Not Compliance.

M. Sutter.

Directly.

Nobody touched the message for twelve seconds.

Then Maggie nodded once.

"Open it."

Jack did.

The message contained only three lines.

```text
Mr. Mercer,

You are investigating the wrong witness.

The Anchor is not Celia.

-- M. Sutter
```

Nothing else.

No attachment. No explanation. No threat.

Just certainty.

Jack hated certainty.

Vanessa stared at the screen.

"The Anchor is not Celia."

Nobody moved.

Nobody spoke.

Because that sentence solved nothing.

It only changed the shape of the mystery.

Christina created a new evidence card.

```text
DIRECT SUTTER CONTACT

Claims: Anchor not equal to Celia
Status: Unverified
Intent: Unknown
```

The countdown resumed.

Not on the screen.

Inside the room. Inside every conversation. Inside every decision.

Forty-seven hours. Then forty-six. Then forty-five.

NorthStar waiting. Sutter watching. Caroline frightened. Vanessa uncertain. Maggie preparing.

Jack thinking about one sentence.

People become components.

The line Voss refused to cross. The line Sutter apparently crossed willingly. The line Lantern existed to stop.

Jack looked around the room.

At the people.

Not the systems. Not the records. Not the archives.

The people.

Then he made the decision.

"We're going."

Silence.

Maggie closed her eyes.

Vanessa stared.

Christina recorded the statement.

Jack continued.

"But not for the review."

Nobody understood.

Not yet.

Good.

Because neither did he.
