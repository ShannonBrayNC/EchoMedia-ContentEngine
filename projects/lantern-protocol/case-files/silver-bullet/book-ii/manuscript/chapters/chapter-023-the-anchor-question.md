# Chapter 023: The Anchor Question

The word sat on the wall for twenty-three minutes.

Nobody touched it.

Nobody defined it.

Nobody even wanted to be first.

That alone told Maggie it was dangerous.

```text
UNCONFIRMED TERM

CELIA ANCHOR

Source: Caroline Reed interview
Status: Undefined
```

Jack stood at the far end of the kitchen. Vanessa sat beneath the display. Maggie occupied the middle.

The geometry felt appropriate.

Three people.

Three theories.

Three different fears.

Jack hated the word immediately.

Not because it sounded mystical.

Because it sounded useful.

Useful words caused trouble. People built systems around useful words. Then they forgot the people.

Vanessa broke first.

"What if she left evidence?"

Maggie shook her head.

"That's not an anchor."

"Why not?"

"Because evidence points. Anchors stabilize."

Nobody argued.

Because Maggie was right.

Unfortunately.

Christina displayed the known references.

```text
ANCHOR REFERENCES

Voss: Living Anchor
Caroline: Celia Anchor
Black Lantern: Witness preservation conflict

Status: insufficient evidence
```

Jack folded his arms.

"I don't think it's a person."

Vanessa looked at him.

"Caroline literally said Celia."

"No. Caroline said Anchor. Not person."

Vanessa looked unconvinced.

Maggie looked interested.

Those were different things.

Jack walked to the display slowly, thinking out loud. Dangerous behavior. Necessary behavior.

"Black Lantern tried to preserve records. Right?"

Nobody objected.

"It preserved summaries. Interpretations. Confidence scores."

Maggie nodded.

"Until it started replacing witnesses."

Jack pointed at her.

"Exactly."

He hated saying exactly. Life punished exactly.

"If Black Lantern replaces witnesses, then Lantern has to do the opposite."

Vanessa stared. Realization beginning. Not finished. Beginning.

"A witness who cannot be replaced."

Nobody spoke.

Christina highlighted an older Voss note. One they had not understood correctly at the time.

```text
Technology cannot be the Anchor.

Authority cannot be the Anchor.

The witness must remain.
```

The room changed. Not dramatically. Quietly. The way understanding usually arrived.

Vanessa looked away first because she understood before she wanted to.

"Celia."

Jack nodded slowly.

"Maybe."

Maggie immediately frowned.

Good.

Someone should.

"Maybe isn't evidence."

"Still isn't."

"Then stop saying it like one."

Fair.

Christina opened another note from the storage-unit archive. Previously ignored, not because it lacked value, but because nobody knew where it fit.

Now it fit.

```text
Failure Mode:

System preserved testimony.
System preserved decisions.
System preserved authority.
Witness absent.

Result: False continuity.
```

Nobody liked that because everybody understood it.

Room 414.

Celia revoked authorization. The system continued.

Thomas Vale died. The hearing continued.

Black Lantern archived recovery accounts. The person remained absent.

False continuity.

The phrase felt horrible, which was usually a sign it was important.

Vanessa stared at the screen.

"Celia wasn't the Anchor."

Jack looked at her.

"What?"

She stood slowly.

"Celia found it."

Nobody spoke.

Vanessa pointed at the notes. At the recovery records. At the hearings. At the authorization dispute.

"She saw it."

The room became very still.

Not because the theory was correct.

Because it was good.

Good theories were dangerous. They made people comfortable.

Maggie wrote it down anyway.

```text
Theory A: Celia is Anchor
Theory B: Celia discovered Anchor
Theory C: Someone wants us asking the wrong question
```

Jack added Theory C reluctantly.

Maggie nodded.

Best theory so far.

Christina created a comparison board.

Not conclusions.

Possibilities.

Exactly the way Voss would have wanted. Probably. Maybe.

Jack hated maybe.

The notification arrived at 7:41 p.m.

Nobody noticed immediately. The conversation had become too focused, too human, too interesting.

Christina noticed.

Of course.

```text
NEW MESSAGE

NorthStar Ethics Review Board
```

The room went silent again.

Jack opened it.

The message was short. Professional. Polite. Threatening in the way only institutions could manage.

```text
Attendance confirmation requested.

Review begins in 48 hours.

Witness participation recommended.

Failure to participate may limit panel conclusions.
```

Maggie laughed once. No humor.

"There it is."

Jack read it again.

Witness participation recommended.

Witness.

Again.

Always witness. Always participation. Always continuity. Always the same language family.

Vanessa stared at the screen. Then at the Anchor board. Then at the old Voss note.

"The review isn't looking for evidence."

Nobody answered.

Because nobody needed to.

She finished the thought herself.

"It's looking for a witness."

The room went cold.

Not supernatural cold.

Recognition cold.

The kind that arrived when a theory stopped feeling theoretical.

Christina created a final evidence card.

```text
ANCHOR QUESTION

Current assessment:

Black Lantern: preserve record
Room 414: preserve decision
Unknown: preserve witness
Review deadline: 48 hours
```

Jack stared at the card. Then at the countdown timer. Then at the word witness.

Somewhere inside NorthStar, someone wanted a witness. Somewhere inside Black Lantern, someone had wanted one before.

And somewhere in the middle of all of it, Celia Vale had disappeared.
