# Chapter 035: The New Witness

Nobody spoke for seven seconds.

Christina recorded six.

Maggie insisted it was eight.

That disagreement turned out to be the most comforting thing in the room.

The update remained on the wall.

Unblinking.

Patient.

Certain.

```text
PREVIOUS REQUEST
Jack Mercer
REVOKED

NEW REQUEST
Vanessa Vale
Status: Pending
```

Vanessa stared at it the way people stare at medical results before opening them, the way people stare at a courtroom door before hearing a verdict, the way people stare at their own name when it appears somewhere it should not.

Jack looked at Sutter.

Sutter looked at the floor.

Interesting.

Very interesting.

Because for the first time since entering NorthStar, Sutter looked surprised.

Not concerned.

Not thoughtful.

Surprised.

Caroline noticed too.

"That's new."

Nobody asked what she meant.

Because they all knew.

Sutter had not expected the change.

Which meant somebody else was making decisions.

Or something else.

Maggie immediately corrected herself internally.

Not something.

Someone.

Always someone.

That rule had saved them more than once.

Vanessa finally found her voice.

"Why me?"

Nobody answered.

Because nobody knew.

The room had spent weeks discussing witnesses, months discussing Celia, years in Sutter's case.

And somehow the first direct selection had landed on the one person everyone had been trying to protect.

Christina began comparing the two requests.

```text
REQUEST COMPARISON

Jack Mercer
Basis: Independent investigation
Reason: Unknown
Revocation: Recent

Vanessa Vale
Basis: Unknown
Reason: Unknown
Activation: Immediate
```

Not helpful.

Infuriatingly not helpful.

Which usually meant they were close to something important.

Jack walked to the evidence board.

Room 414.

Black Lantern.

The Anchor.

Celia.

Vanessa.

The shape was there.

He could feel it.

The problem was that feeling a pattern and proving a pattern were entirely different professions.

Caroline stood slowly, carefully, like someone approaching a memory she did not trust.

"I think I know."

Nobody interrupted.

Good.

Caroline pointed toward the board.

Not at Celia.

At the timeline.

The timeline nobody had examined since the witness request changed.

Christina enlarged it.

```text
812 days ago
Transition Review
Black Lantern suspended
Celia remains

811 days ago
Classification inactive

Current Day
Classification active
Vanessa requested
```

Silence.

Then Caroline pointed to the gap.

The single day between them.

One day.

Twenty-four hours.

Missing.

Nobody had noticed.

Because everyone had been staring at years.

Not days.

Caroline's voice dropped.

"Celia disappeared after the classification shut down."

The room froze.

Not because the statement was new.

Because the sequence was.

Everyone had unconsciously assumed Celia disappeared and then Room 414 stopped.

But the timeline suggested something else.

Room 414 stopped.

Then Celia disappeared.

The distinction mattered.

A lot.

Jack felt the floor shift beneath the investigation.

Not physically.

Structurally.

"What are you saying?"

Caroline answered immediately.

"I'm saying Celia wasn't the target. Celia interrupted something. She discovered something. She protected something."

The room tightened.

Not around Celia.

Around the missing piece.

The thing nobody had identified.

The thing the classification still wanted.

The thing important enough to survive 812 days.

Jack looked toward the Anchor notes. Then toward Celia's page. Then toward Vanessa.

The realization arrived slowly.

Which made it worse.

The Anchor was not Celia. The Anchor was not an object. The Anchor was not a role assigned by an institution.

The Anchor emerged wherever disagreement survived.

And Celia had spent her final known days protecting it.

Vanessa sat down hard.

The room felt smaller suddenly.

Not because of the request.

Because of the implication.

If Celia had protected the Anchor, and if Room 414 was active again, and if Vanessa was now the requested witness, then the classification was not looking backward.

It was continuing.

Sutter finally spoke.

The room almost jumped because he had been silent too long.

"No."

Interesting.

Very interesting.

Jack turned.

"No what?"

Sutter looked at Vanessa.

Not the request. Not the board.

Vanessa.

"The classification doesn't want Vanessa."

Nobody liked that answer.

Because everyone knew what came next.

"What does it want?"

Sutter took a long breath.

"The same thing it wanted 812 days ago. It wants someone to agree."

The room went completely still.

Agreement.

Consensus.

Resolution.

The thing every system eventually wanted.

The thing Celia had spent an entire page arguing against.

Preserve disagreement.

The witness request was not asking Vanessa to testify.

It was asking Vanessa to resolve.

To choose.

To conclude.

To become certainty.

Exactly the thing the Anchor existed to prevent.

Christina generated a new evidence card.

```text
CLASSIFICATION OBJECTIVE

Working Theory

Not: Find witness
Possibly: Resolve disagreement

Risk: Conversion of witness into authority
```

Nobody argued.

Because every road seemed to point there.

Room 414.

Black Lantern.

The Continuity Model.

Different systems.

Same temptation.

Turn disagreement into conclusion.

Turn witness into authority.

Turn uncertainty into continuity.

Vanessa stared at the request. Then at Celia's page. Then at the empty chair.

Finally:

"I'm not accepting it."

Nobody spoke.

Then Maggie nodded.

Caroline nodded.

Jack nodded.

Even Sutter nodded.

For the first time in the review, everyone agreed.

Which frightened Jack more than anything else that day.

Because somewhere inside Room 414, something had probably expected exactly that.
