# Chapter 012: Archive Mirror

The archive mirror looked like a graveyard designed by a bored engineer.

No marble.

No names carved cleanly into stone.

Just broken pages, dead links, half-loaded tables, cached thumbnails, and timestamps that had survived because nobody important had noticed them soon enough.

Jack had always distrusted beautiful systems. Beautiful systems wanted admiration before inspection. The archive mirror had no beauty at all. It was ugly, partial, and stubborn.

That made him trust it more than he wanted to.

Christina opened the recovered crawl in a sandboxed view. The house had gone quiet around the screen. Maggie sat with a pen between two fingers, not writing yet. Vanessa stood behind her chair instead of beside Jack, which he noticed and chose not to interpret.

The mirror's header loaded first.

**MERCYNET RELIEF OPERATIONS / LEGACY SUPPORT TABLES**

Then the page collapsed into missing image icons and text blocks with their formatting stripped away.

"This is what survived?" Vanessa asked.

"This is what got caught," Jack said. "Survival implies intention. Mirrors are usually less noble than that."

Maggie gave him a sideways glance.

"You are very hard on useful things."

"Useful things keep hurting people."

No one argued.

Christina highlighted the first readable row.

```text
Record fragment: language-support roster
Capture source: third-party archive mirror
Capture timestamp: 19 months prior
Integrity: partial
Personal identifiers: limited
```

The table was mostly ruin.

A column for site ID.

A column for deployment date.

A column for role.

A column for authorization basis.

The names column had been scraped badly enough that most entries looked like old bones after weather.

Then Celia appeared.

Not as a theory.

Not as a ghost.

As text.

**Celia Vale / Spanish-English interpreter / Durham overflow site / temporary community liaison**

Vanessa made a sound too small to be called a gasp.

Jack looked away from her because the sight of grief becoming proof felt indecent.

Maggie did not look away. Maggie reached back and found Vanessa's wrist without asking for permission, then let go before comfort became a claim.

Christina continued.

The next row used no name.

**Language-support vendor / Durham overflow site / authorization retained**

The row after that:

**Third-party interpreter / consent support basis / authorization retained**

Then:

**Community liaison / verbal consent history / authorization retained**

Then:

**Volunteer resource / historical support basis / authorization retained**

Vanessa stepped closer to the table.

"How many times?"

Christina answered without softness.

```text
Celia Vale appears by name: 1
Role-language references likely tied to same workstream: 23
Confidence: medium-high
```

Twenty-three.

A person once.

A function twenty-three times.

Jack felt the old anger rise, clean and useless. He had learned to mistrust clean anger. It made the world simple. Simple worlds were where the first lie got comfortable.

"Do we know those are all her?" Maggie asked.

"No," Jack said before Christina could answer.

Vanessa turned on him.

"You cannot look at that and say no."

"I can look at that and say not yet."

"That is not better."

"It is safer."

"For whom?"

The question cracked the room.

Maggie set her pen down.

"For Celia, if this has to survive more than our outrage."

Vanessa stared at her.

For one second Jack thought Vanessa might walk out.

Instead she sat.

Not defeated.

Contained.

Christina moved to the next recovered fragment.

This one came from a cached PDF summary attached to the same relief operation. Most of the page was empty. The mirror had caught text but not the scanned signature block. The surviving paragraph was enough to change the temperature of the room.

```text
Interpreter C.V. has withdrawn authorization for continued use of translated intake summaries outside direct client service. Pending review, legacy reliance may continue for audit continuity unless revoked by program authority.
```

Maggie whispered the key word.

"Withdrawn."

Vanessa leaned over the table until both palms pressed flat against the wood.

"She refused."

Jack nodded.

"She tried to."

There was the cruelty.

Not in the refusal.

In the unless.

Unless revoked by program authority.

Celia could withdraw as a person, but the program could retain her usefulness as history. Her no had entered the system as a concern to be managed, not a boundary to be honored.

Christina flagged the language.

```text
REVOCATION FRAGMENT DETECTED
Subject initials: C.V.
Action: withdrew authorization
Override language: legacy reliance may continue
Responsible authority: program authority, unnamed in fragment
```

"Find the authority," Vanessa said.

Jack heard the command under the grief.

Christina searched the mirror cache. The first three attempts returned empty fragments. The fourth produced a routing table. Jack grimaced at the term and made a note to rename it later. Some words came pre-contaminated.

The table identified a review action.

No full minutes.

No clean order.

Just a line item.

```text
Review action: authorization-retention exception
Location: Room 414
Reviewer: M. Sutter
Outcome: retained for audit continuity
```

Maggie wrote Room 414 on the pad.

Vanessa looked at the name.

"M. Sutter."

Jack watched her face carefully.

"You know it?"

"I know of it," she said.

"That is not the same answer."

"No."

She did not explain.

That bothered him more than if she had lied badly.

Christina opened a side panel.

```text
M. Sutter appears in:
- MercyNet legacy review fragment
- NorthStar advisory roster, year unknown
- Voss index marginal note: SUTTER / ROOM 414 / DO NOT ACCEPT MINUTES WITHOUT ROOM PROOF
```

The room went still.

Voss had known the name.

Or feared it.

Or both.

Maggie drew a line between Room 414 and Sutter, then stopped herself before connecting either to NorthStar.

Jack noticed.

"Narrative temptation," he said.

"I hate that phrase now," Maggie said.

"Good. It means it is working."

Vanessa did not smile.

She was staring at Celia's one surviving full-name row.

"She was there," Vanessa said.

No one corrected her.

Because this time the careful answer and the human answer were the same.

Celia Vale had been there.

Once by name.

Twenty-three times by use.

Jack saved the mirror capture, the revocation fragment, the Room 414 line item, and the Sutter match as separate evidence cards. He did not let Echo merge them. Not yet.

Merged things began acting like conclusions.

Separate things remained honest.

Christina displayed the final recovered line from the cached PDF.

It had been torn from its context by the crawl. No header. No footer. Just sentence debris.

```text
Continued reliance approved pending formal review in 414.
```

Maggie looked at the wall display.

"The room comes before the decision."

Jack nodded.

"Or the room was invented to justify one already made."

Vanessa lifted her eyes from the table.

"Then we find the room."

Jack thought of the car outside the house, the reporter's article, Lena's contract, Marcus's sheriff photo, Priya's apology, and the missing spreadsheet someone had carried away in fourteen minutes.

Then he thought of Celia's name, alone in the mirror, refusing to vanish completely.

"Yes," he said.

"We find the room."
