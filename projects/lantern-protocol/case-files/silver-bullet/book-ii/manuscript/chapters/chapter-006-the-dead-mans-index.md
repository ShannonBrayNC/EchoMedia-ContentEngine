# Chapter 006: The Dead Man's Index

Voss organized fear like a librarian with enemies.

The storage unit inventory was not large. Three bankers boxes. One water-damaged laptop. A pocket recorder with no battery. A coil of red thread still pinned through paper tags. Vanessa had brought only the index, twelve pages copied from a notebook whose original she claimed was locked somewhere safer than Jack's house.

Maggie did not like that answer.

"Safer from whom?"

"From whoever taught three different agencies to forget my sister in the same voice," Vanessa said.

Her sister's name was Celia Vale. Twenty-nine. Translator. Contracted through a humanitarian legal clinic after a flood displaced thousands across two states. Celia had not died in the official record. She had not been listed missing either. Her name had been replaced by function.

**Language-support vendor.**

**Third-party interpreter.**

**Unauthorized advocate.**

Jack understood the violence of that. A name could be killed without a body.

Christina scanned the index from the wall camera. Echo refused to ingest the pages until Vanessa signed a narrow permission statement. Vanessa read every word, asked two sharp questions, then signed with the calm irritation of someone pleased to find resistance.

```text
VOSS INDEX / LIMITED INGEST
Primary headings detected:
- Null Pattern
- Living Anchor failure modes
- Lantern predecessor notes
- Authority transfer without accountable witness
- Names converted to roles
```

"Living Anchor," Maggie said.

Jack heard the capital letters.

Voss had written the phrase in the oldest Lantern notes. Technology could preserve records, but a living witness had to hold meaning against misuse. Jack had treated the idea as philosophy. Now it sat in the room like an unpaid debt.

Vanessa watched him read.

"You know that phrase."

"I know enough to be bothered."

"Good. Bothered people still have skin in the truth."

Maggie gave Jack a look that said do not enjoy that sentence too much.

The first index match surfaced at 4:44 p.m. Celia's name had appeared in a clinic scheduling export, then vanished from a ministry report and returned only as role language. The same report cited her translation work to prove community consent. She had been erased as a person and preserved as authority.

That was The Null Pattern's first visible tooth.

Not absence.

Use without presence.

Jack felt the room tilt around the doctrine.

In Book One, the Silver Bullet had turned an accusation into a weapon. Book Two was showing him a quieter weapon: a missing person still being spent by offices that no longer had to answer to her.

Vanessa leaned over the table. "Now tell me whether Lantern can find her."

The old Jack would have said yes because a woman with rain in her hair and grief under her polish had asked him to become useful. The newer Jack looked at Maggie first.

Maggie nodded once, not permission, reminder.

"Lantern can preserve the trail," Jack said. "It can show where a name changes shape. It cannot promise a person at the end."

Vanessa's face changed. Not disappointment. Appraisal.

"That may be the first honest answer anyone has given me."

Christina interrupted before Jack could make that mean more than it meant.

```text
ALERT: External scan detected against public Lantern disclosure node
Source: masked relay
Query target: Voss Index / Celia Vale / Precedent
```

No one had published those three terms together.

Vanessa looked toward the darkening window.

"Then we are late," she said.

Outside, a car rolled slowly past the house with its headlights off.
