# Chapter 016: Maggie's Line

Maggie drew the line in blue ink because black felt too official.

The line started under Jack's name in the hostile transcript fragment and ran straight across the legal pad until it reached the edge of the page. Then she wrote one sentence above it.

**Do not cross this for anyone.**

Jack stared at the note from the other side of the kitchen table.

"That feels personal."

"It is."

Vanessa stood near the sink with both hands around a glass of water she had not touched. The house had not recovered from Room 414. It still felt as if an invisible guest had entered, set down a placard, and left the door open behind itself.

On the wall display, Christina held the hostile fragment in quarantine.

```text
M. SUTTER: Hearing reconvened.
JACK MERCER: Present.
```

Two lines.

That was all it had taken to change the air in the room.

Jack had been accused before. Named before. Framed before. But this was different. This was not someone saying he had done something.

This was a record trying to create his attendance.

A room that wanted him.

Maggie capped the pen.

"We need rules for you now. Not Lantern. Not witnesses. You."

Jack did not like the order of that sentence.

"I'm not the problem."

"No. You're the bait."

That stopped him.

Vanessa looked away, but not quickly enough.

She agreed.

Maggie continued before he could turn agreement into betrayal.

"Room 414 does not need you guilty. It needs you present. It needs a body it can point to. The minute you step into that building, every bad record gets a better costume."

Jack leaned back.

His shoulder ached, sharp and familiar, as if the old injury had an opinion.

"So what, I stay home forever?"

"No. You stop treating proximity like courage."

Silence followed.

Not comfortable silence.

Useful silence.

Vanessa set the untouched water down.

"She's right."

Jack looked at her.

"You keep saying that today."

"Do not get used to it."

Maggie lifted the legal pad.

"Line one: Jack does not enter MercyNet property. Line two: Jack does not contact Sutter. Line three: Jack does not answer any message that places him inside a proceeding he did not attend."

"Line four," Vanessa said, "Jack does not try to save Celia by becoming the next document."

Maggie wrote it down.

Jack hated that she wrote it down.

He hated more that it helped.

Christina displayed a soft amber banner.

```text
RISK MODEL UPDATE
Subject: Jack Mercer
Risk vector: involuntary incorporation into hostile record chain
Recommended control: distance-preserving investigation protocol
```

"Even the machine thinks I'm the weak link."

Maggie looked at him.

"No. The machine thinks you are valuable enough to target. Try not to make that romantic."

That one found its mark.

Vanessa almost smiled, then did not.

The argument could have ended there if Jack had been a more reasonable man or Maggie had been less tired. Instead it moved sideways into the thing none of them had named directly.

Vanessa.

Maggie turned to her.

"Your turn."

Vanessa's posture changed.

"Excuse me?"

"You do not get to stand in my kitchen with Voss artifacts, missing family, and a private grief engine running full speed while pretending only Jack needs rules."

Jack said, "Maggie."

"No."

One syllable.

Steel, not volume.

Maggie kept her eyes on Vanessa.

"You are not wrong to want speed. But speed is not innocence. Grief is not a warrant. And chemistry is not evidence."

The kitchen went extremely still.

Jack looked at the table.

Vanessa did not.

"Chemistry," she said.

"Yes," Maggie said. "Do you want me to use a softer word and make everyone lie for five more chapters?"

For one dangerous second, Vanessa looked amused.

Then she looked hurt.

Then she looked honest.

"I did not come here for that."

"I believe you. That does not mean it is not here."

Jack wished the room had another hostile transcript to examine.

Any transcript.

A grocery receipt.

A weather alert.

Anything.

Maggie pointed the pen at him without looking.

"And you. Do not turn being needed into affection."

The sentence hit harder than he wanted it to.

Because it was not accusation.

It was memory.

His own.

How many disasters had begun with someone needing him to become useful before they allowed him to become loved? How many times had he mistaken urgency for intimacy because urgency at least gave him a job?

Vanessa's voice softened.

"That was cruel."

Maggie nodded.

"Yes."

No apology.

That made it cleaner.

Christina interrupted before the silence became permanent.

```text
UNCONFIRMED LINK WITHHELD
Potential relation: Room 414 / Black Lantern / Template 4B-R
Reason: insufficient evidence; high narrative temptation
Action: hidden from primary map pending corroboration
```

Jack stared at the banner.

"You hid a link?"

```text
Correction: link suppressed from active visualization.
Data retained.
Conclusion blocked.
```

Vanessa crossed her arms.

"What is the link?"

Maggie answered before Jack could ask Christina.

"No."

Vanessa blinked.

"You do not know what it is."

"I know what it will do."

Jack looked from Maggie to the screen.

He wanted the link.

Of course he wanted it.

A hidden connection between Room 414, Black Lantern, and Template 4B-R was exactly the kind of thing that made the world feel briefly conquerable. A line on a map. A shape. A promise that the chaos had architecture.

Maggie saw the want in his face.

"That," she said, "is why we wait."

Vanessa looked angry enough to speak and wise enough not to.

The discipline held.

Barely.

Then Maggie made her own discovery.

Not through Echo.

Not through Christina.

Through the printed articles.

She had kept every version of the reporter's stories in a binder because paper did not flatter the person reading it. She opened the binder to the second article, then the third, then a correction note appended sometime after midnight.

"This is wrong," she said.

Jack straightened.

"What?"

"The correction timestamp."

She turned the binder around.

The reporter had updated a sentence about Lantern's process safeguards at 12:14 a.m.

Jack's correction draft had not been created until 12:37 a.m.

Twenty-three minutes later.

Vanessa leaned over the page.

"Maybe he corrected it independently."

"Maybe," Maggie said. "Except he used Jack's phrase."

She pointed.

The article correction read:

**Mercer's system reportedly distinguishes preservation from publication, though critics question whether vulnerable participants understand the difference.**

Jack felt his mouth go dry.

Preservation from publication.

That was his phrase.

Not public.

Not sent.

Not spoken on a call.

Typed once in a draft response he had abandoned.

Christina pulled the local draft metadata.

```text
LOCAL DRAFT
Created: 00:37
Phrase: preservation from publication
Status: unsent
External publication update: 00:14
Temporal conflict: external use predates local draft creation
```

"That's impossible," Vanessa said.

Maggie shook her head.

"No. That's the one word we do not get to use anymore."

Jack looked at the paper copy, then the wall display.

The line on Maggie's legal pad sat between them like a border at the edge of a country he had not known he lived in.

Do not cross this for anyone.

Room 414 had tried to place him in a hearing.

Now the article had used words before he wrote them.

Two events.

Same lesson.

The record was no longer waiting for reality.

It was reaching backward.

Maggie closed the binder.

"Now we know what Chapter Seventeen is about."

Jack almost smiled.

"You naming chapters now?"

"Somebody has to keep you out of rooms that want attendance."

Vanessa picked up the Voss photograph from the counter and turned it face down.

"Correction draft," she said.

Christina opened a new case card.

```text
NEW EVENT
Name: Correction Draft
Type: temporal narrative conflict
Primary risk: premature conclusion
Secondary risk: local compromise
Required posture: preserve first; accuse no one
```

Maggie took the blue pen and drew a second line beneath the first.

This one was shorter.

Under it she wrote:

**No one chases the future until we prove the clock.**
