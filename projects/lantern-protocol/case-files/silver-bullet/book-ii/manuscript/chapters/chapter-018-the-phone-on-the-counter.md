# Chapter 018: The Phone on the Counter

The phone stayed on the counter for eleven minutes.

Nobody touched it.

The notification remained on the screen.

**BLACK LANTERN RECOVERY: ACCOUNT VERIFICATION REQUIRED**

The words seemed brighter than the rest of the room.

Not because they glowed.

Because everyone kept looking at them.

Vanessa stood closest.

Jack stood farthest away.

Maggie noticed both facts immediately.

"Good," she said.

Neither of them knew which part she meant.

Christina displayed the consent boundaries.

```text
DEVICE REVIEW PARAMETERS

Approved:
- Account metadata
- Recovery workflows
- Unknown login events
- Recent notifications

Excluded:
- Personal photos
- Private messages
- Unrelated contacts
- Non-Celia content
```

Vanessa nodded.

"Those rules stay."

"They stay," Maggie said.

Only then did Vanessa pick up the phone.

The screen unlocked.

No hidden passcode.

No dramatic hesitation.

That somehow made it worse.

Most people prepared themselves for resistance.

Cooperation was harder.

Christina began with the notification source.

The result appeared almost immediately.

```text
APPLICATION

BLACK LANTERN RECOVERY

Status: inactive

Current installation: none

Local app record: none

Notification origin: archived recovery service
```

Jack frowned.

"How does an app send a notification if it isn't installed?"

```text
Recovery systems do not require active installation.
```

Vanessa looked down.

"I've never used Black Lantern."

Maggie did not challenge her.

Not because she believed her.

Because belief was not evidence.

Christina expanded the recovery chain.

A timeline appeared.

The first entry drew everyone's attention.

```text
RECOVERY TOKEN GENERATED

Date: 14 months prior

Recipient: verified account holder

Status: unopened
```

Vanessa stared.

"Fourteen months?"

Christina confirmed.

Fourteen months.

Before Room 414.

Before the articles.

Before Jack.

Before the first known contact from Voss.

The room became very quiet.

"That's impossible," Vanessa said.

Maggie sighed.

"There goes that word again."

Vanessa ignored her.

"Fourteen months ago I didn't even know what Black Lantern was."

Jack believed that.

The problem was that belief was not evidence either.

Christina continued.

A second recovery token.

Then a third.

Then a fourth.

All unopened.

All ignored.

All directed toward an account Vanessa claimed she had never created.

The account name finally appeared.

```text
ACCOUNT

V.VALE.ARCHIVE
```

Nobody moved.

Not because it was shocking.

Because it was almost expected.

The account looked too neat.

Too obvious.

Real secrets rarely named themselves.

Maggie pointed at the metadata.

"Keep going."

Christina opened the recovery references.

The screen paused.

Then loaded a linked archive directory.

Not the contents.

Only the references.

```text
ASSOCIATED ARCHIVES

CELIA_VALE_ARCHIVE
BLACKLANTERN-414
RESTRICTED
```

Vanessa sat down.

Slowly.

Jack watched her carefully.

Not guilt.

Not recognition.

Loss.

The kind that arrives when a missing person stops feeling missing and starts feeling organized.

Someone had built a place for Celia.

A place Vanessa had never been told existed.

Maggie studied the timestamps.

Then something caught her eye.

"Wait."

Everyone turned.

She pointed at the oldest recovery request.

"Look at the issuing account."

Christina expanded the field.

```text
RECOVERY REQUESTED BY

M. SUTTER
```

Nobody spoke.

The phone remained on the counter.

The screen dimmed.

Then brightened again.

A new notification appeared.

Not a recovery request.

Not a verification code.

A message.

One line.

```text
ARCHIVE ACCESS READY

Witness required.
```

And for the first time, Vanessa looked genuinely afraid.
