# Sprint 201-SB: Book 2 Act II Build Order and Draft Repair

## Status
Complete.

## Scope
Silver Bullet Book 2: The Null Pattern

Act II begins after Chapter 10, where the authorization ghost confirms that Celia Vale survives in legacy authority structures while absent from living identity tables.

## Sprint objective
Define the Act II build order before drafting Chapters 11-24 so the manuscript continues from Act I without becoming repetitive, over-expository, or doctrine-heavy.

## Act I handoff state

### Active pressures
- The Mercer Precedent is now public narrative pressure.
- NorthStar Review Services is linked to Template 4B and anti-analysis language.
- Celia Vale has emerged as the first concrete `use without presence` case.
- Vanessa Vale is inside the house, emotionally involved, and not fully trusted.
- Lena, Marcus, and Priya are living witnesses under pressure.
- Maggie is functioning as the restraint engine.
- Voss has entered through photograph, index, and tape.
- The Null Pattern has been identified as a doctrine event, not full proof.

### Act I rule going forward
Act II must not repeat Act I's discovery loop. Every chapter must either expand the threat, complicate a relationship, or reveal a new mechanism.

## Act II structure

Act II should run Chapters 11-24 and divide into two pressure tracks:

1. Public/legal pressure: The Mercer Precedent becomes a broader media, legislative, professional, and institutional threat.
2. Null Pattern pressure: Celia Vale's authorization ghost reveals how people can be removed as persons while preserved as usable authority.

## Chapter build order

### Chapter 011: The Fourteen-Minute Window

Purpose:
- Recover everything possible from the public bug tracker leak.
- Establish that fourteen minutes was long enough for multiple unknown parties to capture the ticket.

Required scenes:
- Jack reconstructs the bug tracker exposure.
- Maggie insists on contamination labeling.
- Vanessa pushes for immediate action.
- Christina identifies at least three access shadows without naming them as suspects.

New evidence:
- The ticket was viewed from a legislative-network IP, a contractor VPN, and an archive mirror.

Hook:
- One viewer downloaded an attachment the current ticket no longer shows.

Vocabulary guardrail:
Avoid repeating `authorization` more than twice outside the ticket excerpt.

### Chapter 012: Archive Mirror

Purpose:
- Introduce the archive mirror as a place where deleted public records briefly survive.

Required scenes:
- Echo pulls a cached fragment.
- The missing attachment is revealed as a table of relief-site language support contacts.
- Celia appears once by name, then repeatedly as role language.

New evidence:
- Celia's revocation notice existed and was acknowledged.

Hook:
- The acknowledgement was signed by someone who later denied knowing her.

### Chapter 013: Vanessa's Version

Purpose:
- Make Vanessa more than a mystery object.
- Give her direct memory of Celia without turning the chapter into exposition.

Required scenes:
- Vanessa tells Maggie, not Jack, about Celia.
- Jack overhears only part of it and chooses not to intrude.
- Maggie becomes the bridge between Vanessa's grief and Jack's caution.

New evidence:
- Celia had feared becoming `useful after refusal` before she vanished from records.

Hook:
- Vanessa admits she has one more Voss item she has not disclosed.

### Chapter 014: The Consent Afterlife

Purpose:
- Convert the phrase from doctrine into scene evidence.

Required scenes:
- Lena receives a revised agreement.
- Marcus receives a custody-related warning.
- Priya's donor case gets cited by a partner database after withdrawal.

New evidence:
- The same legal phrase appears across all three cases, but with local edits.

Hook:
- One edit mentions `Template 4B parent language`.

Vocabulary guardrail:
Use `afterlife` once. Do not overuse it.

### Chapter 015: The Hearing That Wasn't There

Purpose:
- First institutional horror set piece of Act II.

Required scenes:
- A record shows an emergency review hearing connected to Celia.
- No participant remembers attending.
- The calendar invite exists, but room access logs show the room was empty.

New evidence:
- The hearing outcome authorized continued use of Celia's work.

Hook:
- The transcript contains a sentence from Voss's tape.

### Chapter 016: Maggie's Line

Purpose:
- Give Maggie her own agency and stakes.

Required scenes:
- Maggie challenges Jack's attraction to Vanessa and his rescue reflex.
- Maggie warns Vanessa that grief can become a weapon if aimed carelessly.
- Christina withholds a suggested link because the evidence is too thin.

New evidence:
- Maggie finds an inconsistency in the reporter's timeline.

Hook:
- The reporter received a draft correction before Jack sent it.

### Chapter 017: Correction Draft

Purpose:
- Show public narrative being updated before private action happens.

Required scenes:
- Jack drafts a correction request.
- The article updates in response to points he has not sent.
- Echo detects a phrase overlap between Jack's unsent draft and the published revision.

New evidence:
- Local machine compromise is possible but unproven.

Hook:
- Vanessa's phone contains the same unsent phrase.

### Chapter 018: The Phone on the Counter

Purpose:
- Test Vanessa's trust without making her a simple traitor.

Required scenes:
- Vanessa allows device isolation.
- Jack refuses to look at private content unrelated to Celia.
- Maggie supervises.
- Christina finds a synced note from an account Vanessa says she never created.

New evidence:

Hook:
- Voss's storage unit payment history includes the same account recovery email.

### Chapter 019: Black Lantern Reference

Purpose:
- Seed the larger mythology without stealing Book 2's focus.

Required scenes:
- Voss material identifies Black Lantern as a failed predecessor or containment attempt.
- Jack resists chasing lore instead of Celia.
- Vanessa sees Black Lantern as proof that Voss knew more than he said.

New evidence:

Hook:
- A file list includes Celia's initials dated before her clinic work.

### Chapter 020: NorthStar Denial

Purpose:
- Make NorthStar respond plausibly.

Required scenes:
- NorthStar issues a public denial.
- The denial is careful, corporate, and not cartoonish.

New evidence:

Hook:

### Chapter 021: The Ethics Review Trap

Purpose:
- Put Jack under pressure to legitimize the frame against himself.

Required scenes:
- Jack, Maggie, and Vanessa debate whether to attend.
- Lena warns that declining will be used against him.
- Marcus says attending will validate the trap.

New evidence:
- The review panel includes one person from Voss's old network.

Hook:
- The invitation includes a room number from the hearing that wasn't there.

### Chapter 022: Room 414

Purpose:
- Second institutional horror set piece.

Required scenes:
- Jack visits or remotely investigates the room.
- The room exists physically but not in current facility maps.
- Old signage remains under newer paint.

New evidence:
- A wall mark matches the crossed-out lantern symbol from Vanessa's photograph.

Hook:
- Someone has recently placed a fresh chair in the room.

### Chapter 023: The Witness Table

Purpose:
- Bring Act II's living witnesses together without exposing them publicly.

Required scenes:
- Lena, Marcus, Priya, Vanessa, Maggie, and Jack meet through a constrained, consent-aware process.
- Each person controls what may be shared.
- The witnesses identify a shared moment: they were each asked to sign away analysis.

New evidence:
- Different institutions used the same practical isolation maneuver.

Hook:
- Celia's name appears in one witness packet as a precedent citation.

### Chapter 024: Celia Was the Test

Purpose:
- Act II midpoint reveal.

Required scenes:
- Echo aligns Celia, Template 4B, the empty hearing, Black Lantern, and the witness packets.
- Christina does not overclaim but identifies a sequence.
- Vanessa realizes Celia's disappearance became a reusable model.

New evidence:
- Celia was not only erased; her case became the precedent for later isolation templates.

Act II climax:
- The Null Pattern is no longer an abstract doctrine. It has a repeatable mechanism.

Hook into Act III:
- Voss left a warning: `If Celia becomes precedent, burn the map before the map learns you.`

## Act II anti-repetition rules

### Do not repeat Act I beats
- No more mysterious person arriving at the door.
- No more car surveillance as the primary threat.
- No more generic article follow-up unless it changes the legal/public frame.
- No more doctrine cards unless they add a new operational limit.

### Required variety
- One chapter driven by a leaked technical artifact.
- One chapter driven by Vanessa memory.
- One chapter driven by Maggie's agency.
- One chapter driven by public narrative pressure.
- Two institutional horror set pieces.
- One witness-table chapter.
- One Black Lantern mythology seed.

### Maggie

### Vanessa

Must remain precise, limited, and non-sentient.

Must move from missing name to central mechanism.

### Voss
Must deepen the mystery without hijacking the plot.

## Audiobook guidance

Every chapter should have:
- A concrete object.
- A human cost.
- A restraint moment.
- A final hook.

Avoid long doctrine paragraphs. Convert abstract ideas into:
- tickets
- rooms
- contracts
- transcript fragments
- tables
- calls
- damaged objects
- witness-controlled disclosures
