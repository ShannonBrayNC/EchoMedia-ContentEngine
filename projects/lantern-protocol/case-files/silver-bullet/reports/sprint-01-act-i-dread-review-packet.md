## Chapter-Level Summary

### Chapter 1: The Wrong Initial

Jack discovers a forged initial on his county access renewal. The submission history points to `C-RISK-04`, a restricted family-safety risk review terminal. A hidden attachment appears:

```text
PRE-INCIDENT DOMESTIC RISK REFERRAL - HOLD FOR REVIEW
```

The chapter ends by establishing that the file has not accused him yet. It has prepared the room to believe him dangerous when he denies it.

### Chapter 2: The Panic Call

At Kelly's school assembly, NorthStar Residential Monitoring calls Jack about a panic activation at the house. Jack is not there. The operator says officers have already been dispatched and that the caller provided Jack's current location.

The key dread line is:

> Sir, officers have already been dispatched.

### Chapter 3: First Arrest

Police remove Jack from the school setting. He learns a DVPO petition is in motion and that the report frames his records/documentation behavior as escalation.

The core institutional curse is established:

> I am not here to decide what happened.

Kelly sees enough to understand that her father is being taken away.

### Chapter 4: Rights Removed

The magistrate sequence shows the first domino collapse:

- no contact
- no house access
- no vehicle access
- firearm surrender
- daughter contact disruption
- county access flagged
- evidence access blocked
- phone dying with no charger
- wallet/keys trapped at the house

The chapter also establishes that Jack's military training has been converted into a risk factor on paper.

### Chapter 5: The House Becomes Evidence

Jack returns under civil standby. The house has been staged:

- photos turned facedown
- his mug beside the DVPO packet
- medication arranged too visibly
- packed clothes as performative helpfulness
- Kelly's drawing and safety worksheet arranged as emotional evidence
- red-circled `J.M.` folder seen with Laurel/Claire Voss

Key line:

> The house did not become empty. It became evidence.
