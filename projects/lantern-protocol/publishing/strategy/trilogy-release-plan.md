# Lantern Protocol Trilogy Release Plan

## Goal

Use Book 1 as the lowest-friction entry point into the Lantern Protocol universe, then convert engaged readers and listeners into premium Books 2 and 3.

## Release posture

Book 1 is the trust-builder. It should be easy to access, easy to listen to, and strong enough to make the reader want the rest of the system.

Books 2 and 3 are the paid continuation. They should carry the premium value: deeper stakes, larger world consequences, and a more complete collision between governance, consent, AI, and power.

## Recommended sequence

1. Prepare Book 1 ElevenReader manuscript.
2. Listen to Book 1 end-to-end.
3. Capture and apply listening edits.
4. Build Book 1 KDP retail package.
5. Publish Book 1 as free/low-cost discovery.
6. Promote Book 1 through EchoMedia.ai and Lantern Protocol channels.
7. Prepare Book 2 premium release.
8. Prepare Book 3 premium release.
9. Bundle trilogy after Book 3 has had a standalone sales window.
10. Use performance, reviews, and reader response to position the second series for professional publishing.

## Book 1 launch pattern

- Launch at free where possible, or $0.99 if platform constraints require it.
- Use free listening access as a review tool and audience hook.
- Include a clear call to action at the end: continue to Book 2, join list, visit site, or follow Lantern Protocol updates.
- Capture launch feedback before making Book 2 public.

## Book 2 launch pattern

- Premium pricing.
- Market as the expansion book: the consequences begin escaping the hearing room.
- Include Book 1 recap material only where needed, not as a drag chute.

## Book 3 launch pattern

- Premium pricing.
- Market as the reckoning book: the system, the architects, and the public confront the cost of useful power.
- Prepare trilogy bundle after initial sales period.

## Marketing assets needed

- Book 1 cover.
- Book 1 short description.
- Book 1 long description.
- Author bio.
- Trailer/faceless video script.
- Quote cards.
- Book 2 teaser.
- Newsletter/site CTA.
- Review request message.
- Press/publisher-facing one-sheet for future series.

## Success signals

- Book 1 upload accepted by ElevenReader.
- Book 1 listening pass completed.
- KDP manuscript uploaded successfully.
- Book 1 generates reviews or direct reader feedback.
- Readers click through to Book 2 or the Lantern Protocol site.
- The trilogy becomes credible proof of market interest before pitching the future series.
