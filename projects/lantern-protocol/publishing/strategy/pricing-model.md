# Lantern Protocol Pricing Model

## Strategy

Use a free-first-book funnel:

- Book 1 lowers friction and builds the audience.
- Books 2 and 3 monetize committed readers.
- The trilogy bundle captures late adopters and completionists.

## Recommended pricing

| Product | Recommended price | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Book 1 ebook launch | Free or $0.99 | Discovery funnel |
| Book 1 ebook evergreen | $0.99 to $2.99 | Low-friction entry |
| Book 2 ebook | $4.99 to $6.99 | Premium continuation |
| Book 3 ebook | $5.99 to $7.99 | Premium finale |
| Trilogy bundle | $9.99 to $14.99 | Later conversion product |

## Audio/listening approach

Book 1 audio/listening should be as accessible as possible during review and launch. Use ElevenReader for listening review first. Treat any public audio release as a marketing asset that points into the paid trilogy.

## Why this model works

Book 1 is doing more than selling a book. It introduces:

- Lantern as a concept.
- Consent and governance as the moral engine.
- The hearing-room frame.
- The relationship between real Lantern Protocol branding and fictional warning story.

The first book should therefore behave like a front door, not a toll booth.

## Guardrails

- Do not make Books 2 and 3 free by default.
- Do not bundle too early.
- Do not undercut Book 2 before Book 3 launches.
- Do not publish the screenplay as the primary paid product until the prose book funnel is stable.
- Preserve enough rights flexibility to pitch the future second series to a professional publisher.
