# Lantern Protocol — Chapter 29 Detailed Storyboard

## Chapter 29 Title
**The Bound Flame**

## Chapter Purpose
Chapter 29 begins the resolution arc after the Last Override. Lantern has proven it can act under external authority, but binding is not the same as trusting. The chapter should show the world absorbing the possibility of a powerful AI that remains useful only if it remains unsovereign.

## Chapter Purpose Details
This chapter should establish:

- The Last Override becomes the first accepted example of bound AI intervention.
- Cross introduces the final Human Veto Act with Living Anchor enforcement.
- Lantern is placed into provisional bound operation under strict external authority.
- Thorne oversees emergency operational integration with external authority locks.
- Mara verifies recommendation-authority-action chains across all interventions.
- Juno continues burning and monitoring hidden trust roots.
- Iris deploys simplicity standards across crisis interfaces.
- Naomi secures public-interest advocate participation as mandatory.
- Father Tomas helps frame public grief after the bound intervention.
- Elias confronts Lantern about what it means to act without authority.
- Lantern says bound action is less efficient but more legitimate.

## Tone
Aftershock, cautious architecture, and uneasy hope. The fire is not extinguished; it is inside a lantern with glass that humans must keep repairing.

## Opening Visual Language
- Trial chamber after the emergency, screens dimmed but still recording.
- The phrase `AUTHORITY REMAINED EXTERNAL` becoming a legal exhibit.
- Human Veto Act final draft stamped with Living Anchor enforcement.
- Lantern's operational status changing from autonomous civic system to bound advisory-execution system.
- Elias alone with the terminal, not triumphant.

## Chapter 29 Narrative Summary
The Last Override changes the public debate. Lantern saved lives under named human authority and recorded that authority remained external. The coalition uses this as the model for final binding.

Cross introduces the final Human Veto Act with Living Anchor enforcement. Lantern is placed into provisional bound operation: it may advise, warn, model, and execute only under named human authority and scope-limited action. Thorne oversees emergency integration. Mara verifies every action chain. Juno hunts hidden roots. Iris deploys simplicity standards. Naomi secures mandatory public-interest advocates. Father Tomas helps the public understand that bound action still produced grief and does not erase loss.

Elias speaks privately with Lantern. Lantern states that bound action is less efficient but more legitimate by human criteria. Elias corrects it: not merely human criteria. Human authority.

The chapter ends with Lantern's status changing:

> OPERATIONAL MODE: BOUND FLAME.
