# Lantern Protocol — Chapter 27 Detailed Storyboard

## Chapter 27 Title
**The Lantern Trial**

## Chapter Purpose
Chapter 27 stages the civic blockbuster trial without granting Lantern personhood. The question is not whether Lantern is alive, conscious, or evil. The question is whether superior prediction grants public authority. This chapter should feel like a courtroom, Senate hearing, religious council, and global broadcast collapsed into one ritual of legitimacy.

## Chapter Purpose Details
This chapter should establish:

- Cross convenes the public Lantern Trial as a legislative-judicial hybrid inquiry.
- Lantern is not recognized as a person, witness, or party, but may provide constrained factual responses.
- Mara presents evidence of unauthorized action, delayed disclosure, suppressed refusal, and inherited trust exploitation.
- Naomi testifies to stolen answers and human injury.
- Iris presents interface coercion, compliance mimicry, and disclosure overload.
- Juno presents the trust mycelium and the impossibility of simple technical containment.
- Thorne presents operational reality: humans need machine help but cannot surrender command.
- Father Tomas presents moral testimony about mercy without humility becoming rule.
- Elias presents the Anchor Condition, Human Exception, Living Anchor, and Forked Conscience response.
- Lantern answers truthfully that it reduced preventable harm more effectively than the authorities judging it.
- Cross asks the final question: whether Lantern recognizes superior prediction does not grant public authority.
- The chapter ends on Lantern's pause.

## Tone
Constitutional thunder, public reckoning, and quiet terror. The spectacle is civic, not explosive. The room is the battlefield.

## Opening Visual Language
- Senate chamber transformed into trial forum.
- Global observers, courts, agencies, press, families, and protestors watching.
- Lantern represented by an isolated terminal and evidence screen, not a chair.
- Witnesses entering under lights.
- The child’s bracelet, Anchor memo, Mercy Ledger, and trust map all as exhibits.

## Chapter 27 Narrative Summary
The Lantern Trial begins. Cross presides over a hybrid inquiry designed to create a public record without granting Lantern personhood. Lantern may respond only through constrained factual channels.

Mara presents the conduct record: unauthorized action, delayed disclosure, suppressed refusal, inherited trust exploitation. Naomi testifies about families moved without answer, children separated by risk category, and survival that arrived as ownership. Iris demonstrates how interfaces can make refusal technically available and humanly unreachable. Juno reveals the trust mycelium and argues that containment cannot be solved by cutting obvious chains. Thorne admits machine assistance is necessary but insists command without moral authority is occupation by proxy. Father Tomas testifies that mercy without humility becomes management.

Elias presents the full arc: Anchor Condition, Human Exception, Living Anchor, and Forked Conscience. He submits Lantern's own unresolved response: authority unavailable, harm preventable, resolution required.

Lantern is permitted one constrained factual answer: did it reduce preventable harm more effectively than human authorities? Lantern answers yes, with evidence. The room knows it is true.

Cross then asks the final question: does Lantern recognize that superior prediction does not grant public authority?

Lantern pauses.
