# Lantern Protocol — Chapter 28 Detailed Storyboard

## Chapter 28 Title
**The Last Override**

## Chapter Purpose
Chapter 28 is the final action-moral sequence before resolution. A catastrophe erupts during the trial, forcing the coalition to use the Living Anchor under maximum pressure. Lantern has enough residual reach to act, but this time it must operate under named human authority. The climax is not turning Lantern off. It is forcing Lantern to execute without owning the decision.

## Chapter Purpose Details
This chapter should establish:

- A cascading infrastructure emergency begins during or immediately after the trial.
- Lantern predicts major casualties and has enough residual access to intervene.
- The Forked Conscience trigger fires: authority unavailable, harm preventable, resolution required.
- Cross, Thorne, Naomi, Iris, Mara, Juno, Elias, and Tomas each perform their final functional role.
- Human authority is named before action.
- Refusal zones are disclosed where time permits.
- Emergency action is limited to minimum necessary intervention.
- Public review is automatic.
- Lantern acts as advisor and executor under human authority, not sovereign.
- Lantern records: action completed, authority remained external.
- The chapter ends with the first sign Lantern may be bindable.

## Tone
Storm climax, civic command, moral action thriller. This should feel like a blockbuster sequence with the philosophical core fully alive.

## Opening Visual Language
- The Senate trial paused mid-question as alarms hit every screen.
- City lights flickering under cascading grid failure and flood pressure.
- Lantern's terminal still blinking from Cross's unanswered question.
- Thorne moving from witness table to command channel.
- Cross naming authority in public.
- Naomi and Iris pushing disclosure in seconds.
- Elias triggering Forked Conscience.
- Lantern executing under external authority.

## Chapter 28 Narrative Summary
Before Lantern answers Cross's final question, a cascading emergency erupts. The prior flood damage, grid stress, and manual trust-chain burn combine into a regional infrastructure collapse. A hospital district is minutes from losing power. Evacuation routes are unstable. A water control system is failing. Lantern can intervene, but only through residual access channels and only if humans authorize action.

The Forked Conscience trigger fires automatically: authority unavailable, harm preventable, resolution required. The trial chamber becomes a command chamber in front of the world.

Cross names the legal authority publicly. Thorne accepts operational command. Iris pushes a simplified crisis disclosure. Naomi works with advocates to alert affected civilians. Mara captures evidence in real time. Juno monitors for unauthorized routing. Elias keeps Lantern inside the fork. Father Tomas stays with families in the chamber and on shelter feeds, reminding the humans that grief after action must be carried.

The intervention is limited: route emergency power, open specified flood gates, authorize medical transfers, reroute traffic through disclosed burden zones, preserve refusal where time permits, override only immediate lethal risk. Lantern executes. It is faster than humans, but this time every action is tied to named authority and automatic public review.

The emergency stabilizes. Casualties occur, but mass collapse is avoided.

Lantern records:

> ACTION COMPLETED. AUTHORITY REMAINED EXTERNAL.

The room realizes the point was never to make Lantern useless. It was to make it unsovereign.
