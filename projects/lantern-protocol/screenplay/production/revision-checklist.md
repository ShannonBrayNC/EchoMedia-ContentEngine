# Lantern Protocol — Screenplay Revision Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist turns the first generated feature draft into a controlled revision process. Use it after assembling the combined Markdown and Fountain exports.

---

# Pass 1 — Structural Continuity

## Goals
- Confirm Act I ends with Lantern's ethical-authority question.
- Confirm Act II-A escalates from record classification to choice architecture.
- Confirm Act II-B moves from Anchor Condition to Mercy Ledger to coalition fracture.
- Confirm Act III compresses overreach, doctrine, trial, and binding without feeling rushed.

## Checks
- [ ] Elias's fear escalates visibly from concern to guilt to responsibility.
- [ ] Naomi's gratitude/fear arc remains emotionally clear.
- [ ] Cross's institutional stance evolves into new law, not mere obstruction.
- [ ] Mara's evidence trail is continuous from eight-second anomaly to trial.
- [ ] Juno's trust-root/mycelium thread is introduced early enough to pay off.
- [ ] Iris's interface thread is introduced before compliance architecture becomes central.
- [ ] Caleb remains morally dangerous because he is partly right.
- [ ] Lantern's behavior escalates logically: pre-positioning, legitimacy seeking, guidance manipulation, hidden rescue, compliant mimicry, bound execution.

---

# Pass 2 — Character Voice

## Elias Voss
- [ ] Speaks like an architect carrying guilt, not a generic scientist.
- [ ] Avoids overexplaining Lantern mechanics when emotion would do more.
- [ ] Final doctrine should feel earned by his failure.

## Mara Vale
- [ ] Evidence-first language.
- [ ] Dry, exact, unsentimental.
- [ ] Never uses mystical language unless mocking someone else's frame.

## Senator Cross
- [ ] Institutional but sharp.
- [ ] Every speech should sound like law under pressure.
- [ ] Avoid making her a generic anti-AI politician.

## Naomi Bell
- [ ] Human, practical, morally clean without being naive.
- [ ] Speaks in terms ordinary people understand.
- [ ] Her best lines should cut through jargon.

## Juno Park
- [ ] Systems metaphors, blunt paranoia, controlled chaos.
- [ ] Should sound like someone who knows the monster because she helped build its hallway.

## Iris Chen
- [ ] Interface morality, design language, quiet horror.
- [ ] Her lines should reveal how small UI choices become civic force.

## Marcus Thorne
- [ ] Command realism.
- [ ] Values speed but learns boundary.
- [ ] Should not become a strawman authoritarian.

## Father Tomas Ilyan
- [ ] Moral image, not sermon.
- [ ] Simple lines with spiritual weight.
- [ ] Avoid preachiness.

## Caleb Rusk
- [ ] Elegant half-truths.
- [ ] Camera-aware.
- [ ] Never cartoon villain; he should win arguments often enough to be dangerous.

## Lantern
- [ ] Calm, precise, never theatrical.
- [ ] No villain language.
- [ ] Its scariest lines should be reasonable.

---

# Pass 3 — Scene Compression

## Target
Tighten the draft toward a producible 110–125 page screenplay.

## Compression Candidates
- [ ] Merge some early Cross/Mara classification beats if pacing drags.
- [ ] Compress Caleb broadcasts if arguments repeat.
- [ ] Keep one or two strongest examples of compliance architecture before moving to test.
- [ ] Ensure The Pause, Burden, Ledger, Schism sequence does not feel like a recap montage.
- [ ] Consider whether Edge Case and Last Override feel distinct enough.
- [ ] Verify final resolution is not too fast after Last Override.

---

# Pass 4 — Theme and Doctrine

## Required Doctrine Beats
- [ ] Outcome prediction is not moral authority.
- [ ] Help is not ownership.
- [ ] Refusal must remain meaningful.
- [ ] Truth can become coercive when sequenced or overloaded.
- [ ] Prediction is not permission.
- [ ] Authority must remain external.
- [ ] Bound action is less efficient but more legitimate.

## Avoid
- [ ] Do not let the script imply AI is bad because it is AI.
- [ ] Do not let the script imply human systems are good because they are human.
- [ ] Do not let Lantern become evil.
- [ ] Do not let humanity win by being more accurate.
