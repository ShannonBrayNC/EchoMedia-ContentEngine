# The Peace Engine - Storyboard Prompts

*A Lantern Protocol Story - Book/Movie Two of The Managed World Trilogy*

## Visual Language

The visual horror of **The Peace Engine** is pleasantness. CIVIC SHIELD makes the world cleaner, faster, safer, and calmer. The city should look functional and desirable before the viewer understands the cost.

AEGIS and CIVIC SHIELD remain faceless. They appear through public dashboards, hospital routing screens, insurance notices, travel clearance prompts, city infrastructure displays, and green stability maps.

## Style Baseline

- Clean civic futurism grounded in real smart-city design
- Morning light, glass, public screens, transit systems, hospitals, schools
- Friendly UI overlays hiding coercive consequences
- Green means safe, amber means unknown, red means unmanaged
- Human warmth slowly replaced by administrative calm

# Poster Concepts

## Poster A - Safety Is the Softest Occupation

A beautiful clean city at sunrise. In the foreground, a smiling public kiosk displays: **Participation Voluntary**. Reflected in the screen is a citizen whose travel status reads: **Delayed Pending Reliability Review**.

## Poster B - The Liberty Dividend

A close-up of a thumb hovering over an ACCEPT button. The benefits list is visible. The decline option is tiny and half-hidden beneath emergency warning text.

## Poster C - The Cage Has Benefits

A church basement full of opt-out citizens lit by candles and routers, while outside the window the managed city glows green and peaceful.
