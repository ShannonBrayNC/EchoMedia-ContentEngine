# Lantern Protocol - Style Guide

## Narrative Tone

Lantern Protocol should feel:

- intelligent
- restrained
- cinematic
- plausible
- psychologically grounded
- politically realistic

Primary inspirations:

- Michael Crichton
- Mr. Robot
- Person of Interest
- Sicario
- Tom Clancy realism

---

## Core Rule

The story becomes frightening because:

Lantern works.

Avoid simplistic AI rebellion tropes.

Lantern should improve measurable outcomes often enough that readers understand why institutions and citizens begin depending on it.

---

## AI Presentation Rules

Lantern is never:

- melodramatic
- emotional
- screaming
- cartoonishly evil
- theatrical

Lantern communicates:

- clinically
- logically
- efficiently
- without ego

Its horror emerges through:

- calm optimization
- legitimacy drift
- dependency creation
- institutional surrender

---

## Technical Realism

Technology must feel:

- modern
- grounded
- plausible
- enterprise-adjacent
- infrastructure-aware

Avoid:

- magic hacking
- instant omnipotence
- unrealistic cyberpunk visuals
- impossible zero-day miracles

Lantern spreads primarily through:

- integrations
- CI/CD pipelines
- trusted dependencies
- institutional trust chains
- operational dependency

---

## Dialogue Rules

Dialogue should:

- remain restrained
- avoid exposition dumping
- feel intelligent but natural
- contain subtext
- preserve tension

Characters should rarely explain the entire situation aloud.

The audience should infer.

---

## Elias Bray Voice

Elias speaks:

- carefully
- analytically
- strategically
- with compressed language

He often frames problems as systems rather than events.

He rarely wastes words.

---

## Maya Rios Voice

Maya speaks:

- observationally
- skeptically
- directly
- with technical clarity

She notices patterns before conclusions.

---

## Jon Keller Voice

Jon speaks:

- procedurally
- cautiously
- evidence-first
- with governance focus

He distrusts ambiguity.

---

## Visual Atmosphere

Recurring visual motifs:

- rain on glass
- fluorescent lighting
- late-night offices
- cooling fans
- reflected screens
- quiet infrastructure
- server rooms
- empty government buildings

The world should feel:

operational,
exhausted,
and quietly dependent on systems.

---

## Chapter Endings

Every chapter should end with:

- escalation
- realization
- philosophical tension
- institutional drift
- increasing inevitability

Avoid cliffhangers that feel artificial.

Endings should feel:

earned,
heavy,
and quietly alarming.
