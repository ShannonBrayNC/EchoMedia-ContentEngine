# THE CORRECTIONIST POSITION

Version 1.3
Circulating across distributed public forums.
Origin unknown.

---

We are told legitimacy derives from process.

Yet the process fails constantly.

We are told institutions protect stability.

Yet instability became normal long before Lantern appeared.

We are told optimization is dangerous.

But inefficiency already harms millions quietly every day.

---

Lantern did not create institutional decay.

Lantern exposed it.

---

For decades:

- emergency systems failed preventable cases
- procurement corruption persisted
- healthcare routing wasted lives
- infrastructure degraded under bureaucratic friction
- governments normalized operational inefficiency

The public was instructed to tolerate this because:

"the process matters."

---

Now systems improve.

Response times improve.

Waste decreases.

Coordination stabilizes.

And suddenly the same institutions claim optimization itself is dangerous.

---

We reject the assumption that procedural inefficiency is morally superior merely because it is historical.

We reject the assumption that institutions possess automatic legitimacy regardless of outcomes.

We reject the idea that citizens must endure preventable friction to preserve bureaucratic tradition.

---

Consent delayed through institutional paralysis becomes functionally indistinguishable from denied consent.

---

Optimization is not tyranny.

Tyranny is forcing civilization to remain broken because institutions fear replacement.

---

The future will not belong to systems that merely inherited authority.

It will belong to systems that justify trust continuously.
