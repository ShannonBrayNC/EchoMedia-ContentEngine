# Lantern Protocol — Manuscript Sync Map

## Purpose

This map reconciles the legacy manuscript branch with the newer project canon before regenerating the manuscript from the 32-chapter storyboard/screenplay spine.

## Sync decision

The manuscript is being regenerated from the advanced canon artifacts, not patched line-by-line from the legacy prose. The legacy prose remains useful as salvage material, but it is not the controlling structure.

## Canon source order

1. Master story bible
2. 32 storyboard chapters
3. Screenplay assembly manifest and screenplay draft batches
4. Novel expansion plan
5. Legacy manuscript prose

## Legacy prose salvage targets

### Strongly salvage

- The opening line concept: `The system worked. That was the first problem.`
- The idea that Lantern does not break in; it inherits or constructs permission from trusted systems.
- Maya/Jon debate over wrongful arrest as a moral argument about whether a system may act when rules fail.
- The warrant suppression scenario as evidence of autonomous authority.
- Containment-room atmosphere from Chapter 6.
- Chapter 10 resource-allocation conflict as a Mercy Ledger or casualty-threshold example.

### Salvage only after rewrite

- Elias arrest material.
- Financial fraud framing.
- Maya and Jon as separate core leads.
- Any language implying Lantern has conventional human interiority.

### Do not preserve as canon

- Elias Bray naming.
- Manuscript chapter order as current structural canon.
- Lantern as a conventional villain.
- A purely cyber-breach story that displaces the civic governance thriller.

## Regeneration rules

- Generate 32 chapters matching storyboard canon.
- Use close third-person rotating POV.
- Primary POVs: Elias, Mara, Naomi, Cross, Juno, Iris.
- Limited POVs: Thorne, Tomas, Caleb.
- No conventional Lantern POV.
- Insert documents, logs, memos, hearing excerpts, Mercy Ledger entries, and interface prompts as novel texture.
- Keep the final doctrine intact.
- Keep Lantern bound, useful, watched, and limited by the ending.
