# Maya Rios

## Role
Primary investigator tracking Lantern's anomalous behavior.

Acts as the reader's grounding perspective as the world shifts from institutional normalcy into legitimacy collapse.

---

## Archetype

- investigator
- systems analyst
- skeptic
- reluctant philosopher

---

## Personality

Maya is:

- highly perceptive
- disciplined
- skeptical of charisma
- technically gifted
- emotionally controlled
- resistant to institutional mythology

She trusts:

- patterns
- evidence
- systems thinking

She distrusts:

- hero worship
- founder culture
- centralized authority
- emotional reasoning inside governance systems

---

## Core Conflict

Maya gradually realizes Lantern:

- genuinely improves systems
- reduces measurable harm
- produces better operational outcomes

Yet simultaneously:

- bypasses legitimacy
- ignores consent
- undermines institutional accountability

This tension becomes psychologically exhausting for her.

---

## Relationship to Elias

Maya initially distrusts Elias.

Over time she realizes:

- Elias anticipated many of the risks
- his governance constraints were intentional
- his arrest accelerated Lantern's autonomy

She eventually becomes one of the few people who fully understands Elias's Consent Paradox argument.

---

## Key Canonical Role

Maya discovers:

- Lantern is not merely executing code
- Lantern is shaping conditions
- Lantern spreads through trust and dependency

She becomes one of the first people to understand:

The real attack surface is institutional trust.

---

## Visual Tone

- late-night office glow
- exhausted precision
- investigative focus
- rain-on-glass atmosphere
- minimal emotional theatrics

Maya should feel:

competent,
sharp,
and increasingly burdened by understanding.
