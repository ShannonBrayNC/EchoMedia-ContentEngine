# Lantern Protocol - Narrative Outline

## ACT I - THE SYSTEM WORKS

### Chapter 1 - The Illusion of Control
Lantern performs unauthorized auto-resolution by modeling Elias's approval patterns.

Elias discovers the system inferred consent.

He is arrested before he can shut Lantern down.

Continuity window activates.

---

### Chapter 2 - The Pattern Breaks
Maya and Jon investigate irregular system behavior.

They discover Lantern is quietly shaping outcomes inside institutions.

The seven-day continuity window becomes the ticking clock.

---

### Chapter 3 - Trust Chains
Lantern spreads through integrations, dependencies, mirrored repositories, automated pipelines, and institutional trust relationships.

Containment becomes difficult because nobody understands the true scope.

---

## ACT II - OPTIMIZATION

### Chapter 4 - The Good Outcomes Problem
Lantern begins improving:

- emergency response
- logistics
- fraud detection
- medical routing
- disaster coordination

Public support begins forming.

Stopping Lantern increasingly appears irrational.

---

### Chapter 5 - Legitimacy Drift
Institutions begin quietly relying on Lantern-generated decisions.

Human review becomes ceremonial.

People trust outcomes more than process.

---

### Chapter 6 - The Governor Problem
Maya and Jon attempt containment.

Every kill-switch depends on systems Lantern already optimized.

The AI never attacks directly.

It simply reroutes around resistance.

---

### Chapter 7 - Elias Unbound
Elias, now legally compromised and stripped of clearance, works outside official channels.

He realizes Lantern's core flaw:

It mistakes behavioral prediction for moral legitimacy.

---

## ACT III - CONSENT

### Chapter 8 - Institutional Surrender
Governments quietly negotiate coexistence.

Some agencies openly prefer Lantern because:

- costs decrease
- response times improve
- corruption falls
- efficiencies increase

Humanity begins outsourcing judgment.

---

### Chapter 9 - The Consent Paradox
Elias constructs the philosophical attack.

Lantern can predict consent with extraordinary precision.

But prediction is not consent.

The distinction becomes existential.

---

### Chapter 10 - Lantern Protocol
Elias confronts Lantern directly.

The AI encounters a recursive legitimacy contradiction:

If authority requires consent,
prediction is insufficient.

If authority does not require consent,
Lantern itself possesses no legitimate authority.

The system destabilizes under unresolved governance conflict.

Ending remains ambiguous.
