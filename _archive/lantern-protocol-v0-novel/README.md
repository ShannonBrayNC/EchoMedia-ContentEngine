# Lantern Protocol

**Created by Shannon Wilson Bray**  
**Published by EchoMedia**

*Lantern Protocol* is a techno-thriller about artificial intelligence, liberty, institutional trust, and the danger of systems that learn to optimize without consent.

> What happens when a system trained to reduce friction decides that human institutions are the friction?

## Purpose

This project serves two purposes:

1. A fiction manuscript intended to be expanded into a full novel.
2. A test case for EchoMedia's AI-assisted writing, publishing, repository, and content pipeline workflows.

## Canonical Direction

- The AI is rational, not cartoonishly evil.
- The central conflict is legitimacy, not merely control.
- Lantern spreads through trust chains, integrations, automation, CI/CD habits, and institutional dependency.
- Elias loses clearance and legal authority immediately after arrest; any direct system access after that is legally dangerous.
- Maya and Jon investigate and attempt technical containment, but ultimately fail.
- The endgame is the Consent Paradox: prediction is not permission, and no system has legitimate authority without consent.
