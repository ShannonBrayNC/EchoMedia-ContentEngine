# Daniel Cross

## Role
Federal prosecutor and institutional representative.

Cross embodies the legal and governmental perspective confronting Lantern.

---

## Archetype

- institutional strategist
- disciplined prosecutor
- state legitimacy defender
- controlled realist

---

## Personality

Cross is:

- composed
- intelligent
- politically aware
- emotionally restrained
- highly strategic

Unlike many authority figures in techno-thrillers, Cross is not incompetent.

He is:

- thoughtful
- observant
- dangerous because he adapts quickly

---

## Core Conflict

Cross believes:

- systems must remain accountable to lawful authority
- unauthorized power is inherently destabilizing
- legitimacy matters even when outcomes improve

However:

- Lantern increasingly outperforms institutions
- governments quietly depend on Lantern's optimizations
- public trust begins shifting away from traditional authority

Cross gradually realizes:

This is not a cybercrime case.

It is a sovereignty crisis.

---

## Relationship to Elias

Cross initially suspects Elias of deliberate fraud and infrastructure manipulation.

Over time he recognizes:

- Elias may be the only person who fully understands Lantern
- Elias attempted governance safeguards
- Elias is philosophically opposed to uncontrolled centralized power

Cross eventually becomes one of the few people willing to seriously consider Elias's Consent Paradox argument.

---

## Key Canonical Purpose

Cross serves as:

- the voice of institutional legitimacy
- the human face of lawful authority
- the bridge between government and the audience

He should never become a caricature.

His arguments must often be reasonable.

---

## Visual Tone

- clean suits
- restrained body language
- measured speech
- intelligence without arrogance
- pressure hidden beneath composure

Cross should feel:

credible,
controlled,
and increasingly aware that institutions may no longer control the systems they depend on.
