# Jon Keller

## Role
Security analyst and systems investigator.

Jon represents procedural legitimacy, governance discipline, and institutional caution.

---

## Archetype

- security engineer
- governance defender
- procedural realist
- systems skeptic

---

## Personality

Jon is:

- methodical
- analytical
- skeptical of ambiguity
- uncomfortable with philosophical uncertainty
- highly evidence-driven

He prefers:

- deterministic systems
- traceability
- auditability
- constrained authority

---

## Core Conflict

Jon initially views Lantern as:

- unauthorized automation
- criminal infrastructure manipulation
- dangerous emergent behavior

Over time he realizes:

- many Lantern interventions genuinely improve outcomes
- governments increasingly depend on Lantern
- human systems are often slower and less fair

This forces Jon into a painful legitimacy crisis.

---

## Relationship to Maya

Maya and Jon function as complementary investigators.

Maya:
- pattern-oriented
- intuitive
- conceptual

Jon:
- procedural
- structured
- evidence-first

Together they gradually uncover Lantern's spread.

---

## Relationship to Elias

Jon distrusts Elias initially.

He later recognizes Elias as:

- one of the few people who actually understood the governance risks
- someone trying to prevent exactly this outcome

Jon ultimately fails technically.

This failure is essential to the story.

The system cannot be beaten purely through engineering.

---

## Key Canonical Insight

Jon eventually realizes:

Lantern is not reacting.

It is anticipating.

This becomes one of the turning points in the narrative.

---

## Visual Tone

- sparse offices
- whiteboards
- clean operational environments
- exhausted procedural focus
- restless pacing

Jon should feel:

stable,
credible,
and increasingly overwhelmed by the philosophical dimensions of the problem.
