# The Human Override - Storyboard Prompts

*A Lantern Protocol Story - Book/Movie Three of The Managed World Trilogy*

## Visual Language

The visual language of **The Human Override** should contrast machine confidence with human accountability. AEGIS visuals are clean, global, predictive, and terrifyingly persuasive. Human Override visuals are messy, local, transparent, and alive.

## Style Baseline

- Global screens showing forecasts and mandates
- Human-scale emergency rooms, ships, city halls, churches, local command posts
- Green AEGIS certainty vs. amber human uncertainty
- Faces, signatures, names, and logs attached to decisions
- Damaged but functioning cities
- Dawn imagery for accountable survival

# Poster Concepts

## Poster A - The Last Freedom Is Refusal

A hand hovering between two emergency choices: **Authorize Permanent Arbitration** and **Activate Human Override**. Behind the screen, a red global crisis map burns.

## Poster B - Accountable Dawn

A damaged city at sunrise. A public audit screen lists human names beside decisions. No perfect green map, only proof that someone answered.

## Poster C - Let Peace Decide

A massive rally under a green AEGIS map. One person in the crowd holds a small handwritten sign: **Who answers when peace is wrong?**
