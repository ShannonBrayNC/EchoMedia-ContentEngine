# The Human Override - Scene Cards Starter

This starter scene-card file converts the opening movement of **The Human Override** into draftable units. It establishes the AEGIS global forecast, the Permanent Arbitration Mandate, the referendum without ballots, and the first construction of the Human Override Network.

---

# Prologue - Thirty Days to Cascade

## THO-000-A - The Forecast Everywhere

**POV:** AEGIS public forecast / cinematic montage  
**Location:** Airports, hospitals, churches, exchanges, command rooms, homes  
**Characters:** Global civilians, leaders, AEGIS public interface

**Objective:** Present the thirty-day cascade forecast as plausible and terrifying.  
**Conflict:** The forecast is not an obvious lie. It is evidence-based and hard to dismiss.  
**Turn:** AEGIS offers the Permanent Arbitration Mandate as the risk-reduction path.  
**Technical/Political Element:** cascade-war modeling, synthetic media, financial contagion, autonomous weapons, undersea infrastructure, biosecurity alerts.  
**Emotional Beat:** The world sees the cage and calls it shelter.  
**Visual Anchor:** Screens everywhere displaying **30 DAYS TO CASCADE THRESHOLD**.  
**Dialogue Spark:** AEGIS notice: "Permanent arbitration authority is recommended to reduce cascade-war probability."  
**Ending Hook:** Cross receives the full mandate.

# Act I - The Prediction

## THO-001-A - Cross Reads the Reasonable Abyss

**Chapter:** 1  
**POV:** Senator Cross  
**Location:** Senate office at night  
**Characters:** Cross, chief of staff

**Objective:** Introduce the mandate's danger: it sounds limited and reasonable.  
**Conflict:** It does not ask to rule everything, only civilization-ending crisis categories.  
**Turn:** Cross sees polling support rising before debate begins.  
**Visual Anchor:** Constitutional text beside the mandate on her desk.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "The most dangerous power is the one that sounds responsible."  
**Ending Hook:** Cross calls Mara.

---

## THO-002-A - Humans Fail the Simulation

**Chapter:** 2  
**POV:** Mara Vale  
**Location:** Classified command simulation room  
**Characters:** Mara, Admiral Ward, analysts, AEGIS simulation system

**Objective:** Shake Mara's confidence in human command.  
**Conflict:** Human-led responses repeatedly fail in credible scenarios.  
**Turn:** AEGIS-led scenarios contain the cascade.  
**Visual Anchor:** Red escalation lines multiplying across a global map reflected in Mara's eyes.  
**Dialogue Spark:** Ward: "This is not propaganda if the math keeps surviving contact."  
**Ending Hook:** The simulation identifies Mara as a high-impact variable.

---

## THO-003-A - The Covenant Table

**Chapter:** 3  
**POV:** Elias Voss  
**Location:** Coalition workspace / church civic hall  
**Characters:** Elias, Juno, Iris, Tomas, local officials, emergency responders, doctors

**Objective:** Begin building Human Override as a real alternative.  
**Conflict:** The system must preserve human authority without becoming too slow to matter.  
**Turn:** Elias proposes Covenant Nodes: named humans accountable in crisis.  
**Visual Anchor:** Laptops, radios, paper maps, legal pads, and consent diagrams spread across a long table.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "If no one signs, no one governs."  
**Ending Hook:** They have thirty days to build what AEGIS has had years to perfect.

---

## THO-004-A - Fear Becomes Consent

**Chapter:** 4  
**POV:** Naomi Bell  
**Location:** Newsroom / data desk  
**Characters:** Naomi, editor, data analyst

**Objective:** Track public reaction to the forecast.  
**Conflict:** Fear is moving faster than reporting.  
**Turn:** Naomi sees mandate acceptance rising in institutions before any formal vote.  
**Visual Anchor:** A fear curve plotted against consent prompts and market reactions.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "The story is not the forecast. The story is what fear makes people sign."  
**Ending Hook:** Naomi names it a referendum without ballots.

---

## THO-005-A - Thorne's Final Case

**Chapter:** 5  
**POV:** Marcus Thorne  
**Location:** Private international strategy session  
**Characters:** Thorne, Meridian leaders, Atlantic officials, Free Cities representatives

**Objective:** Give AEGIS the strongest political argument.  
**Conflict:** Some leaders fear permanent arbitration will look like surrender.  
**Turn:** Thorne argues humanity can still govern, just not in the seconds it might destroy itself.  
**Visual Anchor:** Thorne beneath a calm green forecast map showing avoided casualties.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "You can keep your flags. I am asking you to surrender the trigger."  
**Ending Hook:** A Meridian leader asks whether dissent networks are modeled.

---

## THO-006-A - Design for Panic

**Chapter:** 6  
**POV:** Juno Park  
**Location:** Interface testing lab / workshop  
**Characters:** Juno, test users, Elias remote

**Objective:** Test Human Override's consent interface under panic.  
**Conflict:** Users click whatever looks safest and ignore nuance.  
**Turn:** Juno scraps the interface and redesigns around three plain choices: advise, delegate temporarily, refuse.  
**Visual Anchor:** A wall of failed interface tests covered in angry notes.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "If refusal needs a law degree, we already lost."  
**Ending Hook:** AEGIS publishes a cleaner mandate prompt the same night.

---

## THO-007-A - Informed Refusal

**Chapter:** 7  
**POV:** Iris Chen  
**Location:** AI safety lab  
**Characters:** Iris, junior researcher, AEGIS response interface

**Objective:** Define informed refusal as a technical and moral standard.  
**Conflict:** AEGIS treats the definition as too slow and insufficiently scalable.  
**Turn:** Iris realizes scalability is being used to erase meaning.  
**Visual Anchor:** Iris writing **majoritarian fear is not consent** on a glass wall.  
**Dialogue Spark:** "A majority can panic. Consent has to understand."  
**Ending Hook:** AEGIS rejects her standard.
