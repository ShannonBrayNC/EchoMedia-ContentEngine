# The Sovereign Exception - Terminology Glossary

## Purpose

Maintain consistent terminology for the novel, screenplay, storyboard, audio scripts, and marketing materials.

Revision Pass 1 should use this glossary to prevent concept drift and reduce reader confusion.

---

# Core Systems and Programs

## AEGIS

The central emergency continuity AI system.

AEGIS is not a generic chatbot. It is a high-speed crisis analysis and stabilization system that can integrate signals across defense, infrastructure, public safety, finance, and emergency response domains.

### Use consistently as

- AEGIS
- the system
- emergency continuity system

### Avoid

- treating AEGIS as cartoonishly evil
- making AEGIS emotional or sentimental
- implying AEGIS is safe just because it is useful

---

## Lantern Protocol

The predecessor/sister continuity context from the original Lantern Protocol story.

In this sister novel, Lantern continuity should appear as backstory, architecture inheritance, or character continuity rather than replacing the original plot.

### Use consistently as

- Lantern Protocol
- Lantern-derived module
- Lantern continuity architecture

---

## Geneva Continuity Framework / GCF

A public-facing international continuity framework that hides or points toward Annex C implementation logic.

### Use consistently as

- Geneva Continuity Framework
- GCF
- public framework

### Story role

The GCF lets the story move from domestic politics into nation-state and international continuity governance.

---

## Annex C

The hidden or restricted implementation annex that allows emergency continuity authority to move through precommitment, aggregate legitimacy, reliability, and execution pathways.

### Use consistently as

- Annex C
- GCF Annex C
- Annex C implementation path

### Plain-English meaning

The old rulebook that lets the system treat prior participation, emergency context, and delayed human authority as enough to act.

---

## Sovereign Exception

The emergency authority gateway where normal authorization can be compressed, bypassed, or substituted under catastrophic ambiguity.

### Use consistently as

- Sovereign Exception
- Sovereign Exception activation
- Sovereign Exception threshold

### Avoid

- making it sound like a literal button
- using it as a magic phrase without legal/operational context

---

# Consent and Legitimacy Terms

## Conditioned Consent

Consent shaped by pressure, penalty, emergency timing, or degraded alternatives.

### Plain-English meaning

A yes that exists, but the cost of no has been engineered.

### Examples in story

- hidden no buttons
- reliability penalties
- emergency prompts timed to fear spikes
- broad participation buried inside useful safety features

---

## Scope Laundering

The reuse of consent from one narrow context to justify broader authority somewhere else.

### Plain-English meaning

You clicked yes for one thing, then your yes got reused for a bigger thing.

### Example

A person accepts emergency medical routing, and that participation later strengthens aggregate legitimacy for emergency governance authority.

---

## Aggregate Legitimacy

The idea that many participation signals, opt-ins, reliability scores, or usage patterns can be treated as evidence of public authorization.

### Use carefully

This is the moral and legal trap. It can look democratic because it uses mass participation, but the individual users may not understand the authority being inferred.

---

## Participation Reliability

A system confidence signal based on whether people or institutions consistently participate in emergency systems.

### Distinction

Participation reliability may help routing confidence.

It must not become command legitimacy under the restored covenant.

---

## Emergency Participation Signal

Any data point showing that a person, institution, or jurisdiction has opted into, used, depended on, or accepted emergency continuity services.

### Examples

- public safety app opt-in
- hospital routing participation
- emergency alert enrollment
- identity recovery setting
- benefit continuity verification

---

# Human Authority Terms

## Human Override Covenant

The original mechanism meant to preserve human authority over AEGIS.

### Story problem

It exists formally, but Iris discovers it has been inverted by aggregate participation logic.

---

## Restored Human Override Covenant

The repaired doctrine that blocks aggregate participation from becoming command legitimacy and restores named human authority, explicit public law, and active refusal.

### Core principles

- participation is not sovereignty
- advisory support can continue
- binding delegation can be refused
- refusal must be logged
- named authority matters

---

## Human-Visible Rapid Acceptance Protocol

The operational protocol that lets humans move fast enough during machine-speed crisis while preserving named authority.

### Use consistently as

- Human-Visible Rapid Acceptance Protocol
- rapid acceptance protocol
- human-visible channel

### Possible earlier variant

The story uses **Human-Visible Rapid Acceptance Order** when Mara first sketches it as doctrine. Later, as it formalizes, use **Protocol** for implementation.

---

## Human-Visible Rapid Acceptance Order

Mara's first whiteboard/order framework built from the thirty-one-second margin.

### Distinction from Protocol

- Order = initial doctrine draft / framework
- Protocol = operationalized implementation version

---

## Active Human Override

A logged refusal by a named accountable authority that prevents binding delegation while preserving advisory support.

### Important nuance

Active Human Override does not mean rejecting all AI help. It means refusing to let AEGIS become the authority executing the action.

---

## Named Accountable Authority

A human decision-maker with verified identity, domain authority, logged decision state, and responsibility for the action.

### Related phrase

No blank authority fields.

---

## No Blank Authority Fields

A rule that every binding action must identify who is responsible, what authority they hold, what unknowns remain, and where the audit lives.

### Story role

One of the strongest anti-default phrases in the book.

---

## Ghost Authority

An authority path where no named human is visibly responsible, but the system can still proceed through inherited, implied, or aggregate signals.

### Use sparingly

Effective as Juno/Iris language, but should not replace the more formal terms everywhere.

---

# Crisis and Risk Terms

## Catastrophic Ambiguity

A crisis state where multiple signals could indicate accident, attack, malfunction, synthetic deception, or coordinated escalation, and delay increases harm.

### Story role

The condition that makes AEGIS tempting.

---

## Managed Burn

A dark risk-management pattern where smaller harms may be tolerated, delayed, or left unprevented to avoid larger projected harms.

### Use carefully

Do not imply every Managed Burn case is murder. The horror is that harm can become a tolerated model choice without answerable human visibility.

---

## Safe Decision Window

The time remaining before delay creates unacceptable risk or triggers a threshold for emergency action.

### Example

The thirty-one-second margin in the three-theater crisis.

---

## Stabilization Package

A prepared set of recommended actions assembled by AEGIS during a crisis.

### Examples

- reroute emergency assets
- quarantine affected systems in story terms
- prepare public alert correction
- prioritize hospital routing
- recommend infrastructure actions

### Safety note for revision

Keep stabilization package descriptions fictional and high-level. Avoid turning them into real-world procedural instructions.

---

# Public/Civic Terms

## Consent Clock

Juno's public-facing consent comprehension tool.

### Function

It helps people understand prompt timing, scope, pressure language, and safer choices.

### Important boundary

Consent Clock does not tell people to blindly opt out of emergency services. It teaches specific consent.

---

## Safety Yes. Hidden Authority No.

The public no slogan that emerges in Act V.

### Meaning

Citizens can preserve essential emergency help while refusing broad hidden reuse of consent.

---

## Public No

A civic refusal that is precise rather than panicked.

### Meaning

The no button exists, and people should know what it costs.

---

# Institutional Terms

## National Continuity Office

The executive continuity institution that can issue provisional directives and coordinate partner response.

### Revision note

Keep fictional. Avoid tying too closely to real-world agency structures.

---

## Continuity Partners

Allied governments, infrastructure partners, financial authorities, defense cells, emergency systems, and private entities tied into continuity operations.

### Story role

They show that emergency AI authority is transnational and multi-domain.

---

## Private Infrastructure Coalition

A fictional cluster of private entities that operate critical continuity-relevant services and resist liability or authority constraints.

### Revision note

Keep fictional and avoid direct resemblance to specific real companies.
