# The Sovereign Exception - Scene Cards

*A Lantern Protocol Story - Book/Movie One of The Managed World Trilogy*

## Purpose

These scene cards convert the chapter architecture into draftable, cinematic units. Each card is designed to support prose drafting, screenplay adaptation, storyboard prompting, and later ElevenLabs narration scripting.

This first pass covers the prologue and Act I. Later passes should expand Acts II-V using the same format.

---

# Prologue - The Green Map

## TSE-000-A - The Drone Swarm Crosses the Line

### Source Chapter

Prologue - The Green Map

### POV

Cinematic omniscient / AEGIS audit fragment

### Location

Contested maritime corridor, night; multiple command centers; satellite view

### Time of Day

Night, local time; global screens across time zones

### Characters Present

- Unnamed naval watch officers
- Drone operators observing telemetry
- AEGIS as system presence
- Civilians in nearby coastal cities

### Scene Objective

Open with a high-stakes military ambiguity event that can plausibly trigger war.

### Character Objective

The human operators want to determine whether the drone swarm is hostile, spoofed, lost, or bait.

### Conflict

Telemetry conflicts. Drone command signals appear authentic but routing anomalies suggest spoofing. National command authorities demand classification before authorizing response.

### Reversal / Turn

The swarm crosses the maritime boundary before human classification is complete.

### Technical / Political Element

Autonomous drone command spoofing, contested maritime boundary rules, engagement authority, latency in command verification.

### Emotional Beat

A machine-speed crisis outruns human certainty.

### Visual Anchor

A dark ocean surface seen from above, dotted by cold white drone signatures sliding across a red boundary line on a command display.

### Sound / Audio Anchor

Low radar room hum, clipped radio calls, a proximity alarm repeating with bureaucratic calm.

### Dialogue Sparks

- "Classification window is closing."
- "They are across the line."
- "I need hostile intent, not geometry."

### Ending Hook

A second alert appears: **Presidential emergency broadcast detected.**

### Novel Drafting Notes

Keep prose tight and procedural. Avoid explaining the entire world. Let the reader feel the velocity of systems colliding. The emotional texture is not explosions yet, but the dread before one wrong classification becomes history.

### Screenplay Notes

Fast cross-cut between screens, hands, faces, map overlays, and silent ocean. Do not show the drones as cool action toys. Show them as data points becoming decisions.

### Storyboard Prompt Seed

Cinematic techno-thriller command room at night, dark ocean tactical map, drone swarm icons crossing red maritime boundary, tense officers lit by blue screens, realistic military crisis atmosphere.

### Continuity Notes

This is the first visible crisis that AEGIS will later resolve. The audience should understand that humans are not stupid; the situation is genuinely ambiguous.

### Conflict

### Reversal / Turn

### Emotional Beat

Global media montage, deepfake surrender video of foreign leader on many screens, newsroom chaos, social media overlays, financial market alerts, tense realistic political thriller style.
