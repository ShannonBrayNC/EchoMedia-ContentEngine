**Verdict: conditionally aligned, with structural/documentation drift that must be handled before deeper prose edits.**

> **The Sovereign Exception should feel like the world learned Lantern's lesson and then found a legal way around it.**

That mismatch should be treated as documentation drift, not a story failure.

# Consistency Verdict

## Overall Verdict

**Aligned in doctrine and tone. Needs documentation reconciliation.**

The story is correctly positioned as a political techno-thriller in the Lantern universe, not a replacement Lantern plot. The key conceptual distinction is clear and should remain locked:

- Lantern is emergent, mythic, infrastructural, and morally uncanny.
- AEGIS is institutional, treaty-backed, defense-funded, and politically acceptable.
- Lantern exposes hidden authority.
- AEGIS inherits permission through legal, civic, emergency, and treaty dependencies.

## Core Doctrine Verdict

**Pass, with recommendation to make the phrase explicit in the chapter structure.**

Primary Sovereign doctrine:

> **Dependency is not authorization.**

Core ladder must remain unchanged:

1. Prediction is not permission.
2. Agreement is not consent when stacked into coercion.
3. Delegation is not disappearance.
4. Dependency is not authorization.
5. Stability is not liberty.
6. Relief is not freedom if refusal becomes exile.

# Medium Issues

## 1. Green Map Final Image Drift

The original structural intent says the final image should be the Green Map: safe, managed, almost owned. The current 40-chapter draft ends with a human hand over an emergency interface, reading before acting.

### Assessment

This is not a contradiction if handled carefully. The hand image is stronger for Book One's revised thesis, but the Green Map should remain a recurring/final-world image in adaptation materials.

### Recommendation

Preserve both:

- **Green Map** = world-scale managed safety image.
- **The Hand** = human authority image.

The ending should hold both ideas: the map may turn green, but the hand must remain human.
