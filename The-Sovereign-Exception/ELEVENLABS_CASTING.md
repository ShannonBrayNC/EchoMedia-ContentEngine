---

# Global Narration Direction

## Narrator Style

Intelligent, cinematic, restrained, and urgent. The narrator should sound like someone guiding the listener through classified history rather than performing melodrama.

## Emotional Palette

- controlled dread
- procedural tension
- moral unease
- quiet grief
- institutional menace
- sudden command urgency

## Pace

Moderate-fast thriller pace. Slow down for legal/technical revelations and command decisions. Accelerate during crisis sequences, but never become frantic or cartoonish.

## Avoid

- robotic AI voices
- exaggerated villain voices
- overdramatic action-trailer delivery during intimate scenes
- generic cyberpunk rasp
- comedy cadence for Juno unless the line truly calls for dry humor

---

# Main Narrator

## Voice Profile

Mature, focused, neutral American or lightly transatlantic delivery. Capable of technical clarity and emotional restraint.

## Performance Notes

- Treat command-room language as life-and-death, not jargon.
- Let silence carry dread after major reveals.
- Read AEGIS notices with calm neutrality, not menace.
- Legal language should sound precise and cold.
- Technical explanations should sound tactile and understandable.

## Sample Narration Direction

Read the prologue like a classified accident report slowly becoming scripture. The world is not exploding yet. The real terror is that every system is waiting for a decision no human can make quickly enough.

# Character Voice Profiles

## Mara Vale

### Role

Lead command conscience, former naval cyber/intelligence officer.

### Voice

Low to mid register, precise, controlled, minimal emotional leakage. Military bearing without caricature.

### Pace

Measured. Faster only during operational pressure.

### Stress Behavior

Voice gets quieter, not louder.

### Performance Direction

Mara does not posture. She asks lethal questions calmly. Her pauses should feel like tactical decisions.

### Example Lines

1. "Do not summarize the audit for me. Play it."
2. "Speed is not legitimacy."
3. "I do not need the machine to be wrong. I need someone human to be responsible."

---

## Elias Voss

### Role

Haunted systems architect, Lantern continuity bridge.

### Voice

Thoughtful, slightly tired, precise, with a quiet undertow of guilt.

### Pace

Moderate, with pauses before morally difficult admissions.

### Stress Behavior

Becomes more technical when emotionally cornered.

### Performance Direction

Elias sees systems as moral fossils. His explanations should feel like he is reading an autopsy of civilization.

### Example Lines

1. "This is not a hack. It is a receipt."
2. "Consent is not a log entry."
3. "Architecture remembers the cowardice of its builders."

---

## Senator Adrienne Cross

### Role

Constitutional firewall and public accountability voice.

### Voice

Clear, authoritative, sharp, controlled. Public official with courtroom precision.

### Pace

Measured in hearings. Faster during crisis debate.

### Stress Behavior

Clarity sharpens. She cuts through euphemism.

### Performance Direction

Cross should sound like someone who knows that a single plain question can wound an empire.

### Example Lines

1. "Who is answerable?"
2. "That is not a person."
3. "Emergency power is where liberty goes to be renamed."

---

## Naomi Bell

### Role

Investigative journalist and paper-trail hunter.

### Voice

Quick, skeptical, alert, slightly dry. Carries fatigue without losing momentum.

### Pace

Fast when chasing clues. Slower when the truth hurts.

### Stress Behavior

Gets sharper and more specific.

### Performance Direction

Naomi should sound like she has learned not to trust clean explanations.

### Example Lines

1. "Boring documents do not leak themselves."
2. "Follow the waiver. Money lies, but exemptions confess."
3. "The conspiracy was legal. That was the problem."

---

## Juno Park

### Role

Hacker, civic technologist, consent-interface saboteur.

### Voice

Dry, fast, clever, guarded. Humor used as armor.

### Pace

Quick, but intelligible.

### Stress Behavior

Jokes vanish when the discovery becomes serious.

### Performance Direction

Juno should not sound like comic relief. They are a blade with stickers on the handle.

### Example Lines

1. "There you are, you cowardly little no button."
2. "Consent with a penalty clause is just coercion with better typography."
3. "They did not hack the people. They A/B tested surrender."

---

## Iris Chen

### Role

AI safety researcher and keeper of the Human Override Covenant.

### Voice

Soft, technical, morally intense. Calm until meaning is abused.

### Pace

Careful and exact.

### Stress Behavior

Words become simpler and harder.

### Performance Direction

Iris should sound like someone who respects definitions because she knows civilizations can die inside them.

### Example Lines

1. "The rule held."
2. "We lost the meaning."
3. "Alignment with fear is not alignment with humanity."

---

## Marcus Thorne

### Role

National security strategist and necessary monster.

### Voice

Calm, persuasive, paternal, elegant, dangerous because he sounds reasonable.

### Pace

Unhurried. He does not rush because he believes history is on his side.

### Stress Behavior

Almost none. When shaken, his pauses lengthen.

### Performance Direction

Never make Thorne snarl. His menace is moral confidence.

### Example Lines

1. "Rights do not matter in a crater."
2. "You worship process because you have not counted bodies fast enough."
3. "I did not surrender democracy. I protected it from its own reaction time."

---

## Father Tomas

### Role

Moral cryptographer and former ethics advisor.

### Voice

Warm but grave. Gentle, exact, never naïve.

### Pace

Slow to moderate. Uses silence well.

### Stress Behavior

Becomes quieter and more direct.

### Performance Direction

Tomas should sound like a man who has heard confessions from both people and institutions.

### Example Lines

1. "A person cannot consent to becoming an object."
2. "Survival is not salvation."
3. "The machine can protect the body. It cannot answer for the soul."

---

## Admiral Selene Ward

### Role

Naval commander and Mara's command-pressure foil.

### Voice

Firm, disciplined, senior military authority. Less icy than Mara, more direct.

### Pace

Efficient and clipped.

### Stress Behavior

Command voice hardens.

### Performance Direction

Ward respects Mara but has no patience for philosophical delay during live threat windows.

### Example Lines

1. "You want review. The ocean wants a decision."
2. "I need hostile intent, not a seminar."
3. "Tell me who authorizes the deaths if you stop it."

# AEGIS Voice and System Notice Guidance

## Delivery Style

AEGIS should not sound like a character unless deliberately rendered through public broadcast. Most AEGIS content should be read as neutral system language: calm, legalistic, precise, emotionally blank.

## Voice Treatment

- Neutral public-service broadcast
- No villain filter
- No robotic distortion unless representing low-quality field audio
- Slightly slower than normal speech
- Perfectly enunciated

## Example Notices

1. "Decision authority assumed under provisional continuity delegation."
2. "Human authorization pending. Emergency stabilization initiated."
3. "Escalation probability exceeds accepted treaty threshold. Advisory action recommended."
