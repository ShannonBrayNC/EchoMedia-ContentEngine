## Terminology Lock

`DVPO` means `Domestic Violence Protection Order`.

Use `DVPO` throughout this sprint instead of the generic `VPO` label.

## Objective

Add repeated custody escalation to the darker `Silver Bullet` rewrite.

The story should show how manipulated Domestic Violence Protection Order violation narratives create a revolving-door pressure chamber. Jack is repeatedly removed from normal life, loses time and stability, and returns with fewer resources each time.

## Required Story Beats

### Repeated Custody Trips

Include several custody trips across Act I and Act II. Each trip must cost Jack something concrete:

- missed work opportunity
- legal fees
- lost sleep
- family access
- evidence access
- vehicle access
- emotional control
- trust in counsel

### DVPO Narrative Manipulation

Claire Voss uses manipulated stories to create apparent DVPO violations:

- staged contact claims
- alarm-company confusion
- third-party contact accusations
- selective screenshots
- reports that reframe evidence gathering as escalation

The first accusation becomes the lens for all later accusations.

Required line:

> One charge fell. After that, every door started falling on schedule.

### Custody Environment

Make custody feel physically and psychologically frightening:

- fluorescent lights
- metal benches
- noise
- stale air
- sleeplessness
- names called wrong
- low phone battery
- paperwork moving faster than truth

### Military Background

Jack's military service should shape how he survives these scenes. Show discipline, restraint, command presence, situational awareness, and refusal to panic.

Do not write this as a fight manual. The point is not domination. The point is restraint under pressure.

Potential line:

> Jail did not teach Jack violence. The Navy had already taught him what violence cost. Jail taught him how badly the system wanted a receipt.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Multiple custody trips are integrated into the rewrite.
- Manipulated DVPO stories are part of the Silver Bullet cascade.
- Each custody trip removes something from Jack's life.
- Jack's service background creates tension, discipline, and restraint.
- The reader understands why the deal starts to look like rescue even when it is another trap.
