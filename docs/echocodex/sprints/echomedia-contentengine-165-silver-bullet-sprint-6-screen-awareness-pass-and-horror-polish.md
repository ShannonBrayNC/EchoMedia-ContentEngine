## Objective

Perform a screen-first horror pass after the structural rewrite.

The goal is awareness through dread. The manuscript should make readers and viewers feel the trap viscerally, not merely understand the issue intellectually.

## Required Passes

### 1. Cinematic Set Pieces

Ensure the book has at least 8 screen-ready moments:

1. Alarm-company panic call.
2. First calm arrest.
3. Rights removed sequence.
4. House becomes evidence.
5. Jordi calls from hiding.
6. Second arrest with `given the prior incident`.
7. Lawyer ignores the binder.
8. Plea deal arrives as demon.
9. Metadata reveal: packet predates incident.
10. Veteran community recognizes the bullet.

### 2. Fear Motifs

Track recurring dread objects:
- phone buzz
- unreachable charger
- locked car
- unopened lawyer folder
- police body cam
- red circled initials
- alarm log
- plea packet
- phrase `given the prior incident`

### 3. Remove Lecture Drift

Whenever the manuscript explains systems too long, replace explanation with:
- a door closing
- a call unanswered
- a card declined
- an officer saying `I am not here to decide`
- a lawyer refusing evidence
- a friend backing away
- Jordi becoming unsafe

### 4. ElevenReader / Audio Terror Pass

Make chapters work aloud:
- short sharp chapter endings
- repeating phrases
- clean scene transitions
- strong emotional hooks
- no dense legal paragraphs without immediate human cost

## Acceptance Criteria

- At least 8 cinematic horror beats exist.
- Every 3-4 chapters something is taken from Jack.
- Legal/system exposition is tied to immediate harm.
- The manuscript is frightening to a general reader, not only to someone who already understands the system.
- Final export is ready for ElevenReader/ElevenLabs package prep.
