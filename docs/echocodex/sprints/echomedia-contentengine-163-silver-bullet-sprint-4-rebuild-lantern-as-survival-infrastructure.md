## Objective

Rewrite Chapters 16-22 so Lantern is born from urgent survival need rather than abstract archive curiosity.

Lantern must become the only place where sequence, harm, contradictions, and witness safety can still be preserved after ordinary defense paths close.

## New Lantern Doctrine

Lantern tracks the full attack chain:

- Trigger event
- Manufactured emergency
- Third-party system used
- Responder record
- Official narrative
- Legal consequence
- Rights removed
- Income and mobility damage
- Witness contamination
- Contradiction
- Beneficiary of the action
- What each document cost Jack

Primary rule:

> Every document must show what it took.

## Required Story Beats

### Jack's Ordinary Paths Are Closed

Show:
- legal aid waitlists
- private retainers beyond reach
- nonprofit scope limits
- friends afraid to help
- poor counsel discouraging evidence
- witnesses chilled or reframed
- no access to key property/devices
- work opportunities evaporating

### Veteran Community Enters

Daniel Reyes or another veteran recognizes the pattern and explains that the incidents are not separate; they are one connected cascade.

Required line:

> Three arrests in a month is not three cases. It is one bullet traveling through three rooms.

Optional line:

> They do not need to prove you are violent. They only need to make people wonder if the war followed you home.

### Lantern Becomes Rescue Infrastructure

Lantern becomes:
- record shelter
- contradiction tracker
- community triage map
- witness safety protocol
- counsel-prep system
- plea-risk warning system
- timeline reconstruction engine

## Acceptance Criteria

- Lantern is emotionally necessary.
- Every Lantern artifact ties to human cost, not abstract documentation.
- The community feels like survivors recognizing a known weapon.
- Jack's isolation remains frightening even as help begins.
- The mission question appears clearly: Who helps people when helping them looks dangerous?
