## Verification Tests

### 1. Canon continuity

- Elias Voss is the original creator of Lantern.
- Elias Voss is the first Living Anchor.
- Jack activates or operationalizes Elias's unfinished Lantern Protocol; Jack does not create Lantern.
- MaggieOps is absent from the novel continuity.
- Maggie remains a Book 1 character only.
- Vanessa is reserved for Book 2 and does not replace Maggie in Book 1.

### 2. Widow Circle dread

- Widow Circle functions as a human-pressure machine, not only a rumor network.
- The manuscript shows compounding institutional pressure through accusation, paperwork, public narrative, legal ambiguity, employment risk, clearance fear, isolation, disability strain, and sleep disruption.
- Veteran stakes are personal, serious, and survival-centered.
- Any Project 22 / veteran-awareness reference is respectful, contextual, and non-expository.

### 3. Eden truth-anchor

- Eden remains central as Jack's emotional light and truth witness.
- Eden's evidence/witness role affects the plot.
- Eden is never reduced to a passive child witness or emotional prop.

### 4. Claire escalation

- Claire's public voice remains calm, plausible, and safety-coded.
- Claire's private actions escalate strategically.
- Claire understands that the system only needs a trigger, not the whole truth.

### 5. Lantern necessity

- Lantern emerges because human systems cannot see the whole pattern.
- Lantern tracks evidence, timelines, witnesses, public narrative, records, and institutional blind spots.
- Lantern's ethics remain tied to Elias's Living Anchor legacy.

### 6. Safety and tone

- No graphic crisis content.
- No sensationalizing veteran pain.
- Dark material is written with purpose, witness, warning, and survival thread.
- Horror remains personal and procedural rather than cartoonish.

### 7. Book 2 handoff

- Book 1 ends with survival and exposure, not a clean victory.
- Widow Circle is positioned as a node/gate toward The Null Pattern.
- The ending sells Book 2 without cheap cliffhanger mechanics.

## Required QA Artifacts

For each sprint, produce a short verification note containing:

1. Chapters/files reviewed.

3. Dread/pressure beats verified.
4. Safety/tone check result.
5. Continuity risks found.
6. Required fixes before close.
7. Recommended next sprint.

## Definition of Done
