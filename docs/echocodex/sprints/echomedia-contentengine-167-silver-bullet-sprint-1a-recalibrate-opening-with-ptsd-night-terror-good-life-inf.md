## Objective

Revise the current Act I opening so Jack does not begin with paperwork. The book should open inside Jack's nervous system: a PTSD night terror rooted in fictionalized special operations service in Afghanistan/Iraq, then shift into proof that he is managing, healing, parenting well, and living a life worth defending.

The wrong-initial / risk-referral material from Sprint 1 remains valuable, but it should likely move later in the opening sequence after the reader understands who Jack is, what he built, and what Claire's Silver Bullet is about to destroy.

## New Opening Shape

### Opening Scene: Night Terror

Start with Jack waking from a military night terror.

Required tone:
- frightening but not exploitative
- sensory, disorienting, controlled
- PTSD is a wound Jack manages, not a character flaw
- avoid tactical how-to detail
- show restraint, breathing, grounding, and recovery

The dream may involve a fictionalized composite of Afghanistan/Iraq special operations: dust, convoy lights, radio noise, a room cleared too late, a name called over comms, or a civilian face Jack cannot save.

### Recovery Scene: Jack Manages

After waking, show Jack using actual coping discipline:
- grounding
- checking the room
- controlling breathing
- not waking the house unnecessarily
- writing a note or logging the dream
- taking care not to let trauma spill onto the kids

Core message:

> Jack carries trauma, but he is not ruled by it.

### Good Life / Kids Scene

Before the Silver Bullet fires, show life is genuinely good:
- kids trust him
- kids joke with him
- breakfast / school / shared ritual
- he is emotionally available
- the father-child bond is obvious
- the children are witnesses to the real family dynamic

Important canon: the kids do not need convincing later. They saw the events. They know there was nothing. They are Jack's true witnesses and emotional anchor.

### Political Race / Public Life Context

Jack is in the middle of a political race. Public opinion can be turned quickly because he is visible. The Silver Bullet does not just attack his home; it attacks his campaign, public identity, and credibility.

Show:
- campaign messages
- public scrutiny
- volunteers/friends
- public reputation
- how fast people retreat when the accusation appears

### Infidelity Trigger

Before the official legal cascade, Jack discovers evidence of Claire's infidelity. He mentions it, confronts it, or makes clear that he knows.

This becomes the emotional tripwire.

Claire pulls the Silver Bullet because Jack saw too much and named it.

Required story logic:

> Jack does not understand how deep the wound goes yet. Claire already understands how dangerous truth is to her control of the story.

### Silver Bullet Fires

After the family-good-life and infidelity-trigger sequence, introduce the existing Sprint 1 machinery:
- wrong initial
- restricted risk referral
- manufactured alarm/panic call
- DVPO petition
- first arrest/custody event
- rights removed
- house staged as evidence

## Kids Witness Canon

The kids are not neutral props.

They are actual witnesses to the relationship, household dynamics, and absence of violence. They do not need to be convinced Jack is safe. The tragedy is that public systems and public opinion do not ask them or do not weigh what they know.

Required emotional arc:
- early bond is strong
- kids know the truth
- Jack loses contact/access despite that
- later relationship repair is not about convincing them he is good; it is about rebuilding what the process interrupted

## Public Opinion Canon

Jack is publicly visible through a political race. Once the accusation appears, people turn fast:
- friends go quiet
- campaign contacts hesitate
- public supporters vanish
- enemies exploit silence
- proof exists, but people prefer distance over inquiry

Required line candidate:

> The proof was there. The problem was that proof required courage from people who only had curiosity.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Opening begins with PTSD night terror before paperwork.
- PTSD is shown as managed trauma, not proof of danger.
- Jack's relationship with the kids is shown as healthy, loving, and real before the collapse.
- Kids are established as witnesses who know there was no violence.
- Infidelity discovery triggers Claire's Silver Bullet escalation.
- Political-race/public-opinion stakes are established before or during the first accusation.
- Existing Sprint 1 legal-horror material is preserved but resequenced to land after the reader loves what Jack is about to lose.
