## Objective

Rewrite Chapters 6-10 so the reader feels the domino horror: once one charge falls, every later event is interpreted through it.

## Required Story Beats

### Chapter 6: Jordi Saw Her

Introduce Jordi as the friend/witness who proves the villain is active.

Jordi sees Claire near her workplace/apartment/car. Jordi receives a message or warning. Claire has already seeded a report framing Jordi as part of Jack's harassment network.

Required line:

> She followed me, Jack. Then she reported me for following her.

### Chapter 7: Second Arrest

A second manufactured emergency or false contact report appears.

Jack followed the order. It does not matter.

Required phrase from officer/system:

> Given the prior incident...

This phrase becomes the curse.

### Chapter 8: No Contact With The Truth

Show the impossible defense trap:

- Jack cannot contact the accuser.
- Jack cannot easily contact witnesses without looking like he is coordinating.
- Evidence is in the house/car/devices he cannot access.
- Travel is risky.
- Money is draining.
- Work/income opportunities are collapsing.
- Every defensive act looks like obsession.

Required line:

> Evidence was not useless. It was unaffordable.

### Chapter 9: The Lawyer

The lawyer becomes another locked door.

Required beats:
- Jack brings proof.
- Lawyer does not meaningfully review it.
- Lawyer warns against `binders`.
- Lawyer says the court has limited time.
- Jack realizes representation is not the same as defense.

Required line:

> Your name is not what we are negotiating today.

### Chapter 10: The Deal

Introduce the plea/deal as the demon.

The deal offers lower immediate pain but permanent narrative surrender:
- suspended sentence/probation
- no contact
- treatment
- travel restrictions
- firearms loss
- employment disclosure
- clearance damage
- future violation trigger

Required lines:

> The deal was not justice. It was anesthesia before amputation.

> The plea did not ask Jack to confess. It asked him to help the lie become cheaper.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The second arrest or second legal detonation happens before Chapter 10 ends.
- The phrase `Given the prior incident...` is established as recurring dread.
- Bad counsel is shown as part of the injury.
- The deal feels demonic, not procedural.
- Jack's loss of income, movement, vehicle access, and defense ability are concrete on the page.
