## Objective

Add the record-self-replication horror layer: once Jack has a visible criminal arrest history, the negative paperwork begins taking on a life of its own.

The story must show how repeated arrests, DVPO violation narratives, intake notes, summaries, risk labels, and background checks become harder to defeat over time because later reviewers treat earlier records as context rather than allegations that still require scrutiny.

## Core Story Rule

The first record does not need to be true forever. It only needs to survive long enough to become context for the next record.

Required line candidate:

> The paperwork stopped describing Jack. It started introducing him.

## Record Cascade Mechanics

Show the paper trail mutating across systems:

- first police report becomes `history`
- history becomes `pattern`
- pattern becomes `risk`
- risk becomes `prior concern`
- prior concern becomes `given the prior incident`
- arrest history becomes background-check friction
- background-check friction becomes unemployability
- unemployability becomes financial instability
- financial instability becomes legal pressure
- legal pressure makes a plea/deal look like rescue

## Required Horror Beats

### 1. Arrest History as Reputation

Jack discovers that even without conviction, the visible history changes how people treat him.

- recruiters go quiet
- campaign contacts stop calling
- friends ask fewer questions
- lawyers assume complexity is dangerous
- officers read old notes before hearing him speak
- courts reward brevity while his truth requires chronology

Required line candidate:

> His innocence had become a long explanation. The accusation remained a short sentence.

### 2. System Designed Not To Ask

Show that the system is not built to ask deep questions at each stage. It is built to route, process, triage, and close.

- intake workers summarize
- officers respond to current call plus prior history
- prosecutors look for resolution
- defense counsel fears the complexity
- judges have little time
- employers avoid risk

Required line candidate:

> The system did not need to hate him. It only needed to be busy.

### 3. Proof Exists But Arrives Too Heavy

Jack has proof, but proof requires someone to read timelines, compare calls, preserve logs, interview witnesses, and care about contradictions.

The negative record is simple. The truth is heavy.

Required line candidate:

> The lie fit in a checkbox. The truth needed a table of contents.

### 4. Criminal History as Escalation Fuel

Later manipulated DVPO allegations become harder to beat because the prior arrests supply the frame.

Required phrase:

> Given the prior incident...

This phrase should function as the recurring spell that turns unproven history into present suspicion.

## Lantern Requirement

This is why Lantern must exist.

Lantern should counter the self-replicating paperwork by reconstructing sequence:

- what happened first
- what was alleged first
- what record cited which prior record
- what evidence existed but was ignored
- what systems copied earlier assumptions
- who benefited from the assumption chain

Lantern should treat every record as both evidence and possible contamination source.

Required Lantern doctrine:

> A record that cites a poisoned record does not become clean by being newer.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The manuscript shows negative paperwork becoming harder to disprove over time.
- Later arrests and DVPO narratives become more damaging because earlier records exist.
- Jack's criminal-history record harms employment, public opinion, legal posture, and social trust before conviction.
- The system is shown as process-oriented and question-averse, not cartoonishly evil.
- Proof exists, but the barrier is that proof requires time, courage, and sequencing.
- Lantern's role as anti-contamination / sequence-reconstruction infrastructure becomes essential.
