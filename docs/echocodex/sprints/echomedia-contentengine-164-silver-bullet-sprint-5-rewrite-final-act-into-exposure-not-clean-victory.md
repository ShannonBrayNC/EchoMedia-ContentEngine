## Objective

Rebuild the final act so Jack exposes the Silver Bullet mechanism, while avoiding a tidy ending.

The victory should be partial: Jack survives, the trail is proven, Claire is exposed/cornered, but the reader understands the larger process remains dangerous.

## Required Final Act Beats

### Proof Stack

Lantern reconstructs:
- the first manufactured emergency
- alarm-company irregularity
- false report chain
- first arrest as context seed
- later incidents as pattern construction
- Jordi targeting / witness contamination
- evidence packet predating the incident
- poor counsel / plea pressure injury
- financial and legal harm caused before proof could be heard

### Claire Exposure

Claire's mask cracks.

She should not monologue like a cartoon villain. Her horror is her certainty.

Possible line:

> Truth is just the story that missed the deadline.

Or:

> You were innocent too slowly.

### The Court / System Moment

Expose how the process protected itself:
- prosecutor wants closure
- poor counsel minimized complexity
- response records captured procedure, not truth
- court rewarded brevity
- deal was designed to reduce institutional exposure
- Jack's suffering was treated as administratively acceptable

Required line:

> The system does not unwind dominoes. It labels the pile a pattern.

### Ending

Jack is not simply cleared and healed.

He survives. The Silver Bullet is traced. The network forms around Lantern.

Final dread line option:

> Jack had not beaten the Silver Bullet. He had only proved it left a trail. Somewhere beyond the county line, another person was already hearing the knock.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Ending proves the attack chain.
- Claire is exposed as malicious and intentional.
- The legal system is shown as failure-prone without requiring every actor to be individually evil.
- Lantern network becomes sequel/case-file engine.
- Reader leaves asking: How can this happen, and who helps before the first domino falls?
