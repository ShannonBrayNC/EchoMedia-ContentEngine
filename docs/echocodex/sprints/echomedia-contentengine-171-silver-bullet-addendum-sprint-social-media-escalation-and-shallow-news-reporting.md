## Objective

Add the public-amplification layer: after the first arrest and negative paperwork exist, social media and rushed news reporting escalate the damage before anyone asks the right questions.

The manuscript must show that Jack is not only fighting legal records. He is fighting a public narrative that moves faster than evidence, especially because he is visible through a political race.

## Core Story Rule

The public does not need the whole story to punish Jack. It only needs a headline, a screenshot, and a reason to feel righteous.

Required line candidate:

> The report became a headline before it became a question.

## Social Media Escalation Beats

Show social media turning the issue into spectacle:

- screenshots circulate without context
- people clip court or arrest details into accusations
- campaign opponents amplify the story
- supporters go quiet to protect themselves
- strangers claim certainty from fragments
- old posts are reinterpreted through the accusation
- Jack's explanations are framed as manipulation or escalation
- friends fear being tagged, named, or attacked if they defend him

Required line candidate:

> By noon, people who had never met Jack knew exactly what kind of man he was.

## News Reporting Beats

Show local or online news reporting the story without asking the right questions:

- headline centers arrest and DVPO language
- reporter does not verify sequence
- article repeats official wording without testing the timeline
- no one asks who triggered the alarm call
- no one asks whether Jack had been served before the alleged violation
- no one asks what the kids witnessed
- no one asks what evidence existed but was inaccessible
- no one asks about the infidelity trigger or motive
- no one asks why the paperwork predates the incident

Required line candidate:

> They called it balanced because they had asked both sides for comment. They had not asked the evidence anything.

## Political Race Layer

Because Jack is in a political race, the public narrative carries extra damage:

- opponents use careful language to imply guilt
- allies distance themselves pending facts
- public events become unsafe or humiliating
- donors/volunteers vanish
- social posts frame silence as admission and defense as aggression
- the campaign becomes another surface where the Silver Bullet travels

Required line candidate:

> In politics, an accusation does not have to win. It only has to trend.

## Public Opinion Horror

The public narrative should feel like a second courtroom with no rules of evidence.

Show that:

- truth requires chronology
- social media rewards speed
- proof requires patience
- outrage rewards certainty
- Jack's children know the truth but are not asked
- Jack cannot defend publicly without risking the legal case

Required line candidate:

> The truth had witnesses. The lie had distribution.

## Lantern Requirement

Lantern must eventually track public narrative contamination:

- original source
- repost chain
- headline wording
- what was omitted
- what record was cited
- what record cited a prior contaminated record
- who benefited politically or socially
- public harm caused before correction

Required Lantern doctrine:

> A headline is not evidence, but it can still become infrastructure.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Social media escalation appears shortly after the first arrest or public record.
- News reporting repeats official language without asking sequence-based questions.
- The political race context magnifies the damage.
- Jack's inability to publicly defend himself increases the trap.
- Kids are treated as actual witnesses whose knowledge is ignored by the public narrative.
- Lantern's later role includes tracking public narrative contamination, not just legal records.
