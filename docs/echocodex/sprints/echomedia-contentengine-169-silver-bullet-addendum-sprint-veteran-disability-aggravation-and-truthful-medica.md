## Objective

Add a realistic veteran disability layer that is aggravated by the spouse's escalation and the Silver Bullet cascade.

This should raise the truth bar, not become melodrama. Jack's disability must be written as an actual lived constraint that affects sleep, pain, mobility, work capacity, stress tolerance, and access to evidence. The escalation should worsen symptoms in a believable way, especially through stress, sleep loss, repeated custody events, loss of medication access, loss of transportation, and forced overuse while surviving logistics.

## Disability Canon

Jack is a disabled veteran with a service-connected or service-aggravated condition. The strongest realistic options for the manuscript are:

1. **Right shoulder / upper-extremity disability**
   - prior major surgery or joint replacement history
   - chronic pain
   - reduced lifting/carrying ability
   - limited range of motion
   - typing/computer work is possible but physically costly
   - forced logistics, carrying bags, sleeping on couches, and custody stress aggravate symptoms

2. **PTSD / insomnia**
   - night terrors
   - hypervigilance
   - stress reactivity managed through discipline
   - worsened sleep after accusations, custody events, and public collapse
   - used against him on paper as a risk factor despite being managed trauma

3. **Secondary pain / headaches / neck strain**
   - stress and shoulder mechanics can aggravate headaches, neck pain, and fatigue
   - should be shown through ordinary function loss, not medical lecture

## Required Story Rule

The disability does not make Jack dangerous. The disability makes the false narrative more cruel because the process aggravates the very wounds he has been managing.

Required line candidate:

> The accusation did not create his injuries. It found every place the war had already left a seam and pulled.

## Required Aggravation Beats

Show specific, believable aggravators:

- Jack cannot retrieve medication quickly because the house is restricted.
- Jack sleeps badly on couches, in chairs, or in custody settings.
- Carrying bags or evidence binders with one good arm causes pain.
- Stress spikes pain and insomnia.
- Lack of transportation forces him to rely on friends.
- Phone/charger/wallet access issues prevent care coordination.
- Legal appointments and custody trips disrupt medical routines.
- Public accusations make PTSD symptoms easier for others to misread.
- Work collapse removes structure that helped him manage symptoms.

## Medical Truth Tone

Write disability as function:

- What can Jack do?
- What costs him afterward?
- What does he hide from the kids?
- What does he need help with but hate needing?
- What makes him push too hard?
- What symptom gets misread as anger, instability, or danger?

Avoid vague suffering. Anchor scenes in practical limits:

- buttoning a shirt with one painful arm
- carrying property bags after civil standby
- sleeping with the shoulder unsupported
- missing medication
- typing through pain because he has to build Lantern
- rubbing his neck during legal calls
- headaches after sleepless nights
- refusing to let the kids see how bad it is

## Veteran Disability / Employability Link

Tie this to #168:

Jack is technically capable of high-value work with accommodation, remote access, controlled environment, and reasonable pacing. The DV-related arrest and clearance collapse do not only remove income; they remove the accommodations and structure that made work sustainable.

Required line candidate:

> He could work. He had proven that. What he could not do was survive a system that mistook accommodation for weakness and accusation for fact.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Jack has a clearly defined veteran disability profile.
- The spouse's escalation realistically aggravates pain, sleep, PTSD, mobility, or secondary symptoms.
- Disability is shown through function and cost, not exposition dumps.
- The story avoids portraying PTSD/disability as danger.
- The disability layer increases empathy and truthfulness.
- The aggravated disability compounds employability, logistics, and legal-defense pressure.
- The reader understands: here Jack is, surviving, because the wound was real and the process made it worse.
